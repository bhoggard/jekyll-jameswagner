<p>These are two mixed-medium works by <a href="http://www.cooper.edu/art/bio_zimmerman.html">Bryan Zimmerman</a> currently installed in the show, "Begging a Proper Donnybrook," at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/13/903">Archibald Arts</a>.  The image at the top is a detail.</p>

<p>By now it's pretty clear that both <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/005002.html">Barry</a> and <a href="/2005/06/mark_andreas_an.html">James</a> think a great deal of Zimmerman's work, and not just because it doesn't seem to owe its intelligence, its sensibility or its aesthetic to anybody or anything.</p>

<p>We're also not going to ignore Archibald Arts, now that we have an idea of what we've been missing.  Saturday was our first visit, I'm almost embarrassed to admit, and <a href="http://www.freewords.org/biennial/artist/afineart.html">Anthony Archibald</a>'s space has been open for twelve years!</p>
