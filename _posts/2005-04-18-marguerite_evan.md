<p>Yesterday <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/004815.html">Barry</a> wrote about <a href="http://www.margaretevangeline.com/">Marguerite Evangeline</a>'s very strong <a href="http://www.stuxgallery.com/gallery/scripts/artistWork.php?name=Margaret_Evangeline&imageid=1">show</a>, "Shot Through," at Stefan Stux, but regretted he didn't have images of the work.  I ended up with these on my own camera's chip, and thought I'd share them.</p>

<p>The works are each titled, <em>Los Lunas</em>, followed by a separate number.  They are all from 2005, and they are of stainless steel and gunshot.</p>

<p><strong>UPDATE: Bloggy now has a copy of her <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/004823.html">statement</a> as a PDF file.</strong></p>

<p><strong>probably-irrelevant-footnote:</strong> In the shades of Longfellow's <a href="http://john.doucette.com/acadian/longfellow-evangeline.html">heroine</a>, Evangeline is from Louisiana.  Anyway, her work came to me first, but I've always been fascinated with Acadia, Louisiana and her beautiful name.</p>
