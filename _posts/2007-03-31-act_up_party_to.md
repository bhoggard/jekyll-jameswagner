<p>Today South Africa has national health care.</p>

<p>A lot of people still think they can do something to help drag our own country into the [twentieth] century.  Some of them know they have to <em>ACT UP</em> to get there.   But activists also know how to party, and sometimes a little cash is needed to help <a href="/2007/03/act_up_renewed.html">make a stink</a>, so <a href="http://www.actupny.org/">ACT UP</a> is throwing a $20 celebration/benefit bash this evening, and everyone is welcome.</p>

<p>The doors of Manhattan's <a href="http://www.gaycenter.org/">LGBT Center</a> at 208 West 13th Street (just west of 7th Avenue) will open at 7 pm.  The program will start at 8 or 8:30 and will feature readings/performances by Pulitzer-prize winning author <a href="http://www.michaelcunninghamwriter.com/">Michael Cunningham</a>, the notorious <a href="http://www.churchladies.org/">Church Ladies for Choice</a>, Mark Hannay (formerly of the <a href="http://www.nyu.edu/classes/jeffreys/GayandLesbianPerformance/suellentrop/hotpeaches.html">Hot Peaches</a>), and fabulous downtown performance artist <a href="http://www.pennyarcade.tv/biography/index.html">Penny Arcade</a>. The evening ends with a dance party that goes until midnight.</p>
