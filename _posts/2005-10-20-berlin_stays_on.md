<p>We're staying on Rosa Luxemburg-Platz, only steps from the national headquarters of the PDS, or rather of its successor, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Left_Party_%28Germany%29">Die Linke</a>.  I've worn my <a href="/2004/09/the_very_red_bu.html">very red button</a> every time I've gone out, and it seems it escapes the attention of almost no one here.  [in the U.S. I sometimes think it's invisible]</p>

<p>Anyway, a few days ago an open-faced youth stopped us a hundred meters from our door to ask if we knew where the PDS office was.  I was silly-proud to point him in the right direction, and it was near their building that I spotted <a href="http://www.stressfaktor.squat.net/index.php?id=77&PHPSESSID=114ee918a913e7c7b789a4cd19c1a655">this poster</a> this morning.</p>

<p>This is a very political people and they will not be deceived this time.  It's my own country that I'm very worried about.</p>

<p><strong>FOLLOWUP:</strong>  It <a href="http://www.rbb-online.de/_/nachrichten/politik/beitrag_jsp/key=news3280894.html">happened</a>.  Everybody marched.  No one was hurt.  There were eggs thrown (by the anti-antis).  The counter-demonstrators outnumbered the neo-nazis three to one.  The police outnumbered both.  There were ten arrests however, none of them of partisans from the extremist right.</p>
