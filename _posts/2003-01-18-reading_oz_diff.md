<p>Interesting take on the HBO series, "Oz" from a <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/01/19/arts/19MAIL.html">letter</a> in tomorrow's <u>NYTimes</u> "Arts & Leisure" section:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>[excerpt]</p>

<p>I like "Oz" for many reasons, but I suspect that it touches something in me that the article ["In the Brutal World of 'Oz.' a Rare Place for Women" by K.A. Dilday {Jan. 5}] did not mention:  As someone who went to college in the mid-60's, when women were locked into the dorms at night to protect us from the men, who suffered no such restrictions, I find the sight of violent men behind bars deeply comforting.</p>

<p>Mary McKenny<br />
San Rafael, Calif.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Without taking anything from Ms. McKenny, for the record, I feel compelled to admit that, while I was a man in college in the 60's, I too was locked into the dorm at night.  Also for the record, I must admit that while there I enjoyed neither the horrors nor the rough pleasures of "Oz."</p>
