<p>Barry spotted it first:  We had just left "<a href="http://www.artcat.com/exhibits/9859">Blood Drive</a>" the excellent installation curated by Kate Levant at Zach Feuer (more on that in my next post) when we saw this plywood piece attached to a plywood section of a construction fence outside of what is arguably Chelsea's <em>baddest</em> monument to pure excess, 200 Eleventh Avenue condo project, which includes "<a href="http://www.luxury-insider.com/blog/200EleventhAvenueCollectibleCondos.aspx">En-Suite Sky Garages</a>"*.</p>

<p>I was embarrassed that I couldn't identify the artist and had to ask for help from an authority.  Our friend <a href="http://hragvartanian.com/">Hrag</a> came through with the answer:  It's <a href="http://hragvartanian.com/2008/04/20/jason-eisners-outdoor-art-show-in-williamsburg/">Jason Eisner</a>.  Eisner collaborated with Jason Balicki in the exciting installation, <a href="http://www.artcat.com/exhibits/9782">"Back & Forth"</a>, at English Kills earlier this summer, and the two of them (as "J & J") are a part of the group show at <a href="/2009/08/young_curators_new_i.html">P.P.O.W.</a> which closes today.</p>

<p>And there's still more, much more, of Eisner on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tags/jasoneisner/">Flickr</a>.</p>

<p>Oops.  Looking for the links I'm using in this post I just discovered the identity of the artists who created the <a href="/2008/11/street_art_bench_in.html">bench in Ascenzi Square</a>.  I don't know why it took me so long, when the English Kills site makes it pretty clear:  It was <a href="http://englishkillsartgallery.com/artists/jason-eisner-and-jason-balicki.shtml">Jason Eisner & Jason Balicki</a>.</p>

<p>*<br />
Here there appears to be more than one message in the <em>almost</em>-painted-out posters underneath Eisner's piece.  They read, "<a href="http://www.adpunch.org/entry/peta-against-mccruelty/">i'm hatin' what I see</a>", the text of PETA's anti-McDonald's campaign against McDonald's chicken-slaughtering practices (which play on the corporation's familiar "i'm lovin' it").</p>
