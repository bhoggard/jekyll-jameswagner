installation view of Linda Ganjian's assemblage of ten separate sculptures at Annina Nosei</p>

<p>Her medium is somewhat less than digestible here, but <a href="http://www.re-title.com/artists/linda-ganjian.asp">Linda Ganjian</a>'s little sculptures in "Everland," the David Gibson-curated show currently at the <a href="http://www.anninanoseigallery.com/ang_n.html">Annina Nosei Gallery</a> are very easy on the eyes and great fun to contemplate.</p>

<p>They're also unforgettable:  I recognized the work immediately when I walked into the space.  Ganjian was a delicious part of Parker's Box anniversary show, <a href="http://www.parkersbox.com/iam5.html">"I Am 5"</a> last month.  There, in a delightful move made even more fun for its element of risk in a small crowded temporary gallery space, she had added to her Polymer clay pieces some work which actually <em>was</em> edible.  I'm very happy to say that I sampled the right ones.</p>
