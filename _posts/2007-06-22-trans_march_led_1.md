<p>One of our sources tell us that tonight's <a href="http://www.alp.org/calendar.php">Trans March</a> was phenomenal!</p>

<p>Donald Grove commented on Bloggy's <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/006368.html">post</a> about the <a href="http://www.alp.org/">Audre Lord Project</a>'s experience trying to secure a permit for a transgender march tonight.  This is the text of this queer supporter's brief report:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I just finished the Trans March, and I am a bit too footsore to do the Drag<br />
March.	But I am thrilled to say that the Trans March was very VERY big.  I<br />
would guess around 500 people.	Lots of trans folks with a strong mix of<br />
gender non-conforming and queer support.  It was excellent!</p>

<p>We got to march in the street most of the way, which was smart, because the<br />
march was so big.  But we were preceded almost the entire way by a paddy<br />
wagon, with smiling cops holding their bundles of plastic handcuffs.  I<br />
suppose they could make some screwy stupid statement about protecting the<br />
march from bashers, but who would buy that?  They were their with the paddy<br />
wagon first because they wanted to send a message to that uppity Audre<br />
Lorde Project for taking them to court.</p>

<p>Well screw the cops!  The march was huge, and all the police accomplished<br />
was to look puffy, pasty and pointless in the company of so many awesome,<br />
sexy, spirited gender-self-defining folks, both young and old, of many<br />
colors!!!</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I wasn't there, but I have to add my own wee commnent:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>This has got to stop!  A happy group of peaceful marchers who have been brutalized by the police for millenia were forced to accept the deliberately-confrontational imposition of an NYPD police wagon, together with its armed and restraint-laden crew, as their very visible "grand marshal" when they chose to parade through their own neighborhoods.</p>

<p>Were there police snipers on the roof, like during the Puerto Rico Day Parade? I'm also surprised that our guardians of public order don't seem to have swept up spectators who were wearing their pink or lavender <a href="http://www.ny1.com/ny1/content/index.jsp?stid=6&aid=70679">gang colors</a>.</p>
</blockquote>
