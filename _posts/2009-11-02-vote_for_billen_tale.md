<p>I would argue that <a href="http://gawker.com/5395311/gawker-endorsement-dont-vote-for-bloomberg">Gawker</a>* doesn't quite go far enough in its condemnation of Bloomberg's candidacy, since it stops a little too short of suggesting the obvious alternative.  I have no hesitation myself in endorsing the <a href="/2009/03/billys_the_real_thin.html">Billy Talen</a> for <a href="http://www.voterevbilly.org/">mayor</a> over Thompson.  Thompson (unless he's actually working for a Bloomberg victory) ran an extraordinarily incompetent campaign, and he finally appears to be something of a fool (okay, just for starters, look at <a href="http://www.streetsblog.org/2009/09/22/bill-thompson-was-for-bike-lanes-before-he-was-against-them/">where he stands</a> on bike lanes).</p>

<p>Talen is the candidate of a significant political party, the Green Party, but you may never have seen him or heard him;  you may not have heard <em>of</em> him:  The commercial media ignores Talen and he's not allowed to participate in their vaunted mayoral debates or in their interviews with the <em>approved</em> candidates.   But I've heard him talk, of course to crowds, in character as the colorful and <em>truly</em>-righteous Reverend Billy, but also as "layman" Billy in small groups, and to individuals, and he has a better (in both senses) understanding of the city and the world than any of the politicians foisted upon us by the corporations in whose pay they perform, and certainly superior to the small-minded billionaire who blithely, and regularly, buys his high office outright.</p>

<p>Vote for someone tomorrow whose ideas you share.  You deserve it;  we all deserve it.  Talen's mayoral <a href="http://www.voterevbilly.org/platform">platform</a> is a <em>dream</em> - unfortunately - but that's not a bad place to start.</p>

<p>Hell, if I could I'd even endorse him for president - right now - this time confident we'd get change when we voted for it.</p>
