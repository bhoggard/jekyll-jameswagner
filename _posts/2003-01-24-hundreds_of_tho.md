<p>Everybody out there knows I read the <u>NYTimes</u>, but that doesn't mean I eat it up, and I don't suggest such a narrow or uniform diet for anyone.</p>

<p>Today's edition included on page A6 an image of the "Turmoil In Venezuela," as the headline above the caption reads, but no news story, so it can't be found online.  Yes, there is a caption--five lines they give it--but no real story, no background or explanation.  Other news sources (even our own government's <a href="http://www.voanews.com/article.cfm?objectID=1753B062-E84F-4601-A7423830918E3F82">Voice of America News</a>) featured the story prominently yesterday and today, but the Times must have decided it was too difficult to reconcile the story behind the image with the impression they have been giving for months that Venezuela is in the midst of a popular revolt against a mad dictator.  The importance of the story, almost ignored by the <u>Times</u>, is suggested even in the few words of their own tiny item  [my italics].</p>

<blockquote>
<p>One of the <i>hundreds of thousands </i>of supporters of President Hugo Chávez who gathered yesterday in Caracas, Venezuela's capital.  They denounced a strike by the opposition that has slashed oil production.  Several blocks away, an explosion killed one person, a 45-year-old man, and wounded 14.  No one claimed responsibility.</p>
</blockquote>
