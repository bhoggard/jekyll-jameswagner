<p>No, it isn't <em>the</em> holiday that's being exacted.  By "enforced holiday" I mean that my little Air is back in the shop, this time with an audio output malfunction (it simply stopped).  If I'm very lucky, and my neighbors at <a href="http://www.tekserve.com/">Tekserve</a> are really kind,  I'll have it back on Wednesday, but I'm preparing to expect a slightly longer wait because of, yes, <em>the</em> holiday.   In the meantime I'm using the equivalent of a borrowed jalopy, so I won't be posting here for a while.</p>

<p>A lot of people aren't looking at their computers this week or next anyway, between the preparations for and the distractions of Thanksgiving and the Miami art fairs, so I'm concentrating on the arrangements for our own little roasted bird in good conscience:  It's a small <em><a href="http://ladies-with-bottle.blogspot.com/2009/05/le-canard-de-barbarie-muscovy-duck.html">Canard de Barbarie</a></em>, and I picked it up yesterday at <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/ottomanelli-and-sons-prime-meat-market-new-york">Ottomanelli</a>'s.  Our plans to celebrate the feast in Brooklyn with 14 others were sadly cancelled at the last minute.  Unable to invite company only days before the date, we had to re-think our favorite harvest <em>féte</em> as a much smaller and more home-based event.   If it's a culinary success, there will be a report in our <a href="http://food.hoggardwagner.com/">Food Blog</a>.</p>
