<p>It seems everybody loves a banner, these days probably more than ever, and the Williamsburg gallery Front Room has been inspired by their popularity to <a href="http://www.frontroom.org/banner.htm">commission</a> works by a number of artists over the years.  The current installation is by Andrew MacDonald.</p>

<p>I saw only the more minimal side of the piece as I entered the gallery to see the Mark Stillwell <a href="/2007/09/mark_stillwell.html">show</a>, but I loved it.  It suggested the warmth and elegance of a thick white heirloom quilt, but at the same time it obviously wasn't taking itself too seriously.  Only on my way out did I see the other face, and in the late-afternoon light the colored curves of the grommeted squares seemed to be dancing inside the fringe of pom-poms they shared with the surface I had seen first.</p>

<p>It made me smile.</p>
