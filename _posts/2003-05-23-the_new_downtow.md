<p>A wild <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/05/23/nyregion/23TURK.html">turkey</a> on the 28th-floor balcony of an apartment on West 70th Street?  The healthy-looking female has now taken up residence in the more interesting environs of Chelsea and the Village, and we hope she's happy.  Unlike most Downtowners however, she may find it difficult to find friends.</p>

<p>A National Audubon Society ornithologist says that turkeys have not been known to fly as high as the 28th floor.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>"They are not vertical fliers," said [Greg Butcher]. "You will see them maybe 20 feet up in trees, but not 100 feet. I'd say that turkey went up an elevator."</p>

<p>Mr. Lindenauer [the Upper West Side guy] insisted that the turkey was not planted on his balcony. [E.J. McAdams, a director of New York City Audubon] said she could have made her way to the 28th floor by flying up from balcony to balcony, like an elevator making all the stops.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Where is the turkey now?  After making some stops in our own neighborhood, she headed further downtown to the Village, where she was last sighted this past monday, on top of a garage on Barrow Street.</p>
