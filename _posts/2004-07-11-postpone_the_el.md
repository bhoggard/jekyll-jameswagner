<p>Dictatorship will be the answer.</p>

<p>Why of course we can't go on as a constitutional republic if a terrorist act occurs within our borders - or so the Administration would have us believe.  Essentially it's what has already happened since September 11, but now the only people who profited politically from the events of that day now want to make it <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=578&e=4&u=/nm/20040711/ts_nm/politics_election_terror_dc">official</a>.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>U.S. counterterrorism officials are looking at an emergency proposal on the legal steps needed to postpone the presidential election in case of such an attack, Newsweek reported on Sunday.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The Democrats will probably sign on of course.  Perhaps someone should first point out, as <a href="http://www.bloggy.com/mt/">Barry</a> did this afternoon, that even during the Civil War there wasn't an interruption in the election process.</p>

<p>Still, one way or another these people will see to it that there is no real election, this November - or ever.</p>
