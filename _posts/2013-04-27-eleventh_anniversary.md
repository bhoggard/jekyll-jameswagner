<p>Today is the eleventh anniversary of this blog.  During the past year posting has remained a little sluggish, especially when compared to peak times, say, before the modern miracle, and seductive distraction, of <a href="https://twitter.com/wagnerblog">Twitter</a>, but I have no intention of letting the site lapse altogether.</p>

<p>In the meantime, this is a brief description of its history, in the same words I used a year ago:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The blog began when, finding myself totally frustrated with the idiocy and brutishness of my country's response to the events of September 11 and feeling almost totally isolated in my disgust, I started sending a series of emails to people I knew well, sharing my thoughts and my anger. A few months later I started jameswagner.com, intending it to be a more structured - and more widely broadcast - form for the kinds of unelicited rants with which I had been testing the patience of my friends. It was also intended to include ruminations on subjects in which I thought others might share my interest.</p>

<p>Almost from the start there were entries on politics, the arts, queerdom, history, New York and the world, and within a year they began to be accompanied by images and photographs. Many of the latter have been my own.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>April 27 also marks the anniversary of the day I met Barry, my perfect partner in everything (and Wunderkind webmaster);  it was exactly twenty two-years ago tonight.</p>
