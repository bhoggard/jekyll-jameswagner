<p>[This is not going to be the biggest issue any of us have to deal with today, but, what the heck, we can't do important stuff all the time.]</p>

<p>I did not know until this morning that <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/08/29/sports/tennis/29TENN.html">this</a> was the reason I normally have no interest in tennis, but does the story surprise me?  Uhuh.</p>

<blockquote>
<p><a href="http://www.gaysportsfancentral.com/tommyhaas7.jpg">Tommy Haas </a>simply took the dare to bare. If <a href="http://www.i-collect.com/supercelebs/Anna-Kournikova-564%20.jpg">Anna Kournikova </a>could expose her tan hipbones in a low-ride skirt, if <a href="http://www.repubblica.it/gallerie/online/sport/williams/1.html">Serena Williams </a>could pack her dangerous curves into a Lycra cat suit, Haas saw no reason he could not follow the skin-is-in trend at the United States Open by showing a little . . . biceps.</p>

<p>His sleeveless shirt was breathable and built for range of motion but completely illegal in the discriminating eye of Brian Earley, the tournament referee. In Earley's opinion, Haas's attempt yesterday to inject a little zing into the moribund ATP Tour, to employ the same sex-appeal strategy the women have used so well, was out of step with Earley's interpretation of the rules for customary attire.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Yikes!  Are these guys real?  I don't know where to begin to address this stupidity, but let's just say that not all of us out there thinks <a href="http://espn.go.com/tennis/news/2002/0408/1364740.html">this</a> is equivalent to <a href="http://espn.go.com/tennis/aus2001/news/2001/0116/1019644.html">this</a>.  And why would anyone want to see <a href="http://www.graffitipress.it/Gp_Sp200205_6_11InternazionaliTennis.htm">these gentlemen </a>work in more comfortable attire, even if it meant we had to be exposed to a little more of their physical beauty?</p>

<p>Ah, much <a href="http://www.rowztennis.com/gallery/gall19_blake.html">better</a>.</p>
