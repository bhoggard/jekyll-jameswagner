<p>More on the American car vs. public transit thing.</p>

<p>The rest of the world is becoming more and more <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/programmes/from_our_own_correspondent/2225505.stm">aware</a> of our special cult and addiction, and they clearly aren't going to be indifferent to its planetary impact going forward.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>... Beaufort county [South Carolin] planners have been meeting to discuss a regional transportation system.</p>

<p>The [county's daily] paper explains what this is - it would link the county to outlying areas including the nearby city of Savannah, Georgia and the holiday resort of Hilton Head.</p>

<p>People wouldn't have to use their cars. But outraged residents want to use their cars - and they fear the kind of people who use public transport just would not fit in these parts.</p>

<p>"We're not that kind of community", one of them is quoted as saying - and that is the rub.</p>

<p>America is not that kind of community. It is a car-driving society - not in an easy going, take-it-or-leave-it "oh we'll try something else" sense, but in a profound, almost religious way.</p>

<p>The right to drive is a deeply valued blessing - and one that will not be given up lightly, in fact will not be given up at all.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The BBC correspondent realizes that we worship our own gods here.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>In the hotel in Mobile I saw on American television a mention of the development summit and a discussion about the plight of the Maldives - that gorgeous island archipelago which we are told is threatened with inundation as sea levels rise.</p>

<p>When I say a discussion - well it wasn't quite that - by the time they had worked out where they were and marvelled at how small they were there was no time to talk about saving the islands.</p>

<p>Do Americans know that the rest of the world is ganging up on them again and accusing them of polluting the planet? - yes vaguely.</p>

<p>Do they care? Not much.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Yet.</p>
