<blockquote>
<p><b>Context Is Everything</b></p>

<p>Tel Aviv, Sunday, August 24, 2003</p>

<p>I came to Tel Aviv from Jayyous on Thursday evening<br />
intending to stay for one night, but I awoke on Friday<br />
morning with one of my all-too-familiar sinus<br />
infections.  I saw a doctor, got some antibiotics, and<br />
extended my stay at the Tel Aviv hotel until my<br />
departure in the wee hours of Monday morning.  It's<br />
Sunday afternoon now and I'm fever-free, although<br />
still a little nervous about flying with congestion.</p>

<p>My hotel is right on the beach, and it's lovely here. <br />
Lots of people out and about, bar-restaurant-cafes<br />
right out on the sand, streets bustling until late<br />
into the night.  Jayyous is 10km from here, and my<br />
friends there haven't seen the sea for 4 years.  You'd<br />
never know here that there's so much suffering so<br />
close, being inflicted by the Israeli army.</p>

<p>On Wednesday night two Palestinian-American friends<br />
were staying at the same hotel.  They were not<br />
permitted to bring their Palestinian Israeli friends<br />
to the room--the armed security guard was quite<br />
insistent.  I, in contrast, have had free run of the<br />
place, welcome to come and go as I please, even when I<br />
was their late-night guest before I registered at the<br />
hotel myself.</p>

<p>I've been pretty starved for news all summer. I've<br />
been living without radio or TV, in communities where<br />
all the newspapers are in Arabic.  Poor email access<br />
kept me away from Internet news sites.  Now that I'm<br />
in Tel Aviv, lolling about in front of the TV and with<br />
English-language newspapers, I see how the present<br />
grisly turn of events is being framed.</p>

<p>Israeli media are forefronting the suffering inflicted<br />
by the bombing in Jerusalem.  This is perfectly<br />
reasonable.  It was a horrific attack, and its<br />
ultra-Orthodox victims conjure up images of terrible<br />
Jewish suffering in Europe in the 20th century and<br />
before.  The sequence of events presented in the U.S.<br />
and Israeli press seems to be (1) bombing in<br />
Jerusalem, (2)assassination in Gaza (3) end of Hudna<br />
[cease fire].  Journalists (or their editors) don't<br />
seem interested in what happened prior to the<br />
Jerusalem bombing, in what the Hudna was really like.</p>

<p>A look at the website of B'Tselem, the Israeli<br />
Information Center for Human Rights in the Occupied<br />
Territories (www.btselem.org) shows that between July<br />
1 and August 13 of this year, 9 Palestinians (1 of<br />
them a minor) were killed by Israeli security forces<br />
in the Occupied Territories.  In addition, the<br />
construction of the Apartheid Wall and destruction of<br />
Palestinian property continued apace, as did the daily<br />
violence and humiliation that is part and parcel of 36<br />
years of military control of a civilian population of<br />
millions.</p>

<p>During the same period, 4 Israeli civilians (none of<br />
them minors) and one member of the Israeli security<br />
forces were killed by Palestinians.</p>

<p>Then came the Israeli attack on Askar Refugee Camp in<br />
Nablus, in which 4 Palestinians were killed by Israeli<br />
security forces.  The Rosh Ha'Ayin and Ariel bombings<br />
followed, with one Israeli death in each case,<br />
followed by the Israeli assassination of an Islamic<br />
Jihad student activist.  Then the bombing in<br />
Jerusalem.</p>

<p>The attack on the bus of worshippers was not<br />
justified.  It was sickening.  Its planners and its<br />
perpetrator distort Islam beyond all recognition.  But<br />
it was, without a doubt, provoked, and the Sharon<br />
government knew it.  They knew it when they attacked<br />
Askar, and they knew it when they went into Hebron. <br />
Now the situation has spun out of control again,<br />
Israelis will again see their buses, cafes, and<br />
restaurants blowing up, and Palestinians will die (as<br />
always, in much larger numbers) as the occupation army<br />
storms through Nablus, Jenin, Tulkarm, and Gaza.  The<br />
Israeli media today are proudly showing photos of<br />
dozens of tanks assembled at Erez Checkpoint ready to<br />
reinvade Gaza; I can only think of the 1.3 million<br />
desperately poor Palestinians packed into ramshackle<br />
housing in the tiny Gaza Strip, and what these killing<br />
machines are going to do them.</p>

<p>So now Bush and Powell and Rice have stopped bugging<br />
Sharon about the Apartheid Wall, and Mayor Bloomberg<br />
is flying to Jerusalem tomorrow to stand with Israeli<br />
victims of terror, even as the Israeli army creates<br />
untold numbers of Palestinian victims of state terror<br />
in the Gaza invasion.  So it seems like the Sharon<br />
government got exactly what it wanted.</p>

<p>I'll he home tomorrow afternoon.  I'm looking forward<br />
to doing lots of public speaking about the occupation,<br />
as well as reconnecting with family and friends.</p>

<p>Peace,<br />
Steve</p>
</blockquote>
