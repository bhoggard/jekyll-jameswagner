<p>So reads the excellent headline on Stanley Crouch's excellent <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/ideas_opinions/story/43748p-41233c.html">column</a> in today's <u>Daily</u> <u>News</u>.</p>

<p>My favorite sentences, here pulled out separately from the whole:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>What stands before the Republican Party, however, is something much deeper than the Day-Glo red in Brother Lott's neck.</p>

<p>We have to remember that white Southerners were Democrats because the party of Lincoln had won the Civil War (which Lott has referred to as "the war of Northern aggression").</p>

<p>After Johnson's burst of civil rights legislation, the old-time religion of racism lost its power in Democratic Party circles, and reluctant ex-segregationists began to join up with the Republicans, who made them feel at home.</p>

<p>If the Republicans are not what white racists think they are, they need to raise their elephant bottoms up off the dime and get to work.</p>

<p>Long before his name was even mentioned as a presidential candidate, Bush told me in Texas that if the Republican Party did not expand itself beyond its white base, it would die. Though I doubt it, let us hope he was right.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Die.  Yeah, die would be better for all of us.</p>
