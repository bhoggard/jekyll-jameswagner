<p><em>"The fault, dear Brutus, is not in our stars, but in ourselves, that we are underlings." </em> <br />
[Cassius, in "Julius Caesar", Act I, Scene II]</p>

<p>The stars, the fates, Hecate, Parcae, Fata, the norns, the three sisters, they're us, and we should start acting like we know it.</p>

<p>The <a href="http://www.tsa.gov/">Transportation Safety Administration</a> can't give us security;  it's in our hands.   All of the grotesque, costly, and invasive measures the TSA has already introduced, or might still introduce, are only reactive, and cannot match the efficacy of the initiatives which a look at the broader geopolitical picture would demand.    We should also remember that all the current fuss is about passenger air travel, which is only a tiny portion of our national security responsibilities.</p>

<p>The only comprehensive security measure that makes any sense, and which incidentally would be acceptable to, if not applauded by, the entire world (including air travelers everywhere) would be an elimination of the cause, not a continual search for the effect of the intense resentment and hatred behind suicidal and other terrorist acts.</p>

<p>We should begin by looking at ourselves as others see us.  We should end all of our current, totally <em>optional</em> wars, close the U.S. military bases and operations currently located within well over 130 countries, and begin to show a decent respect for the cultures of other peoples. </p>

<p>Of course it would also be helpful if we could actually bring ourselves to extend real foreign aid, not military hardware, and only where it can be constructive, not where we believe we can buy love or increase our own wealth. </p>
