<p>Today's <u>NYTimes</u> has a front page article reporting that Kerry says Bush isn't telling the truth about Iraq.  Only a close reading of the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2004/09/17/politics/campaign/17kerry.html">story</a> (now buried inside the online edition) would reveal that the real news, more important even than what the contender says about it, is that a report from the administration's own National Intelligence Council told Bush way back in July that the outlook for Iraq was gloomy at best, and that at worst it was likely to be an absolute disaster, with the balance clearly weighing closer to the latter.</p>

<p>The paper did report the intelligence assessment in <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/reuters/news/news-iraq-intelligence.html?pagewanted=print&position=">yesterday</a>'s edition, but even if the editors think that incontrovertible proof that Bush is lying about the defining event of his presidency is just yesterday's news, one could at least expect some editorial or OP-ED comment.  Once again the U.S. commercial media, including here one of its most respected outlets, demonstrates that it can only cover politics, and especially campaigns for political office, as a competitive sport.  Any mention of actual issues has apparently become unAmerican.</p>
