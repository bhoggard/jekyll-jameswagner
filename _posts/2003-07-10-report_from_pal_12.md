<p><a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200307#955">Steve</a> writes today:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>East Jerusalem, Thursday afternoon, July 10, 2003</p>

<p>We're off to the West Bank tomorrow morning for our<br />
two-day training.  We're still working on where we'll<br />
go and what project(s) we'll work on.  Our affinity<br />
group includes one activist with dual American and<br />
Israeli citizenship and an obviously Israeli name.  It<br />
will be difficult for her to pass through Israeli army<br />
checkpoints because Israeli law forbids Israeli<br />
citizens from entering Palestinian areas (and vice<br />
versa--for me, a strong reminder of South Africa's<br />
apartheid Pass Laws of years gone by).</p>

<p>In addition to the checkpoint problem, there are some<br />
West Bank regions where we can't work, because the<br />
local people are just too suspicious of Israelis.  For<br />
instance, a woman who said she was an Israeli reporter<br />
recently went to Tulkarm Refugee Camp to interview men<br />
wanted by the Israeli army.  She returned a week later<br />
in uniform with an army unit that then arrested the<br />
men she had interviewed.  After experiences like this,<br />
people in Tulkarm are not ready to accept an Israeli<br />
who presents herself as a peace activist, even though<br />
it's true.</p>

<p>Fortunately, there are other areas who are willing to<br />
accept Israeli allies and eager to work with them. <br />
Our affinity group will go to one of those areas,<br />
provided we can get through (around?) the relevant<br />
checkpoints.</p>

<p>Below, I've copied action alerts about the Jenin<br />
arrestees, and today's detentions in Nablus.   If<br />
you're one of the people on this list who has<br />
political reasons for receiving my reports, rather<br />
than only personal reasons, read on, please make the<br />
suggested phone calls and send the suggested emails,<br />
and forward the reports widely.</p>

<p>Peace,<br />
Steve</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I did not include the alerts and contact information here, but I will forward that material to anyone who <a href="mailto:james@jameswagner.com">emails</a> me a request.</p>
