Andrea Loefke, detail of installation, "Little Objects"</p>

<p>We first <a href="/2003/11/looking_good_in.html">saw</a> her work at S1 last November and when we wandered into her <a href="/2004/06/more_on_the_sma.html">space</a> at <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/">Smack Mellon</a> open studios earlier this year I was pretty certain I'd see her again, soon.  Now <a href="http://www.andrealoefke.com/text_pages/home.html">Andrea Loefke</a> has a one-person show at <a href="http://www.phgallery.com/">PH Gallery</a> on 27th Street, and if it's not <i>quite</i> so totally mad as the environment she had created in D.U.M.B.O., she's easily forgiven;  after all, a commercial gallery has to think about access and safety issues.</p>

<p>The materials are wonderful, and the constructions are a mix of a perfect minimalism and a very imperfect assemblage of loose debris, everything invested with enough chuckles to support the show's title several times over:  "when the green frog changed into a happy prince the nearby well - splish, splash - turned into sweetened lemonade."</p>
