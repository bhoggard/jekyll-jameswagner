<p>Remember <a href="/2005/06/_and_they_dont.html">these guys</a>?  I did I post almost two years ago about a show of internet-based art, hosted by <a href="http://www.rhizome.org/#">Rhizome</a>, installed in the New Museum's temporary quarters on 22nd Street.  Rhizome is a young community of new media artists, curators, critics and enthusiasts, and they're hosting a very interesting and almost totally affordable benefit next monday night.</p>

<p>This very special occasion, for which non-members will be asked to put up $35, is actually a concert featuring what the invitation describes as three genre-bending bands: <a href="http://www.ganggangdance.com/">Gang Gang Dance</a>, <a href="http://www.professormurder.com/">Professor Murder</a> and <a href="http://www.teamyacht.com/">YACHT</a>.  Here's more:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Each band integrates a wide range of musical influences and instrumentation to create innovative sounds and style. This line-up of new music will celebrate Rhizome’s commitment to emerging forms of art, across sound, video and digital technologies. The evening will be introduced and mc-ed by computer artist Cory Arcangel [<a href="/2006/10/cory_arcangel_a.html">Cory Arcangel</a>, folks!], and will also include a silent auction with work by artists, such as <a href="http://www169.pair.com/klucas/archive/">Kristin Lucas</a> and <a href="http://cultureandcommunication.org/galloway/press/villagevoice_apr_12_2004/">Alex Galloway</a>, who work with the Internet.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Full disclosure:  Barry and I are on the Honorary Committee, but that just shows we're even more enthusiastic about this thing than we can possibly let on.</p>

<p>You may head <a href="http://rhizome.org/events/benefit/">here</a> for all the details on the concert.</p>
