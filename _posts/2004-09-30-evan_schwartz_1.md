<p>I really like it when I come across a subject, any subject, for a second time and it's arrived from another direction altogether.</p>

<p>It was at least a number of months ago that I had read a story (I believe it appeared in the <u>NYTimes</u>, but I can't find it now) about a young student who was raising money in very imaginative ways to pay for gender reassignment surgery.</p>

<p>While visiting Williamsburg galleries this past Sunday I spotted a photograph which bowled me over at the time and which remains etched in my mind tonight.   The image was a powerful and very beautiful self-portrait by Evan Schwartz, the young woman whose courageous story had impressed me earlier.</p>

<p>Evan is an artist;  I don't remember that part, and maybe it wasn't even a part of the account I had read, but I'm not going to forget it now.</p>

<p>I saw Schwartz's image (it's the one which appears at the top of this post) hanging in the long hall at the <a href="http://www.schroederromero.com/">Schroeder Romero Gallery</a> and now I know that he will have his own show there in a few months.  It opens January 7th.  I don't remember ever before recommending, or even mentioning, a show on this blog months before it had been hung, but this is a good time to start.</p>

<p>There is more work on Schwartz's own <a href="http://www.orioninsight.com">site</a>, including this image, which is from the same series, “Reclaiming Puberty,” as the one above.</p>
