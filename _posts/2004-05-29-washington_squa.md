<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_White">Stanford White's</a> elegant <a href="http://www.aviewoncities.com/nyc/washingtonsquare.htm">Washington Square Arch</a> has been restored, but virtually every square inch of its carved surfaces is now covered in an <i>almost</i> invisible high-tech screening material designed to keep out the New York pigeon.  Only the flat and molded surfaces (including the base, a section of which is shown above) and the two pier sculptures of George Washinton manage to avoid the veiling.</p>

<p>I suppose Ancient Athens didn't have much of a pigeon problem.   This is just  a thought, but if it had, without sophisticated modern plastic netting the Parthenon frieze would have been hidden under guano for over 2000 years and Lord Elgin would never have known <a href="http://www.museum-security.org/elginmarbles.html">his "Marbles"</a> were even there.</p>
