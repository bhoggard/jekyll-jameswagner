<p>Under normal circumstances things skull-like hold absolutely no fascination for me, but Dinter Fine Art's current show, "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/5324">Death & Love in Modern Times</a>", manages to totally transcend the dry calcium props which <em>enliven</em> its macabre theme.  The press release suggests I may be in the minority when it comes to interest in the subject of skeletons, so I should thank a lot of people besides Ingrid Dinter, the curator, for the mounting of this exciting collection.</p>

<p>The intense, more-or-less salon-hung exhibition is assembled from work by an extraordinary company of artists:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Michael Byron, Billy Copley, David Dupuis, Dan Fischer, Rico Gatson, Tomoo Gokita, Leon Golub, George Horner, Peter Hujar, Daniel Johnston, Dan McCleary, Kelly McCormick, Ana Mendieta, Tomas Lopez Rocha   James Romberger,  Julie Ryan, Phil Sims, Aaron Sinift, TABBOO! Stephen Tashjian, Marguerite Van Cook, Valaire Van Slyck, Mike Walton, Andy Warhol and Rob Wynne</p>
</blockquote>
