Anish Kapoor <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_Gate">Cloud Gate</a> 2004-2006 polished stainless steel  33' x 66' x 42' [detail of installation]</p>

<p>I think I expected to be charmed by Anish Kapoor's sculpture in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millenium_Park">Millenium Park</a>, but when Barry and I encountered "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_Gate">Cloud Gate</a>" on the first full day of our visit to the city two weeks ago I thought it was even better than the reviews had reported - and even more fun than its billings.</p>

<p>But it's also an incredible photo opportunity, and this was one of those rare times I totally went with it.   The underside of what Chicagoans had early on dubbed "the Bean" is described as an <em>omphalos</em>, or navel, a complex, curving indentation whose mirrored surface multiplies anything found beneath it, but in the other images I snapped the same afternoon I concentrated entirely on the first of the foreshortened  Barrys I spotted above me.</p>
