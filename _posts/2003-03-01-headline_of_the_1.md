<p>Actually the moment has already passed.  On Friday the <u>NYTimes</u> print edition included the following amazing headline in the "Business Day" section:</p>

<blockquote>
<p><b>Random House Names an Editor With Literary Ties</b></p>
</blockquote>

<p>By evening at least, the online edition was displaying the somewhat less satirical caption to the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/02/28/business/media/28BOOK.html">same story</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
<p><b>New Editor at Random House</b></p>
</blockquote>

<p>Someone decided not to reference quite so dramatically what the <u>Times</u> article calls the publishing house's recent "unusually harsh dismissal" of the new editor's predecessor, Ann Godoff, in what most people in the publishing world considered a move away from quality and toward the more commercial status of the firm's sister company, Ballantine, with which the Random House label itself was consolidated at the same time.</p>

<p>[editor's note:  I suppose we should just be happy we get anything at all these days other than John Grisham and Danielle Steele.  Look at what's happened to radio and television.]</p>
