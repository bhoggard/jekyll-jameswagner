<p>[This goy would wear it as a badge of honor, if he could imagine it involved any merit on his part.]</p>

<p>Tonight I was surprised to find myself the target in a classic and somewhat extended anti-semitic confrontation.</p>

<p>While Barry and I were waiting to enter the Greenwich House Theater this evening, a blond, sixty-ish, middle-class woman (she looked like she lived in the Village, perhaps as a retired schoolteacher) walked up to me and peered closely at my slash war button.  She mumbled something about having initially thought it was a slash <i>Bush</i> button, adding, "jews hate Bush," and walked on.</p>

<p>I asked Barry what she had said about jews, and he confirmed what I thought I had heard.  I chased after and confronted her, to ask whether she had really said that.  (At this point I actually thought there was a chance we had misunderstood or had missed some New York irony.)  But no, she repeated that jews hate Bush and asked if I hated Bush.  I told her I thought Bush was a dangerous idiot and she asked if I was jewish.  I asked her why she was asking such a question.  She could only reply that jews hate Bush and then insisted repeatedly that I was Jewish.  A small crowd was gathering at this point and the play was to begin soon, so I left her wading in her hateful paranoia with the gentle suggestion that she should seek help.</p>

<p>The entire experience was incredibly disturbing for both of us, not least because of the nature of the beast, and the turf.</p>

<p>It's tough enough being queer, lefty, atheist, and philo-semitic in Manhattan.  How do people survive in the hinterland?</p>

<p>p.s.  The <a href="http://www.botz.com/nytheatre/kalamazoo.htm">play</a> was quite wonderful.</p>
