<p>I don't think we should be surprised to find that it is the poets who may <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/01/31/politics/31POET.html">showing</a> the greatest courage in the face of tyranny in the White House.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Laura Bush has postponed a White House symposium on the works of Emily Dickinson, Langston Hughes and Walt Whitman after some of the poets invited said they hoped to use the event to protest American military action in Iraq.</p>

<p>. . .</p>

<p>In his message [to his colleagues, one of the poets invited to the symposium, <a href="http://www.poets.org/poets/poets.cfm?prmID=750">Sam Hamill</a>] said he felt "overcome by a kind of nausea" as he read his White House invitation, and decided the only response would be to reconstitute a "Poets Against the War Movement." Mr. Hamill said that he had not planned to attend the White House event himself but that the submitted poems and statements would be compiled into an antiwar anthology to be presented to Mrs. Bush on Feb. 12.</p>

<p>By Wednesday, Mr. Hamill said he had received 1,500 responses, and had to create a Web site, which he named <a href="http://poetsagainstthewar.org/">poetsagainstthewar.org</a>, to handle the e-mail messages that were overloading his system.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I don't see any other community showing the same resistance.  Most people, as individuals or as groups, can't even be discreet about their glee when they are invited to add themselves to a Bush photo opportunity.  Are they all starstruck, or do they just think they have to be super polite?</p>

<p>One of the poets who submitted compositions to Hamill was <a href="http://www.poets.org/poets/poets.cfm?45442B7C000C0F06">Marilyn Hacker</a> whose poem included these lines:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The world is howling,</p>

<p>bleeding and dying in banner headlines.</p>

<p>No hope from youthful pacifists, elderly</p>

<p>anarchists; no solutions from diplomats.</p>

<p>Men maddened with revealed religion</p>

<p>murder their neighbors with their righteous fervor,</p>

<p>while claiming they're "defending democracy"</p>

<p>our homespun junta exports the war machine...</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Mr. Hamill plans to organize anti-war poetry readings across the country on Feb. 12, in what he would like to make "A Day of Poetry Against the War."</p>

<p>Resist!</p>
