<p>American Fine Arts [no website] opened a smashing new show, "Election," last night, but the legendary gallery founded by <a href="/2003/03/colin_de_land_o.html">Colin de Land</a> (and currently located in the last home of the equally fabulous gallery created by  <a href="http://www.artnet.com/magazine/features/finch/finch8-21-00.asp">Pat Hearn</a>) will close when this show is taken down November 18.</p>

<p>This is a very big loss, but I can't imagine a gallery scene without Daniel McDonald around and I don't expect we're going to lose sight of him.</p>

<p>Apart from that, Mrs. Lincoln really enjoyed the show in the space Daniel has been managing full-time at least since Colin's tragic death last year (just three years after we had grieved for his wife, Pat) is fully worthy of its history.  She adds that it's a must-see, and preferably before the momentous [civil?] war-time election going down just eleven days hence.</p>

<p>The show was organized by James Meyer.  There's no gallery checklist yet, so the images I can show below have only a skeletal description.</p>
