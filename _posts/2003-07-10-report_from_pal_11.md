<p>> INTERNATIONAL SOLIDARITY MOVEMENT<br />
> July 9, 2003<br />
> For Immediate Release<br />
> <br />
> <br />
> FOUR INTERNATIONALS ARRESTED AT PEACE CAMP<br />
> <br />
> [ARRABONY, Jenin Region]  Four international<br />
> volunteers with the <br />
> International Solidarity Movement were arrested<br />
> today while <br />
> maintaining a presence at the peace camp set up by<br />
> Arrabony <br />
> villagers and the ISM to protest the confiscation of<br />
> Palestinian <br />
> land for the Apartheid Wall.  The four arrested are:<br />
> <br />
> <br />
> Tobias Karlsson from Sweden<br />
> Tariq Loubani from Canada<br />
> Bill Capowski from New York, USA<br />
> Fredrick Lind from Denmark<br />
> <br />
> Full details are not yet known and none of the peace<br />
> activists are <br />
> answering their phones, however we just received the<br />
> following text <br />
> message: "at Salem abused and beaten".  This seems<br />
> to indicate that <br />
> the four are being held at the Salem Military Base,<br />
> north of Jenin.<br />
> <br />
> Since the peace camp was set up on Monday, July 7,<br />
> 2003, activists <br />
> have faced threats and harassment from the Israeli<br />
> Military, from <br />
> heavily armed security guards working for the<br />
> Israeli company <br />
> building that section of the Wall, and from Israeli<br />
> settlers.  <br />
> Activists have been threatened with violence,<br />
> removal, and arrest. <br />
> The response of international activists was that<br />
> they were there at <br />
> the request of the people of the village, and didn't<br />
> recognize <br />
> Israeli military authority over the area.  On<br />
> Monday, soldiers came <br />
> to the area of the camp to photograph international<br />
> activists and <br />
> local villagers and yesterday armed guards<br />
> threatened to destroy the <br />
> camp.<br />
> <br />
> Despite the harassment there has been steady and<br />
> enthusiastic <br />
> support from the people of the village of Arrabony. <br />
> Men, women, and <br />
> children have been a twenty-four-hour presence at<br />
> the camp, and are <br />
> coming every day in greater numbers.  Activities at<br />
> the camp have <br />
> included games and sports, music, and more.  <br />
> <br />
> For the past year the Israeli government has been<br />
> building a massive <br />
> wall that it claims is for purposes of "security". <br />
> The wall, <br />
> however, is being built inside of the West Bank,<br />
> destroying and <br />
> confiscating from Palestinians their most fertile<br />
> agricultural <br />
> grounds and de facto annexing into Israeli illegal<br />
> settlements and <br />
> valuable underground water aquifers. Tens of<br />
> thousands of <br />
> Palestinian fruit and olive trees have already been<br />
> destroyed and <br />
> farmers are being prevented from working on land<br />
> that they've lived <br />
> off of for decades.  The Palestinian people have<br />
> been marching and <br />
> protesting this land confiscation and destruction of<br />
> livelihood but <br />
> have been met with violence from the Israeli<br />
> authorities and silence <br />
> from the international community.  The Arrabony<br />
> peace camp is one of <br />
> 4 similar protest camps in the West Bank.<br />
> <br />
