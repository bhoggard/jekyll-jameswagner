<p><img alt="LinaresMichaelDOasis.jpg" src="/mt_archives/LinaresMichaelDOasis.jpg" width="600" height="399" /><br />
Michael Linares <em>Oasis</em> 2006 wood, screws, enamel, plastic, assorted beers 2' x 5' x 5' [installation view]</p>

<p>The beers were gone by the time we arrived yesterday, but we got the idea.  It's a beautiful sculpture, even absent any knowledge of its congenial concept.  The work is by <a href="http://www.artnet.com/Galleries/Artists_detail.asp?G=&gid=423855952&which=&aid=423899697&ViewArtistBy=online&rta=http://www.artnet.com">Michael Linares</a>, one of six exciting artists working in Puerto Rico who are represented in the current show, <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/2038">"The Lovers"</a>, at CANADA Gallery.</p>

<p>The rooms on Christie Street are one of our favorite New York destinations, and the gallery is now doing an exchange with San Juan's very impressive* <a href="http://www.artnet.com/comercial.html">Galeria Commercial</a>.  About the time this show ends [the announcement reads this Saturday, although it may be extended] a group of Canada artists will begin a show in the Puerto Rico space.</p>

<p>CANADA explains the New York installation in this excerpt from their press release:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Though not intended, the group of works in The Lovers<br />
resembles an adulterated version of a bar. The sum of<br />
art put together for this exhibition spins a jukebox,<br />
a cooler, a pool table and a hippie bead curtain.</p>

<p>. . . .</p>

<p>Michael Linares' piece, titled Oasis (2006), is a<br />
pedestal stuffed with beer. Strategically located<br />
between other works in the exhibition, it works<br />
simultaneously as gathering and refreshment area. The<br />
seemingly abstract forms contrast with the open-ended<br />
relational formations produced by them.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>*<br />
After our return from the Miami fairs last December Barry <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/005279.html">wrote</a>:  "Several galleries impressed with their overall programs. One was Galería Comercial, which is located in San Juan, Puerto Rico. It exists somewhere between the non-profit and commercial gallery worlds -- but on purpose unlike some galleries! At NADA, they showed posters and other works by Pedro Vélez, plus a number of other artists working in media ranging from painting to video."</p>
