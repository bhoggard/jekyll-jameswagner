<p>I brought us home a big bunch of beautiful off-red tulips tonight.  I really admire more creative responses to the ancient <a href="http://atheism.about.com/od/catholicsaints/p/SaintValentine.htm">Valentinus</a> challenge, but one story totally intimidates me.   John Waters has lately been <a href="http://64.78.33.181/features/index.cfm?id=3330&cat=1&page=features&sub_page=weekly">talking about</a> his own special valentine tradition: "I used to always send boys a little chicken heart . . . that I'd get at the butcher.  I thought that was a nice way of telling them that I had a crush . . . ."</p>

<p>I wasn't surprised that I couldn't find a pretty picture of a chicken heart, so I went for this dramatic abstraction I found on an Amsterdam medical site where the image is described without any elaboration as an "embryonal stage of a chicken heart".</p>
