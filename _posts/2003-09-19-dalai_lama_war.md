<p>Phew!  I'm relieved, both as an atheist and as a small-"d" democrat, to find that the Tibetan political and religious leader, the Dalai Lama, has feet of clay.</p>

<p>Lama now says that the Iraq war may be <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/uslatest/story/0,1282,-3132067,00.html">justified</a>, and has always said that homosexuality is <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/09/19/opinion/19FREN.html?pagewanted=print&position=">not</a>.  It seems that his reputation for peace and understanding is something of an artificial creation.</p>

<p>On Bush's wars:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The Dalai Lama said Wednesday that the U.S.-led war in Afghanistan may have been justified to win a larger peace, but that is it too soon to judge whether the Iraq war was warranted.</p>

<p>"I think history will tell," he said in an interview with The Associated Press on Wednesday, just after he met with President Bush.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>And, as for my wretchedness:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>For example, the Dalai Lama explicitly condemns homosexuality, as well as all oral and anal sex. His stand is close to that of Pope John Paul II, something his Western followers find embarrassing and prefer to ignore. His American publisher even asked him to remove the injunctions against homosexuality from his book, "Ethics for the New Millennium," for fear they would offend American readers, and the Dalai Lama acquiesced.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>He sounds like a not-so-moderate Republican to me.</p>

<p>What's the good of a lama if he won't defend truth and justice everywhere?  And besides, we can even weep and fight for Tibet itself without the ministrations of a Buddhist fakir.</p>
