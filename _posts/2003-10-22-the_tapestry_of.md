<p><a href="http://www.maiamarinelli.com/">Maria Marinelli</a> was also a part of the d.u.m.b.o. festival this past weekend where she was represented by a performance of "Arazzo," one of the four parts of her mixed media project, "IMPERO."</p>

<p>What I saw on Saturday was two women knitting separate long panels or "tapestries" in red yarn of an almost painfully bright hue.   They were separated that afternoon by a full block's length of the same skein of thread each was incorporating into her work.</p>

<p>The two pieces being woven by the performers will eventually meet in the center where no more unused thread will remain.  In order for one piece to be completed the other one must be destroyed.</p>

<p>in English, the name of the greater project is "EMPIRE."  The artist explains, "This metaphor makes visible the paradigm by which every imperialistic culture operates."</p>

<p>"Women's work," indeed!</p>
