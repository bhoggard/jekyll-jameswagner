<p>The City still doesn't get it, or at least the police and the <u>Daily</u> <u>News</u> still fail to understand, making the <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/crime_file/story/47779p-44806c.html">second Union Square box incident</a> this month look like it was as worthy as <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/12/17/nyregion/17FEAR.html">the first</a>.</p>

<p>Ani Weinstein was arrested yesterday while taping a number of black boxes, labelled "FEAR ART," to the walls of Union Square station.  She was booked with essentially the same charges made against Clinton Boisvert December 11 when he was arrested for taping boxes, labelled "FEAR," in the same subway station.</p>

<p>The Daily News headline read, "Another art hoax in Union Sq. subway," called Weinstein a " copycat prankster" and referred to each of the installations as a "stunt," but the news article at least allows that "investigators believe Weinstein acted to show solidarity with the art student [Boisvert]."</p>

<p>Boisvert told us that our fears are not being addressed rationally;  Weinstein showed us that some of us still can't understand this, which more than confirms <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200212#574">the original message and its imperative</a>, and why it will have to be repeated again.</p>
