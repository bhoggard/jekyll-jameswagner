<p>The powerful documentary, "<a href="http://www.arnaschildren.com/">Arna's Children</a>," is now at the <a href="http://www.quadcinema.com/">Quad Cinema</a> here in Manhattan.  I'd be much more excited if it were playing in every town in the U.S., but unfortunately it's not going to happen.  If you want to know why, see what I <a href="/2004/05/we_must_all_be.html">wrote</a> last May.</p>

<p>I just noticed that of the four films currently being shown at the Quad, Barry and I have actually seen three.  This is pretty amazing, since the two of us rarely get around to slipping into a movie theatre in the crush of so many seductive live (therefore more time-sensitive) performance offerings.  Like "Arna's Children," the other two films would not be described as directed toward mainstream audiences (whatever that means), but I can recommend both <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0355683/">"The Child I Never Was"</a> and, most enthusiastically, Bruce LaBruce's <a href="http://www.brucelabruce.com/">"Raspberry Reich."</a></p>

<p>If these films have anything in common, it's the ability of each to re-arrange minds which might have thought everything was already nicely in place.</p>
