<p>The Mayor's office has <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2007/08/04/nyregion/04filmmakers.html?ref=nyregion">backed off</a> from its outrageous set of <a href="/2007/06/police_to_regul_1.html">proposed rules</a> for people using photography anywhere in New York City.  It's a great victory for a free people alert to the threat of arbitrary government and willing to oppose it, but I'd advise against relaxing any guards.</p>

<p>They'll be back.   City officials said they would redraft the rules</p>

<p>In the end any proposed regulation absolutely must be held to a standard that freely permits photography anywhere in the city so long as people are not interfering with anyone else.     <a href="http://www.yearlykosconvention.org/node/263">Beka Economopoulos</a>, the co-founder of <a href="http://www.pictureny.org/">Picture New York</a> said it best:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I already have a permit for my camera;  it's called the First Amendment</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Corporations and governmental units of every size have their own surveillance cameras trained on me willy-nilly virtually everywhere I go in this city and at all times of the day and night.  I don't recall their ever applying to me for a permit.  I should not have to consult their directives or ask their permission to flip a shutter when I wish to do so myself.</p>
