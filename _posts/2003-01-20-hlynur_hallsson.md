<p>The Icelandic artist, <a href="http://www.hallsson.de/">Hlynur Hallsson</a>, has, in an extrordinary gracious gesture, included my name along with those of a New York artist/curator and a German curator as part of his email announcement of his <a href="http://www.hallsson.de/cv/bio_-_kino_-_movies/bio_-_kino_-_movies.html">current show</a> in Reykjavik ["20 pages Catalog with text from Horst Griese,  James Wagner, Paul T Werner and more.  ISBN 9979-60-826-9"].  I had been impressed with a <u>NYTimes</u> account of Hallsson's installation in Marfa, Texas, so I posted <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200209#383">some words</a> inspired by admiration and respect for his art and what I could divine even then of his good humor and charm.  He found my blog and thanked me for those comments.  The installation had created quite a stir last summer in a sleepy, yet somehow jumpy, in our post-September-11 way, West Texas town.</p>

<p>I love conceptual art so much I can like it even without actually seeing, hearing or feeling whatever its <i>it</i> is, and sometimes it's better only reading about it, but damn, I wish I could get to that show in Reykjavik!</p>
