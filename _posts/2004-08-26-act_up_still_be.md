<p>It's AIDS, stupid!</p>

<p><a href="http://www.actupny.org/">ACT UP</a> pulled off a classic <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/004292.html">action</a> this afternoon across from Madison Square Garden, the site of the Republican National Convention, scheduled to open formally on Monday.</p>

<p>In the spirit and the extremity of these strange times, the boys and girls did it while entirely naked, and a beautiful sight they were.</p>

<p>Excerpt from the ACT UP New York/ACT UP Philladelphia press release:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>A dozen activists stopped traffic in front of Madison Square Garden and <br />
stripped naked, exposing the fact that when it comes to AIDS, "the emperor <br />
Bush has no clothes."  Slogans were painted on their bodies that read "Drop <br />
The Dept" and "Stop AIDS Now," while [two other activists] held a banner <br />
with the same message - another was dropped over one of the trailers parked <br />
nearby.</p>

<p>We were protesting the Bush administration's refusal to agree with the <br />
proposal of other G7 nations to cancel 100% of the debt of poor nations - <br />
money that could be used to fight AIDS.</p>

<p>While Bush has promised $15 billion over 5 years to fight Global AIDS, poor <br />
nations pay that amount each year in debt payments.  Cancelling the debt <br />
would have significant impact on money spent toward health care and other <br />
desperately needed social programs.</p>

<p>After blocking traffic for 15 minutes, the naked activists were arrested and <br />
taken into custody.</p>
</blockquote>
