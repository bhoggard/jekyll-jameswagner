<p>Virtually 34 years to the day after the <a href="http://www.indegayforum.org/authors/marcus/marcus56.html">Stonewall</a> rebellion, the U.S. Supreme Court has <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/06/26/politics/26WIRE-SODO.html?pagewanted=print&position=">decided</a> we have rights.</p>

<p>Today's ruling invalidates <a href="http://www.sodomylaws.org/usa/usa.htm">sodomy laws</a> in the 13 states which have retained them until this moment.  Until 1962, when Illinois repealed its statute, every state had these laws on the books.  The next repeal had to wait until after Stonewall, when Connecticut joined Illinois in 1971.  New York shuffled along until 1980 when its law was invalidated by its own judiciary, but the statute itself had remained on the books until it was legislatively removed this month.</p>
