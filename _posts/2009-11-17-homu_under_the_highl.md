<p><a href="http://www.homelessmuseum.org/">HOMU</a> is out, and <a href="http://bloggy.com/2007/02/museum_director_1.html">the director</a> is in.</p>

<p>We've just received word that the continually enthralling, yet characteristically elusive <a href="http://www.homelessmuseum.org/hmu_pages/interview_6.html">HOMU booth</a> will be out and about today, Tuesday, on West 20th Street (between 10th and 11th Avenues, next to the entrance to the Highline Park), as the Director writes, "circa 1-4 PM".</p>

<p>These images were taken <em>last</em> Tuesday, when just before Barry and I had squeezed ourselves onto the little chairs in front of the "Director is [hanging square wood tile] <strong>in</strong>" sign mounted on the front of the portable booth.  We were having so much fun, both constructive and unserious, that we hadn't realized a small crowd had gathered above and behind us.  We've been enthusiastic <a href="/2007/02/homeless_museum_1.html">members</a> of the Museum for years, so we had no problem getting up and making room for new visitors.</p>
