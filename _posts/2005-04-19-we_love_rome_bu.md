<p>We love Rome, but we're very happy we didn't plan a trip this month.  The beautiful ancient streets are filled with cultists and the more than idly-curious;  the city's broad and narrow ways fill with their trash as soon as it is cleared away;  and the pavements and walls smell of urine and sometimes more.</p>

<p>But People live and work in that great city.  Some of them are less-than-eager hosts.  The Roman father of an Italian friend of ours, an eminent linguist known for his bon mots, describes a city overwhelmed by unwashed hordes of media victims seeking to be <em>a part of history</em>,  "It's like Popestock here!"</p>
