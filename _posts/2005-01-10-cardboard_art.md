<p>I love cardboard.  It's warm, accessible, forgiving and versatile, and it comes in all sizes, colors and surfaces.  It's also totally without pretension, even when it's pretty expensive.  All these virtues describe the stuff before it's been worked, and sometimes the work really moves.</p>

<p>While one afternoon's experience doesn't suggest a school, as we were heading home last night I thought about the fact that in three gallery stops and one studio visit this humble material had been the basis for most of the art we had seen.  Odd.  Maybe it was only because all of the venues were very downtown, but it actually seemed very right.</p>
