<p><strong>OH, THE AUGURY!</strong></p>

<p>Part of the main front of the Supreme Court Building <a href="http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20051128/ap_on_go_su_co/scotus_cracking_facade">collapsed</a>, only a few minutes ago.</p>

<p>CNN <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2005/US/11/28/scotus.facade.ap/index.html">reports</a> that Washingtonians themselves aren't too worried.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>A group of students from Columbus, Ohio, pocketed some of the fragments as souvenirs, [Ed Fisher, a passing government worker] said.</p>

<p>Mark Goodman, a Washington resident, said some of the people who picked up larger pieces of stones took them back. "We'll have to look on eBay tomorrow," Goodman said.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I don't think we could do much worse if that were also where Bush's nominees were found.</p>
