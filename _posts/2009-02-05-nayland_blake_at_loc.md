<p>Just about as inscrutable as Matt Mullican, but different.  <em>Very</em> different.</p>

<p>Soho's Location One is hosting what the gallery describes as a 25-year survey of <a href="http://www.naylandblake.net/">Nayland Blake</a>'s work in almost every medium.  Curated by <a href="http://www.brooklynmuseum.org/community/blogosphere/feministbloggers/author/reillym/">Maura Reilly</a>, the show is titled "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/7/8375">Behavior</a>".  Even for a visitor familiar, even comfortable with the transgressive, it seems <a href="/2006/01/nayland_blake.html">Blake</a> doesn't really care whether you <em>get</em> much of what he's doing.  But then he's something of a <a href="http://www.artfagcity.com/2008/06/24/the-guys-we-would-fuck-an-interview-with-nayland-blake/#more-2677">virtuoso</a> in this field.   You can get lost in this installation, but you won't get out unaffected by some of the images.</p>

<p>Oh, the printed text on the soiled shirt in the image above reads, "GNOME FONDLER".</p>
