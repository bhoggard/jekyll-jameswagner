<p>Why didn't somebody tell us?</p>

<p>Kerwin Matthews, the actor who played Sinbad in the 1958 film, "<a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0051337/#comment">The 7th Voyage of Sinbad</a>", died July 5 in his home in San Francisco.  The <u>NYTimes</u> <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2007/07/13/movies/13mathews.html?_r=1&ref=obituaries&oref=slogin">obituary</a> says that his death "was confirmed by <em>his lover of 46 years</em>, Tom Nicoll".</p>

<p>Yes, the film was aimed at a young audience, but we weren't too young to fall in love with the beautiful and dashing hairy-chested Sinbad.  Who could possibly have imagined that he wasn't as straight as everyone else (everyone except me, of course, and all the other queers of whose existence I would have no suspicion until years later)?</p>

<p>My favorite part of the short item in this morning's paper is this sweet memory recalled by his partner:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Except as Sinbad and Gulliver, Mr. Nicoll said, Mr. Mathews was never satisfied with merely playing action roles.</p>

<p>“He always wanted to do light comedy, or something more weighty,” he said.</p>

<p>Then, in 1963, Mr. Mathews was cast as Johann Strauss Jr. in the Disney television production “The Waltz King.”</p>

<p>“He was most proud to play Strauss,” Mr. Nicoll said, “and that he had to conduct the Vienna Philharmonic. Whether they actually followed him I don’t know, but he tried very hard.”</p>
</blockquote>
