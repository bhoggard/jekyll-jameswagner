<p>Oh yes!  But I think my favorite is the piece on the artist's <a href="http://www.melissapokorny.com/index.html">home page</a>.</p>

<p>Prominent highlights of the Fountain fair across the highway from Armory were Melissa Pokorny's two wonderful/weird sculptures on the floor just inside the door.  They were part of <a href="http://www.frontroom.org/index.htm">The Front Room</a>'s contribution.</p>

<p>Pokorny was part of the gallery's January/February <a href="http://www.frontroom.org/realimage.htm">group show</a>.</p>

<p>From her site:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Forget meaning.  This work functions like some sort of homemade cultural probe. Think of a kid poking a stick into swamp water and removing it to see what lies beneath. Pokorny's creative imagination seems to work that way.</p>

<p>People have a hard time imagining living with Pokorny's sculpture, but in a sense we all do already.</p>

<p>— Kenneth Baker</p>
</blockquote>
