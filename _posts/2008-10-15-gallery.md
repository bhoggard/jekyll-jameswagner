<p>After a month's run the party is over and by now the walls of the white box must have been cleared, but when Barry and I arrived on the last day of "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/7771">Party At Phong's House</a>" at Galeria Janet Kurnatowski there was still plenty of fun to go around.</p>

<p><em>The times</em> (and at times my own peevish taste for serious drama) may demand more dour or even doleful work than the dozens of beautiful mostly-abstract works created by almost as many living artists  (including one elephant) which the painter <a href="/2008/02/chris_martin_at_3.html">Chris Martin</a> had assembled for this show, but let those <em>times</em> wait, and right now I think we all need a little revel.  Martin ended his short curatorial statement:  "The reason for curating this show was to have a party afterwards at <a href="http://brooklynrail.org/2008/07/art/nancy-spero-in-conversation-with-phong-bui">Phong</a>'s house."</p>

<p>I thought both the idea and its realization were unaffected and delightful, a perfect match for another perfect fall afternoon, and an excellent elixir for sweeping away gloomy thoughts about our socialist capitalist <em>end of days</em>.  I've included a tiny sampler of images from the exhibition here.  The information below each is all that was printed on the checklist.</p>
