<p>On what was thus far one of the hottest days of the summer, any show with the tag "Flight Plan" would be welcome.  The work installed at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/2664">Morgan Lehman</a> this month doesn't disappoint the visitor looking for distraction and relief, even if the only real soaring experienced is that of the spirit.</p>

<p>The artists included are Brook Caballero, Dana Carlson, <a href="/2003/07/michael_exposed.html">Orly Cogan</a>, Sean Cavanaugh, Kirsten Deirup, Franklin Evans, Philip Knoll, <a href="http://www.jennyladen.com/">Jenny Laden</a>, Dona Lief, Jeffrey Milstein, Amy Ross, and Paul Villinski.</p>

<p>My capture of <a href="/2004/12/jenny_laden.html">Laden</a>'s painting was pretty successful, but I obviously couldn't include images of all the pieces here, even those that pleased me the most.  Under the circumstances then I suppose it's a little perverse of me to upload a peek at two works on paper which are not in this show but which were created by an artist who has two other drawings that are.</p>

<p>My image of one of the framed drawings, <a href="http://www.baileygallery.com/artists_02.cfm?fid=100">Franklin Evans</a>'s "FFnineplayers", didn't make the cut mostly because of my technical error with the camera.  Like much of the art in this show however his delicate, condensed, heavily-worked and textured paper works must be seen in person to be appreciated.  Before we left Barry and I were invited to look at two of his drawings just before they were wrapped for transport to the gallery's Lakeville, Connecticut location where a small <a href="http://www.morganlehmangallery.com/dynamic/exhibit_show_statement.asp?ExhibitID=61">group show </a> opens August 12.</p>
