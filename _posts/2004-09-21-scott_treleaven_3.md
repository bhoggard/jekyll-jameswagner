<p>Some artists are comfortable in just about any medium.  It looks like <a href="http://www.scotttreleaven.com/">Scott Treleaven</a> is working on it.</p>

<p>When we first <a href="/2003/06/there_is_no_sce.html">saw</a> his work it was in the form of an amazing film, "THE SALiVATION ARMY," shown at the 2003 <a href="http://www.newfest.org/cgi-bin/iowa/index.html">New Festival</a>.  Only after we acquired a copy of it and began communicating with him did we learn that some people might have first encountered Treleaven through a series of zines he had produced earlier bearing the same title.  We now have copies of the zines, but our connection with the artist hasn't stopped there.</p>

<p>Lately the Toronto-based Treleaven has moved into another medium, creating a number of amazing collages assembled from found images, wrapping paper and his own gouache, crayon and watercolor interventions.</p>

<p>We managed to acquire one of the early pieces from a show at <a href="http://www.damelioterras.com/">D'Amelio Terras</a> in 2003, and last week we were fortunate to be able to see a preview of his latest work at a reception in Simon Watson's <a href="http://www.scenic-group.net/">Scenic</a> space, and it's pretty spectacular.</p>

<p>For some of the images shown on Simon's walls, see this <a href="https://jameswagner.com/gallery/album03">gallery</a>.   Some of the titles will be less enigmatic for those who know their history.  Google.</p>

<p>Treleaven's work will be shown in three galleries outside of New York over the next few months, but I'm certain we will be seeing more of him here very soon.</p>
