<p><a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200307#955">Steve</a> was in a Tel Aviv court today - as an observer.  The indented email text below is complete as it was received.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Hi folks,</p>

<p>I'm in Haifa at the apartment of parents of a member <br />
of <a href="http://www.jewsagainsttheoccupation.org/">JATO</a>.  We were in Tel Aviv today to observe a court <br />
hearing with broad implications for <a href="http://www.palsolidarity.org/">ISM</a>.  Our report <br />
of the hearing is below.</p>

<p>(A little background: anyone perceived by Israeli<br />
border authorities to be a peace activist of any sort<br />
is routinely turned away.  We believe that thousands<br />
have been turned away since the spring of 2002, most<br />
of them undoubtedly tourists believed erroneously by<br />
the border authorities to be activists.  Since the<br />
murder of <a href="http://electronicintifada.net/v2/article1248.shtml">Rachel Corrie</a> by an Israeli soldier in <br />
March of 2003 and its attendant bad publicity <br />
for the state of Israel, the authorities have <br />
been particularly on the lookout for ISM activists.)</p>

<p>Peace,<br />
Steve</p>

<p>Today, July 7, 2003, the District Court in Tel Aviv<br />
held a hearing on Patrick Connor's complaint against<br />
the Israeli Ministry of the Interior for refusing him<br />
entry into Israel this past March.  The Israeli<br />
attorneys representing Patrick received the Ministry's<br />
brief only today, and got an adjournment to July 22<br />
for a decision, so that they could respond to the<br />
brief.  The chief of the Shabak (Israel's General<br />
Security Service) was in the courtroom, and the<br />
Ministry's lawyer kept looking back at him, apparently<br />
for guidance.</p>

<p>Here is our translation of the relevant page of the<br />
Ministry's brief.  We can't vouch that the translation<br />
is 100% accurate, but we did our best.  It starts at<br />
item #12; as far as we know, items 1-11 are just<br />
procedural.</p>

<p>The Position of the Ministry of the Interior</p>

<p>12.   As was told to the plaintiff, the position of<br />
the Ministry of the Interior is that his entry into<br />
Israel is not permitted due to a security concern and<br />
because of a lack of proper authorization.</p>

<p>13.  It is the position of the Ministry of the<br />
Interior that the plaintiff should not be allowed to<br />
enter Israel on the basis of the recommendation of<br />
security sources.</p>

<p>14.  Security sources are in possession of information<br />
according to which the plaintiff is a senior activist<br />
in the organization ISM.</p>

<p>15.  This organization has as its goal to hamper the<br />
activities of the security forces in the territories<br />
and to impede their work in preventing terrorism via<br />
altercations with the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] <br />
soldiers, staying in the houses of suicide terrorists <br />
to prevent their destruction, transporting Palestinians <br />
among various areas during periods of closure, and all <br />
that proceeds from this.</p>

<p>16.  The activities of members of the organization, as<br />
described above, interfere with the security<br />
activities of the IDF and sometimes even endanger the<br />
well-being of the IDF soldiers.</p>

<p>17.  In the framework of activism in the organization<br />
the plaintiff initiated, organized, and took part in<br />
demonstrations in the territories of Judea and Samaria<br />
against the activities of the security forces during<br />
the period November 2002 - January 2003.</p>

<p>18.  From past experience with activists with this<br />
organization, it comes up that some of these activists<br />
tend to deceive the border authorities on entrance to<br />
Israel and do not convey the true purpose of their<br />
arrival in Israel.</p>

<p>19.  As was told to the plaintiff in the letter from<br />
the legal office (that was attached to the original<br />
complaint), the entrance of the plaintiff in March<br />
2003 was denied also because he did not have proper<br />
authorization.  In order to serve various<br />
organizations as the plaintiff requested (some for pay<br />
and some on a voluntary basis) he must equip himself<br />
with the proper authorization of Type B1 or Type B,<br />
and not suffice with a tourist authorization of Type<br />
B2.  The plaintiff is aware of this, and he himself<br />
acknowledges that in the past he has been in Israel<br />
with authorization of Type B1.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I expect to hear about another, much uglier reality in future reports.  This one is an account of the almost-civilized side of the policies produced by Israeli insecurity.  It seems that Israel is at least giving a hearing to the people who complain about being kept from entering the country, although the state may really only be going through the motions.</p>

<p>I think we can say that the U.S. is being a lot less generous with its own prejudices and insecurity.</p>

<p>Still, notice that on Israel's own terms, item #15 admits that Connor, as a member of the ISM, is to be kept out because he might end up "staying in the houses of suicide terrorists  to prevent their destruction, transporting Palestinians among various areas during periods of closure."  This is not a nice picture of the Israeli state's dominion in an illegally-occupied territory.</p>
