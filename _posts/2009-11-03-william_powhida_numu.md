<p>Sometimes a picture really is worth a thousand words, especially if it's been drawn by <a href="http://www.williampowhida.com/">William Powhida</a>.  The artist's editorial cartoon, addressing the current weird curatorial course of the <a href="http://www.newmuseum.org/">New Museum</a>, completely fills the cover of the <a href="http://www.brooklynrail.org/">Brooklyn Rail</a> out tomorrow.</p>

<p>I wrote <a href="/2009/09/new_museum_commits_s.html">this post</a> back on September 25th, immediately after reading the NuMu's <a href="http://docs.google.com/gview?a=v&q=cache:U7Msg-9aktoJ:www.newmuseum.org/assets/general/pressreleases/200909dakis.pdf+the+new+museum+joannou&hl=en&gl=us&pid=bl&srcid=ADGEESjLjwj24zdvshxHem74QVhuotXPfOGHhVb7OJUQny7x4aqOfHSwSA5LD4LzpdNP69cZyL4VHoFuBliRYEALFq6nwkOi3rHIZxxYX9AHMupVZs8OWu0HzudkwaNhzPILeW_bR3Rz&sig=AFQjCNGBzHAEqhl0mMKPrCg6Yx5mUAprgw">press release</a> announcing a metamorphosis which will find it installing a series of museum-wide shows displaying the collections of one or more of its own wealthy trustees and curated by those collectors' favorite wealthy artists.</p>

<p>I followed it with this <a href="/2009/10/numu_urs_fischer_and.html">related entry</a>.</p>

<p>From the beginning I was sure this story had legs, but then I noticed almost no one wanted to talk or write about it, even if they were as outraged as I was.  Now I think its time has finally come.  Thanks, William.</p>

If not, and if you have some coin, you can subscribe (to what I think is the most vital and definitely the most <em>beautiful</em> magazine published anywhere) <a href="http://www.brooklynrail.org/subscribe/">here</a>.</p>
