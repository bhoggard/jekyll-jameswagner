<p>I know there have been few postings here lately, but there's no particular reason other than the lethargy or discomfort of a warm and humid urban summer, what the Greeks and Romans, when they wanted to speak English, called the "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dog_Days">dog days</a>".   There was a best-seller some 200 years back, apparently considered extremely entertaining on a number of  levels, called "Clavis Calendaria;  or, A Compendious Analysis of the Calendar;  Illustrated with Ecclesiastical, Historical and Classical Anecdotes"*.  In it the author, the Englishman John Brady, described the ancient seasonal phenomenon as:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>. . . an evil time when the seas boiled, wine turned sour, dogs grew mad, and all creatures became languid, causing to man burning fevers, hysterics, and phrensies.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>In spite of such fearful obstacles, I really am going to try to do better soon.  At the moment however, while it's probably just coincidence, I actually seem to have come down with a cold, so I might finally almost have an excuse for not putting stuff up, even if I wasn't asking for one.  I just want to nap.  Ach, there's probably no one out there looking this way right now anyway.</p>

<p>Hey, tomorrow's our 16th anniversary!  I hope old Brady was wrong about the wine thing.</p>
