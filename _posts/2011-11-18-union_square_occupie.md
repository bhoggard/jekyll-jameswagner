<p>I love <a href="http://occupywallst.org/article/november-17-historic-day-action-99/">Occupy</a>.</p>

<p>Along with thousands of others I was in Union Square on Thursday afternoon.  There I was struck by the minimalism of the sentiment expressed by the sign shown at the top (and also the color, of course), and I snapped a picture of it before it was clear to me that it was only a part of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erich_Fromm">the message</a>.  When the marcher passed I got the other side;  it wasn't until I was home that I read the smaller lettering at the bottom.</p>

<p><img alt="OWS_saying_no_to_power.jpg" src="/images/OWS_saying_no_to_power.jpg" width="600" height="400" class="mt-image-none" /></p>

<p>But a really smart demonstration, one with even the faintest smell of revolution, is just not complete without a sign in French.</p>

<p><img alt="OWS_l'imagination_au-pouvoir.jpg" src="/images/OWS_l%27imagination_au-pouvoir.jpg" width="600" height="399" class="mt-image-none" /></p>

<p>The text is from a longer slogan, "<em>On ne revendiquera rien, on ne demandera rien. On prendra, on occupera</em>" (We will beg for nothing. We will ask for nothing. We will take, we will occupy).  It made its first appearance during the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/May_1968_in_France">May 1968</a> student protests in France.</p>

<p>I think it's safe to assume that the fact that I was in the midst of a huge crowd of ebullient NYU students had something to do with the erudition displayed on both placards - and maybe with the colors as well.</p>
