<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cato_the_Elder">Cato the Elder</a> didn't have a blog, but he was still able to repeatedly harangue his fellow citizens at every opportunity, even at cocktail parties, with the slogan, <em>CARTHAGO DELENDA EST!</em>  [Carthage must be destroyed!]  He continued at it for years.  Cato meant that Rome would never be safe while Carthage stood.</p>

<p>I do have a blog.  The headline of this entry, a modification of Cato's ancient mantra, means that modern Rome, our own threatened republic, will never be safe while Guantanamo is allowed to stand.   Whether in Rome really had to destroy Carthage may be at least arguable;  the imperative for the eradication of Guantanamo is not.</p>
