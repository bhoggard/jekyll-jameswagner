<p>I wasn't going to single out this one article in one periodical for a post here on my site, thinking it would appear too self-serving, but then I realized that people were already reading the provocative piece by James Kalm, "<a href="http://www.brooklynrail.org/2007/7/artseen/brooklyn">Gangs of New York</a>", in the latest <u>Brooklyn</u> <u>Rail</u> and my not saying anything might look like a statement itself.  Besides, it's about much more than art blogs, and I would definitely have wanted to be told about it myself, and read it, even if I didn't already know some of the names involved.</p>

<p><a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/006458.html">Bloggy</a> already did a small post about Kalm's piece and there he referred to the broadsheet's accompanying photo as broadly "horrific" (he was being kind, since although he didn't single out my own appearance I swear I've still never seen a bad picture of Barry!).</p>

<p>The article is about the future of art criticism, the growth of online art communities, the disintegration of older art authorities, etc., and it includes interviews with <a href="http://painternyc.blogspot.com/">PaintersNYC</a>, Barry and myself.</p>
