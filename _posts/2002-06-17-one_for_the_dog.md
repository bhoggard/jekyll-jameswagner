<p>So, the life of a lesbian, ok, a woman, even an attractive, vigorous, healthy middle class woman, and even in San Francisco, isn't worth <a href="http://www.reuters.com/news_article.jhtml;jsessionid=RM5SGXP4GW3F0CRBAE0CFFAKEEATGIWD?type=topnews&StoryID=1099724">a dog's life</a>, to the dog's owner.  What's next, the murderers suing the murdered for lack of the canine companionship of the euthanized dogs?  <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2002/LAW/03/21/dog.mauling.trial/">Accounts </a>from early this year of the horrorible assault and of the owners' culpability left little to the imagination or to reasonable doubt.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The five-week trial gripped much of the nation as prosecutors described a horrific attack in which Whipple was bitten all over her body -- her throat ripped, her clothes torn off -- by at least one of the dogs.</p>

<p>The jury of seven men and five women saw graphic photos of the victim's ravaged body, with wounds visible from her ankles to her face, and pictures of the blood-stained hallway where the attack occurred. <br />
....<br />
Don Newton, the jury foreman, said the number of prior incidents involving the dogs undermined the defense claim that the mauling was nothing more than a tragic accident.</p>

<p>"It was a series of actions -- a series of failures to heed warnings, a series of careless taking of the dogs out and allowing them to lunge at people and attack people, that they had fallen into a pattern of actions which were inevitably leading to this result," Newton said.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>In today's report, once again the defendent present when the dogs attacked that fatal day, ". . . insisted she had no idea her 'loving' pets were capable of such an attack."</p>
