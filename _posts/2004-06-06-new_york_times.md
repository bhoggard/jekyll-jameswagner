<p>The <u>NYTimes</u> begins its <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2004/06/06/obituaries/06REAG.html?hp=&pagewanted=print&position=">obituary</a> of Ronald Reagan today with a three-column headline on the front page and it continues inside for a total of four more full-page sheets uninterrrupted by advertising.  The size of this death notice may be unprecedented, but the most newsworthy item is what's missing.</p>

<p>The words AIDS or HIV do not appear once.</p>

<p>This is beyond politics;  it's criminal <a href="/2004/06/reagan_more_dea.html">neglect</a>, if not part of a deliberate agenda, from the newspaper which was itself so guilty in ignoring or mishandling accounts of the plague during the Reagan years.   Now that same newspaper would have us regard as serious journalism its account of the life of our second-most-disastrous president, the man whose administration, in surviving its general <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/004147.html">malfeasance and treasons</a>, marked the final disintegration of American democracy.</p>

<p>We won't buy it.</p>
