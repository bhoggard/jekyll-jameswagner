<p>Willam Cohn's <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/ideas_opinions/story/50171p-47103c.html">letter</a> may have just pushed us over the edge.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>JETS IN OVERTIME</p>

<p>I have been a football fan since I was 14 years old, but I don't think a sporting event is important enough to rate the entire first, second and third pages of your paper. We had better reassess our values as to what is important, or our country will make the fall of Rome look like a picnic.</p>

<p>William Cohn</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Great headline for the letter!</p>

<p>The football splurge was followed yesterday by another blockbuster story.  In the midst of the heady competition offered by events unfolding throughout the city, the state and the world, every square inch of the first three pages of the paper, below the banner at the top, were devoted to the world-shaking news that the Wendy's murderer had penned a rap song in which he bragged about the slayings.  Headline:  "WENDY'S MURDER RAP."</p>

<p>There are a lot of reasons why we get the <u>Daily</u> <u>News</u> delivered every morning in addition to the <u>NYTimes</u>. but I think we're ready to wean ourselves from its seductions, now that <u>Newsday</u> seems to have re-entered the Gotham market.</p>
