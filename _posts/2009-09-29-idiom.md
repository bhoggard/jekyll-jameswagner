<p><a href="http://idiommag.com/">Idiom</a>.</p>

<p>It's beautiful (I love the blue logo), and it's very smart.  That's exactly how I wish I could describe <em>everything</em> in the world.  Unfortunately I can't always arrange it.  But Barry and I are going to have a lot to do with this new thing we're putting together, along with some very beautiful and awesome-smart people, so we're certainly going to be working on that beautiful and smart thing.</p>

<p>We're hoping our efforts will please visitors as well.</p>

<p><a href="http://idiommag.com/">Idiom</a> is our collaboration with a few other arts fanatics in the creation of a digital magazine, which we're describing as an online publication of urban artistic practice.  As announced in today's <a href="http://idiommag.com/about/">press release</a>, we will be asking creative and articulate "emerging artists, writers and arts professionals to report on, review, and otherwise cover overlooked or under-thought aspects of the larger creative community, Idiom offers a local, engaged counterpoint to the prevailing discourse of contemporary art."</p>

<p>That means <em>all</em> the arts, although we anticipate a heavy emphasis on the visual arts.</p>

<p>Stephen Squibb will be the editor, and the senior editor is B. Blagojevic.</p>
