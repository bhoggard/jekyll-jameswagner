<p>A gentle letter to the editor in today's New York City <u>Newsday</u> ends with this terse critique of the Republicans' evil politics of stem-cell research: <strong>"After all, we may differ as to when human life begins, but it certainly does not end at birth."</strong></p>

<p>The <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/news/opinion/letters/">full text</a> follows.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>President George W. Bush's antipathy to stem-cell research is a paradox wrapped in a conundrum. How can he have any respect for human life when his rush to war has resulted in the deaths of thousands of innocent people?</p>

<p>To say nothing of his role as governor of Texas, where he executed numerous people. If Bush was truly concerned with the dignity of human life, his policies would be 180 degrees different in almost every category. After all, we may differ as to when human life begins, but it certainly does not end at birth.</p>

<p>Max Podrecca</p>

<p>Manhattan</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Is anybody listening?</p>
