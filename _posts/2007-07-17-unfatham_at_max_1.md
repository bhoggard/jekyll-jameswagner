<p>It's been a while since Mid-Chelsea looked as exciting as it does right now.  I just counted nine "TOP PICKS" on <a href="http://www.artcal.net/">ArtCal</a>, and seven of them are Chelsea galleries;  that may be a record.  I suspect it has something to do with the fact that a number of artists are currently making an appearance there who have not previously been associated with our far-west-side, grown-ups-art scene.  Barry and I headed west for a few hours this afternoon and much of the time it felt like we were actually visiting Williamsburg or the Lower East Side - except that our local big-deal galleries can afford excellent air conditioning, their market being rather demanding.</p>

<p>We encountered our first stash of treasures at "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4996">Unfathom</a>", the beautiful Max Protetch group show curated by the artist <a href="http://artfagcity.blogspot.com/2006/01/good-abstraction-missed-during-press.html">Aaron Williams</a> and Stuart Krimko, the gallery's Director of Exhibitions.   The artists are Cameron, Nicolas Rule, Saul Chernick, <a href="/2006/04/meacham_stephan.html">Gary Stephan</a>, Jessica Dickinson, <a href="/2006/12/abstract_at_mit_1.html">Chris Martin</a>, Byron Kim, <a href="/2007/04/a_cloudy_days_e_1.html">Andrew Guenther</a>, Alfred Jensen, Mira Dancy, <a href="/2006/04/rico_gatson_at.html">Rico Gaston</a>, Marc Handelman, <a href="/2006/04/eric_heist_at_s.html">Eric Heist</a>, Katherine Keltner, Mike Cloud, Ed Blackburn and <a href="/2007/04/intelligent_des_1.html">Aaron Williams</a>.</p>
