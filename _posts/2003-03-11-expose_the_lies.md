<p>We need more heroes to match this one!  <a href="http://www.ellsberg.net/bio.htm">Daniel Ellsberg</a> <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines03/0311-11.htm">asks</a> for leaks of information which would discredit a maniacal and immoral policy.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Ellsberg, an ex-Marine and military analyst, said he held out hope that exposing alleged lies by the Bush administration could still avert an unjust war. He warned that whistleblowers may face ruin of their careers and marriages and be incarcerated.</p>

<p>"Don't wait until the bombs start falling," Ellsberg said at a Tuesday press conference in Washington. "If you know the public is being lied to and you have documents to prove it, go to Congress and go to the press."</p>

<p>Ellsberg did not leak the Pentagon Papers to the New York Times until 1971, although he says he had information in the mid-1960s that he now wishes he had leaked then.</p>

<p>"Do what I wish I had done before the bombs started falling" in Vietnam, Ellsberg said. "I think there is some chance that the truth could avert war."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>You know the stuff's out there.</p>
