<p>The picture's about a month old, but it <i>is</i> still Spring.  The image is that of the little <a href="Amelanchier canadensis">Shadblow Serviceberry</a> tree outside our back windows on the "<a href="/2002/08/a_tree_and_its.html">roof terrace</a>."  The name supposedly comes from its habit of blooming at the same time the Shad run in the Spring, but I can't vouch for its timing, as there have been no Shad sightings on the roof, or, for that matter, anywhere else in walking range of Chelsea.</p>

<p>Right now the tree is busy pushing out what will eventually be its reddish-purple crabapple-like <a href="http://project.bio.iastate.edu/trees/campustrees/Amelanchierfruit.html">fruits</a>.  The birds in the garden should go crazy over them.</p>
