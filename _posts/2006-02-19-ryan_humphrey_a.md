<p>Ryan Humphrey pulls out all the stops every time he does an installation, including near-arrest scenarios [think of <a href="/2005/06/ryan_humphrey.html">selling guns on the street</a>, even terrifically-fake guns, and even if you're an artist, should you happen to be peddling your stuff <a href="/2005/11/gallerist_asks.html">near</a> an insecure gallerist].</p>

<p>I like <a href="/2004/06/from_chelsea_to.html">his style</a>, but I always stay for the execution and the intelligence of his work.  Humphrey's art has never lacked <em>targets</em>, but this time he created real, target-practice targets.  Then he went and labelled each one, referencing, according to the press release, "adverse events with individuals, the pettiness of the 'art world' and engagement with larger entities."  He completed his project by pulling them out of the studio and shooting them.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/1891">Humphrey's show</a> has been beautifully installed at DCKT Contemporary on West 24th Street.  There's a larger variety of work to be seen in the gallery than what I'm showing here - more than just the targets.  I think I just got totally distracted by the beauty of these injured objects.</p>
