<p>I had heard months ago that the 150-year-old annual tradition of the White House Easter Egg Roll was cancelled this year, because of security concerns for the executive lawn.</p>

<p>But in the end we were not to be disappointed, at least not all of us.  A solution was found which superbly suits the current regime occupying the mansion and the nation.  The AP headline <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/aponline/national/AP-Easter-Egg-Roll.html?pagewanted=print&position=">says it all</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
<p><b>White House Holds Egg Roll for Military</b></p>
</blockquote>

<p>Can you say, "military occupation?"</p>

<blockquote>
<p>WASHINGTON (AP) -- Wide-eyed kids from military families scampered over the White House South Lawn on Monday, towing parents from egg pushing contests to close encounters with the Easter Bunny.</p>

<p>Lynne Cheney, wife of Vice President Dick Cheney, hosted the scaled-down version of the annual Easter egg roll, which included about 12,000 parents and young children. The occasion normally draws a crowd of about 40,000, but because of security concerns this year's tickets were distributed through the Defense Department exclusively to military families.</p>

<p>"All of you have dads and moms who have been defending America,'' Mrs. Cheney told the children.  "We think your moms and dads are terrific."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Even during World War II the event remained open to the nation, although it was moved from the White House lawn to the National Zoo.</p>
