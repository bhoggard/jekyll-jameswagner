<p>D'Amelio Terras has two dynamite shows up at the same time, and they will both be running for almost four more weeks, until August 10.  "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/5048">Heralds of Creative Anachronism</a>", which occupies the gallery's smaller space, includes five abstract works by four artists, <a href="http://www.saatchi-gallery.co.uk/artists/joe_bradley.htm">Joe Bradley</a>, <a href="http://www.danielhesidence.com/">Daniel Hesidence</a>, <a href="/2006/12/abstract_at_mit_1.html">Chris Martin</a> [see also my July 17 <a href="/2007/07/unfatham_at_max_1.html">post</a>], and <a href="http://www.rogerwhite.net/">Roger White</a>.</p>

<p>The gallery describes the choice of four male painters for this show as a deliberate reference to the 60's art group known as <a href="http://arts.guardian.co.uk/features/story/0,,1948009,00.html">BMPT</a> (Daniel Buren, Olivier Mosset, Michel Parmentier, Niele Toroni), but admits that the exhibition is "a light-hearted attempt to create a movement, even a temporary one, for the duration of this exhibition".   In fact I think what they're saying is that the idea here, very unlike BMPT's, is to question the whole notion of movements or "schools" in the practice of contemporary abstract painting.</p>

<p>Whatever the show's conceit, the work is terrific.</p>

<p>No curator is credited.</p>
