<p>Barry and I had seen the film several years ago, through the good offices of <a href="/2007/02/homeless_museum_1.html">Filip Noterdaeme</a>, but last Thursday we both jumped at the chance of seeing "<a href="http://www.thebrucehighqualityfoundation.com/Site/Leau_de_Vie.html">L'eau De Vie Un Film De Jean Luc Godard</a>" again, especially since it meant lounging about on the roof of the <a href="http://x-initiative.org/blog/">X Initiative</a> with other fans of <a href="https://jameswagner.com/2009/08/bruce_at_harris_lieb.html">The Bruce High Quality Foundation</a> on a beautiful summer evening above the Hudson River.</p>

<p>It was even better than I had expected - both the film and the ambiance we found on the roof of the former Dia space on West 22nd Street.  I think "L'eau" is a small masterpiece;  well, maybe not so small.  The soundtrack was really, really brilliant, and I think I noticed just how brilliant for the first time, thanks to the terrific rooftop sound system.  I also appreciated the additional edge provided by the wavy, "silver screen" on which it was projected.  No, really!</p>

<p>The video was shot in December, 2005 on location in Miami during the annual Art Basel tradeshow, on sound-synched black and white super 8.  It's an homage to Jean-Luc Godard, a <em>cri de coeur</em> for real community, and a paean to a simpler world before the invention of art fairs.</p>
