<p>The erotic frisson of much of the work in his earlier walls is missing in the <a href="/2006/05/joe_ovelman_ret.html">installation</a> Joe Ovelman completed early this morning.  Ovelman has been spending a lot of time in São Paulo and it seems to agree with him.</p>

<p>The photography is almost painfully beautiful, but his work is always beautiful and very often there's some pain too.  These photographs are also, well, very <em>happy</em>, but they are not dull.   There is nothing casual about their composition and they are clearly the work of a master.  They are also intensely personal and they reveal a private world which is, while not quite exotic, warmly exceptional and pretty inaccessible even to those who may be privileged to travel a lot.</p>

<p>It's very interesting to me that many of the images are of walls, but these walls seem to shelter more than they exclude.  Ovelman lives in these spaces in more ways than one, and we can wish we did too.</p>

<p>Actually, until these fragile paper bills disappear this world really is a little bit of ours as well.</p>
