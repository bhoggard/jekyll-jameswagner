<p>Doesn't <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&ncid=578&e=3&cid=578&u=/nm/20030306/ts_nm/iraq_britain_dc">this</a> say just about everything we need to know about democracy in the U.K., the U.S., or indeed, the world?</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Until now, Blair has said he reserved the right to go to war without U.N. authorization in case a singular "unreasonable veto" was wielded.</p>

<p>But in a debate on music television channel MTV he appeared to extend that proviso to include multiple vetoes.</p>

<p>"If there was a veto applied by one of the countries with a veto or by countries that <i>I</i> [my italic] thought were applying the veto unreasonably then in those circumstances <i>I</i> [my italic] would," Blair said when asked if he would go to war without a new U.N. mandate.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Baby Bush [who himself routinely speaks in terms of "I" will do this or that] and toady Blair each see war and peace as their personal decisions and do not even pretend to be representing a constituancy.</p>
