Hany Armanious <em>Wall Rubbing #1</em> 2007 clogged sandpaper 8.5" x 10.5" (12" x 16" framed) [large detail of installation]</p>

<p><a href="http://www.foxyproduction.com/home">Foxy Production</a> showed several small, ethereal drawings by Hany Armanious in their booth at the <a href="http://www.thearmoryshow.com/">Armory</a> [large detail of one shown here].   The artist alters simple pieces of black sandpaper (emery paper?) by carefully orchestrating their contact with white wall surfaces.   The result resembles a photographic image of a distant galaxy, but with an anomalous textural life inhabiting its surface.   Each piece rests near the plexi on the bottom of a deep fillet inside its frame, and then leans back on the mat; they are as much sculptural objects as drawings.</p>

<p>Armanious is currently in a small group show at the gallery, titled "Surface Wave".  It opened February 25, but I haven't yet seen what looks like very interesting work from artists new even to this gallery of new artists.</p>
