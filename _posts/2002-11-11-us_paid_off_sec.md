<p>But of course we already knew <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines02/1111-02.htm">this</a>.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>UNITED NATIONS - Friday's unanimous vote in the U.N. Security Council supporting the U.S. resolution on weapons inspections in Iraq was a demonstration of Washington's ability to wield its vast political and economic power, say observers.</p>

<p>''Only a superpower like the United States could have pulled off a coup like this,'' an Asian diplomat told IPS.</p>

<p>The unanimous 15-0 vote, he said, was obtained through considerable political and diplomatic pressure. The lobbying, he added, was not done at the United Nations, but in various capitals.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>We can also imagine how much we promised the pesky permanent members, Russia, China and France (not discussed in this piece, but all of whom opposed the Bush war until last week), in money, silence, oil concessions, military goodies or whatever is currently dearest to their own hearts or pocketbooks.</p>
