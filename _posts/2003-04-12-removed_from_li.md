<p>The Israeli government has <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/04/12/international/middleeast/12MIDE.html">killed</a> another young <a href="http://www.palsolidarity.org/">International</a>, as he tried to shield children from a tank-mounted machine gun in Gaza.</p>

<p>About a dozen members of the peace movement had been trying to set up a protest tent on a road in an attempt to block Israeli army incursions into a Gaza refugee camp.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Along the way, the protesters were joined by several children, the witnesses said. When the group was about 200 yards away from three tanks, soldiers opened fire from a tank-mounted machine gun, the witnesses said.</p>

<p>Hurndall and another foreign activist tried to get two children out of the line of fire, [the witnesses] said.  "Thomas (Hurndall) grabbed one of their hands and as soon as he did that a tank fired at him, hitting him in the head," [AP photographer] Hamra said.</p>

<p>The photographer said the children were not throwing rocks at the troops and that he saw nothing that would have provoked the troops.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The incident occurred yesterday, friday.  Today the beautiful 21-year-old Manchester, England, photographer has been <a href="http://electronicintifada.net/v2/article1360.shtml">declared brain dead</a>.  As I am writing this, there is a <a href="http://sf.indymedia.org/news/2003/04/1599348.php">report</a> that he has been removed from life support.  On the scale of decades of Israeli and American crimes in Palestine and throughout the Middle East, this news would be unlikely to shock a world inured to news about the mad policies which have already removed so many others from our support for their very lives.</p>
