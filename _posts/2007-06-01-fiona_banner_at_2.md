<p>I spotted this drawing by <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/britain/exhibitions/turnerprize/2002/banner.htm">Fiona Banner</a> from the floor of the atrium space at MoMA yesterday.  It's hanging on the third floor and part of it can be seen through a cutout in the wall.  It's a very beautiful thing, a new <a href="http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?criteria=O%3AAD%3AE%3A8033&page_number=3&template_id=1&sort_order=1">acquisition</a> for the Museum, but I'm not surprised that I was attracted to it, because of what I had seen the last time I saw the artist's work.  <a href="/2006/03/fiona_banner_at.html">Here</a> is a  2006 post.</p>

<p>Please excuse the imperfect image (although MoMA's own is even less adequate, and the reflections on the plexiglas here do seem to add another level of mystery to the work).</p>
