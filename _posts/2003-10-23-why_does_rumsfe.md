<p>Last night I wrote that I was scared after hearing that the Secretary of War is going around saying that we're losing the "war on terrorism".  Why?  It just can't be good news if it's coming from him.  I can't expect that he's saying this stuff in order to admit that the whole thing was misconceived in the first place, or because he's about to resign in favor of a replacement which the world might actually survive.</p>

<p>No, I've come to expect the worst from this administration, and I'm still usually unpleasantly surprised.  I think Runmmy's current ruminations are all about preparing us all for something really big.</p>

<p>Things aren't going well?  The problem is obviously the liberals and the Constitution huggers, those who just aren't taking this war seriously enough.   We still have too many freedoms.  In fact the <em>world</em> still has too much independence.</p>

<p>Remember that these guys don't expect to ever leave, so this administration would be more than comfortable with a solution which would be some combination of really scary secret police, an elite security staff and perhaps a federal army command, all of them loyal directly to the White House.</p>

<p>Sound familiar? Well, it doesn't have to so redolent of another time and place, but similar dispositions find similar solutions.  The necessity for all of this would of course become perfectly clear after the next terrorist event, scheduled or otherwise.  We'll hardly notice the imposition of martial law when it comes.</p>

<p>There, I've just outlined the worst-case scenario but I really would like to be <em>pleasantly</em> surprised this time.</p>

<p>If we actually do survive this regime, I hope we will finally be a people without illusions - of blessedness, grandeur, superiority, sanctity, whatever.   Enlightenment.  Let's go for enlightenment instead of the other stuff.</p>
