<p><strong>UPDATE:</strong>  <a href="http://manbartlett.tumblr.com/post/631242058/on-24hecho">the artist speaks for himself</a></p>

<p>As I'm writing this <a href="/2010/03/the_last_hour_of_man.html">Man Bartlett</a> is in the third hour of his contribution to P.P.O.W. Gallery's <a href="http://www.thehostessproject.com/">Hostess Project</a>, "<a href="http://manbartlett.com/24hEcho/">#24hEcho</a>".</p>

<p>The artist has vowed that for 24 hours he will repeat, into a webcam, whatever we tell him to.  "I will be present in repeating your words. I will be your puppet, your sounding board, your refuge. Otherwise, I will be silent."</p>

<p>If you want to part of the performance go to Bartlett's <a href="http://manbartlett.com/24hEcho/index_live.htm">website</a> for further directions.</p>

<p>Barry and I were at the gallery at 7 o'clock tonight, just as he had arranged himself behind his computer, and the tweeting half of our partnership communicated this from his iPhone, an homage to a bit of the history we share:</p>

<blockquote>
<p><a href="http://www.williampowhida.com/">William Powhida</a> is the kindest, bravest, warmest, most wonderful human being I've ever known in my life.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The artist will be present until 7 o'clock tomorrow night, and, as they used to say in the darker ages, <em>the lines will be open</em> throughout the night.   Help keep the guy company, and see if you can inspire a conversation with strangers.</p>
