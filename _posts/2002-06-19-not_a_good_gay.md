<p>I've admired Richard Goldstein for years, largely through his pieces in the <u>Village</u> <u>Voice</u>.  This week <a href="http://www.thenation.com/doc.mhtml?i=20020701&c=2&s=goldstein">his essay </a>in <u>The</u> <u>Nation</u> really did it for me.</p>

<p>I'm linking it here because Goldstein has done an excellent job of describing my own social and political posture and my position as a member of a pariah community.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The queer community is the spawn of a marriage between socialism and bohemianism more than a century ago. This heady union, which begat gay liberation, has been all but ignored by the culture.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Some of my friends and relatives will be surprised to know that I am not a "retreating liberal" and I am not a "good gay."  I blame over sixty years of a certain amount of dissembling in order to survive in a frightened and frightening world for any appearances which may have encouraged that misunderstanding. I am grateful however to chance, and for the good graces of friends and strangers, that I have become more and more radical over the years.  I intend to keep heading in that direction.</p>

<p>What I am now is a leftist and a queer, and for me the two are inseparable.</p>
