<p><strong>A BROKEN NEW ORLEANS ROUSES ITSELF</strong></p>

<p>I have no way of knowing how central this particular appeal may become, but it came to me through a friend and I share its anger and its emphasis on preserving a devasatated community intact.  The call comes from <a href="http://www.qecr.org/Public_QECR.nsf/PublicLinkIDs/Public2AA857E1647E8AE185257074003D5B82?OpenDocument&justframe&style&notitle">some really good people</a>, and I believe it should be broadcast widely.  I decided not to wait for the promised formal press release.</p>

<blockquote>
<p><strong>Displaced New Orleans Community Demands Action, <br />
Accountability and Initiates A People’s <br />
Hurricane Fund</strong></p>

<p>Not until the fifth day of the federal government’s <br />
inept and inadequate emergency response to the <br />
New Orleans’ disaster did George Bush even acknowledge<br />
it was ‘unacceptable.’ ‘Unacceptable’ doesn’t begin to <br />
describe the depth of the neglect, racism and classism <br />
shown to the people of New Orleans. The government’s <br />
actions and inactions were criminal. New Orleans, a <br />
city whose population is almost 70% percent black, 40% <br />
illiterate, and many are poor, was left day after day<br />
to drown, to starve and to die of disease and thirst.</p>

<p>The people of New Orleans will not go quietly into the<br />
night, scattering across this country to become <br />
homeless in countless other cities while federal <br />
relief funds are funneled into rebuilding casinos, <br />
hotels, chemical plants and the wealthy white <br />
districts of New Orleans like the French Quarter and<br />
the Garden District. We will not stand idly by while <br />
this disaster is used as an opportunity to replace our <br />
homes with newly built mansions and condos in a <br />
gentrified New Orleans.</p>

<p>Community Labor United (CLU), a coalition of the <br />
progressive organizations throughout New Orleans, has <br />
brought community members together for eight years to <br />
discuss socio-economic issues.  We have been <br />
communicating with people from The Quality Education <br />
as a Civil Right Campaign, the Algebra Project, the <br />
Young People’s Project and the Louisiana Research <br />
Institute for Community Empowerment.  We are <br />
preparing a press release and framing document that <br />
will be out as a draft later today for comments.</p>

<p>Here is what we are calling for:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>We are calling for all New Orleanians remaining in the<br />
city to be evacuated immediately.</p>

<p>We are calling for information about where every <br />
evacuee was taken.</p>

<p>We are calling for black and <br />
progressive leadership to come together to meet in <br />
Baton Rouge to initiate the formation of a <br />
Community Oversight Committee of evacuees from all the<br />
sites.  This committee will demand to <br />
oversee FEMA, the Red Cross and other organizations <br />
collecting resources on behalf of our people.</p>

<p>We are calling for volunteers to enter the shelters <br />
where our people are and to assist parents with <br />
housing, food, water, health care and access to aid. <br />
We are calling for teachers and educators to carve out<br />
some time to come to evacuation sites and teach our <br />
children.</p>

<p>We are calling for city schools and universities near <br />
evacuation sites to open their doors for our <br />
children to go to school.</p>

<p>We are calling for health care workers and mental <br />
health workers to come to evacuation sites to <br />
volunteer.</p>

<p>We are calling for lawyers to investigate the wrongful<br />
death of those who died, to  protect the land of <br />
the displaced, to investigate whether the levies broke<br />
due to natural and other related matters.</p>

<p>We are calling for evacuees from our community to <br />
actively participate in the rebuilding of New <br />
Orleans.</p>

<p>We are calling for the addresses of all the relevant <br />
list serves and press contacts to send our <br />
information.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>We are in the process of setting up a central command <br />
post in Jackson, MS, where we will have <br />
phone lines, fax, email and a web page to centralize <br />
information.  We will need volunteers to staff this <br />
office.</p>

<p>We have set up a People’s Hurricane Fund that will be <br />
directed and administered by New Orleanian evacuees.  <br />
The Young People’s Project, a 501(c)3 organization <br />
formed by graduates of the Algebra Project, has agreed <br />
to accept donations on behalf of this fund.  Donations <br />
can be mailed to:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The People’s Hurricane Fund <br />
c/o The Young People’s Project<br />
99 Bishop Allen Drive<br />
Cambridge, MA  02139</p>
</blockquote>

<p>If you have comments of how to proceed or need more <br />
information, please email them to Curtis <br />
Muhammad (muhammadcurtis@bellsouth.net) and Becky <br />
Belcore (bbelcore@hotmail.com).</p>

<p>Thank you</p>
</blockquote>
