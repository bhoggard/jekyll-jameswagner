<p>The front page of this morning's real <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/pages/pageone/scan/index.html">New York Times</a> looks an awful lot like the fake <a href="http://www.nytimes-se.com/">New York Times</a> <a href="http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2008/11/12/pranksters-spoof-the-times/?scp=1&sq=alex%20jones&st=cse">published</a> by the <a href="http://theyesmen.org/">Yes Men</a> with the help of many others last November 12.  My own hard copy of today's Late [City] Edition differs only slightly from the one shown above.  It adds a story which suggests the feds are getting closer to nationalizing the banks.</p>

<p>Probably the most significant element <em>missing</em> from the February 27, 2009, paper is the banner headline on the July 4, 2009, edition shown below:  "<strong>IRAQ WAR ENDS</strong>" - but then we still have more than four months to get that one right.</p>
