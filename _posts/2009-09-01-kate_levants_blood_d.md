<p><strong>CORRECTION:  The blood collection is today (Wednesday) and Thursday, not Thursday and Friday.  The show comes down at the end of the day on Friday.</strong></p>

<p>Kate Levant appears to have pulled off a pretty amazing stunt at Zach Feuer with her exhibition, and performance, "<a href="http://www.artcat.com/exhibits/9859">Blood Drive</a>", what the artist describes as the product of "an open platform invitation towards a commission to produce promotional material for a blood donation drive."</p>

<p>Not your usual late summer art show.</p>

<p>The visuals have already been assembled in the gallery for some time, but the art all  comes together over the next three days, beginning with a performance tomorrow night at eight by Jacques Vidal and Noel Anderson, "a glue that will stick anything together forever".   A real-time community blood drive will follow from 1:30 until 7 on both Wednesday and Thursday, with the show wrapping at the end of the day on Friday, September 4.   You can sign up by emailing the gallery at  info@zachfeuer.com.  I have no idea how it's going to go off, but this last act will be as interactive as the crowd wants it to be, and I trust these artists to make it more than interesting.</p>

<p>The artists "commissioned" for the installation itself are Noel Anderson, BOBO, Brian Faucette, Michael E. Smith, Elaine Stocki and Jacques Vidal.</p>

<p>Levant already has an auspicious triumph, but this is hardly a surprise to anyone who has had the privilege of encountering her before.</p>

<p>We first met Levant in October, 2006, when she arrived to help <a href="/2007/07/jacques_vidal_m.html">Vidal</a> install his large light box, "<a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/274386096/in/set-72157594318351010/">The Holy Art Project</a>", while we were installing the show we curated at Dam, Stuhltrager, "<a href="http://danglingbetween.com/">Dangling Between The Real Thing And The Sign In The Window</a>".  Just two months later we were delighted to learn that her art was as impressive as her ability to pitch in for a friend and charm the pants off two strangers.   We saw her work in a wonderful installation, "<a href="http://www.artcat.com/exhibits/3703">Attic</a>", inside Anton Kern's 21st street annex for a couple of weeks in the middle of December.  That show, which was curated by Erin Somerville, also included work by Smith, who is represented in "Blood Drive".  Barry put together a small <a href="http://bloggy.com/2007/01/attic_at_anton.html">slide show</a> of images from "Attic" on Bloggy.</p>

<p>There are more images of this show on <a href="http://www.16miles.com/2009/08/blood-drive-at-zach-feuer-gallery.html">16 miles of string</a>, and documention of other work by Levant and Smith at <a href="http://detroitarts.blogspot.com/2007/06/mike-smithkate-levant-hilberry.html">Detroit Arts</a> and <a href="http://try-har-der.blogspot.com/2009/07/artist-of-1143-am-michael-e-smith.html">tryharder</a>.</p>
