<p>He doesn't belong there either.</p>

<p>Bush and his wife are planning to be in Britain this month on a very rare "<a href="http://www.army.mod.uk/ceremonialandheritage/household/state_visit.htm">state visit</a>".</p>

<p>There are a lot of Americans who would be very very happy to see him thrown out.  The Brits have our best wishes for every success in accomplishing that, but I expect they'll do very well indeed without any help from us (even if that were possible, protests already having been effectively proscribed here in the U.S., where they are not totally ignored).</p>

<p>Forgive us the presumption, but, Like Lord Nelson, we expect every Englishman and Englishwoman to do his or her duty.  The urgency arises from the fact that  we ourselves cannot.</p>

<p>It's interesting that most of the peaks and perks that actually define such a glamorous visit seem to have already been <a href="http://english.aljazeera.net/NR/exeres/869845E7-0A91-4025-906D-3508073BBD01.htm">ruled out</a>, because of the British and American authorities' fears that the people will in fact be doing exactly that when the Bushes arrive.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>President Bush, visiting London in November for three days, was looking forward to meeting the Queen at Buckingham Palace.</p>

<p>This was to have entailed a grand  procession along the Mall with all the pomp and ceremony of a state visit, but it has been cancelled over fears that antiwar campaigners would stage a colourful and angry protest to overshadow the event.</p>

<p>The President was also due to address the British Parliament on his three day visit. However, that too has been cancelled.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I mean, Harold Pinter is on the barricades!  How much more respectable can a movement be?  See the Stop the War Coalition <a href="http://www.stopwar.org.uk/">site</a> for details and helpful hints..</p>
