<p>I've uploaded <a href="http://jameswagner.smugmug.com/Performance/Stockhausen-Originale-Kitchen/i-8qC5VkM">347 iphone images</a> I had captured - with the enthusiastic encouragement of the curator, Nick Hallet - during the performance of <a href="http://www.karlheinzstockhausen.org/">Karlheinz Stockhausen</a>'s <a href="http://thekitchen.org/event/karlheinz-stockhausen-s-originale">"Originale" on November 7 at The Kitchen</a>.  They are of varying quality, but I show virtually every picture I shot, including a few blanks, or near-blanks, because the bricolage suggests the beautiful energy of the evening, along with its [suggestion of] mayhem.</p>

<p>I virtually <a href="/2004/10/stockhausen.html">grew up listening to Stockhausen</a>.  His music was broadcast by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westdeutscher_Rundfunk">Westdeutscher Rundfunk [WDR]</a> in the 1950s, and that station's avant garde music programs (which included the music of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_Werner_Henze">Hans Werner Henze</a>, among other composers considered late-20th-century giants today) were carried by the CBC, to which I listened in Detroit, via their Windsor, Ontario outlet across the river.</p>

<p>While I missed the first performance of this piece, Stockhausen's first venture into theater, in Köln, in 1962, and was living in Germany when <a href="http://vimeo.com/8384545">"Originale" was produced at Judson Church</a>.  I wouldn't have missed this one for the world, and I felt deeply honored to be at The Kitchen on Friday, where I rewarded far beyond all my expectations.  I was in heaven, and when it was over, I didn't want to leave.</p>

<p>It was a very funny, and a very happy evening.  Because of that I think I have a much better understanding - and even more love - for the composer's cycle of seven operas, "Licht", than I had before being immersed within this fantastic musical theater piece, and I already loved those operas dearly.</p>
