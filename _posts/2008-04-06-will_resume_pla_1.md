<p>Yeah, this site's been mighty quiet for over a week, and to me the pause button seems like it's been pressed for twice that long.</p>

<p>Right after the art fairs I was first feeling pretty burned out, but I had begun to post entries describing some of the work I had liked most when I found myself distracted by things totally unrelated to the visual arts, including preparing a special dinner for friends early this week.  The next day I came down with some general malady which developed into a full ague.  It may suffice to say that my head swam, my skin ached, and I found it painful even to <em>think</em> of touching the keyboard with my fingertips.</p>

<p>I'm feeling much better today, even after seeing Caryl Churchill's not-so-upbeat "<a href="http://theater2.nytimes.com/2008/03/17/theater/reviews/17drunk.html?scp=1&sq=drunk+enough+to+say+I+love+you&st=nyt">Drunk Enough To Say I Love You</a>" just two hours ago.  Starting tonight or tomorrow I'll resume where I <a href="/2008/03/ann_craven_with_1.html">left off</a> on March 29th, with some more quick notes drawn from my camera.  These will probably continue until I become bored doing the same series, or until I'm distracted by some new baubles.</p>
