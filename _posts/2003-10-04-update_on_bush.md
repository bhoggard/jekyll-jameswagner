<p>I've located the complete statement I described <a href="/2003/10/better_lock_tha.html">yesterday</a> in which Bush announced that "Free nations don't develop weapons of mass destruction."  Silly us.  After all the fuss it appears right on the White House <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/news/releases/2003/10/20031003-4.html">website</a>, in the section, "Jobs & Economic Growth."</p>

<p>The context is described as remarks delivered at the Midwest Airlines Center in Milwaukee yesterday morning.  This is the entire paragraph:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>We have more work to do in Iraq. A free Iraq, a peaceful Iraq will help change an area of the world that needs peace and freedom. A peaceful Iraq and a free Iraq is part of our campaign to rid the world of terror. And that's why the thugs in Iraq still resist us, because they can't stand the thought of free societies. They understand what freedom means. <em>See, free nations are peaceful nations. Free nations don't attack each other. Free nations don't develop weapons of mass destruction.</em> [my italics]  There will be a free and peaceful Iraq. What's taking place in Iraq is the evolution of a society, to be democratic in nation -- nature, a society in which the people are better off.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>So there it is, boys and girls.<br />
