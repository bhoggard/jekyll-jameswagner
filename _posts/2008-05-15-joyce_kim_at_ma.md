<p>Joyce Kim was showing some great paintings at the <a href="http://sharpeartfdn.qwestoffice.net/">Marie Walsh Sharpe Foundation</a>'s Space Program open studios late last month.   When Barry and I walked in we were surprised to get a partial preview of what I'm certain must be a very impressive show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/7011">Thierry Goldberg</a>.   We haven't yet visited it, but I'd have already made it a must just to see <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2008/04/11/arts/design/11moma.html?_r=1&partner=rssnyt&emc=rss&oref=slogin">Jonathan Hartshorn</a>'s contribution.</p>

<p>I'm shocked that these two images reveal so much.  The camera seems to want to show us everything at once.  While I was actually in the space most of the detail you see here wasn't immediately apparent, in spite of the fact that these canvases were hanging in a south-facing room with strong natural afternoon light.  Sometimes I can't say it enough:   If you can, go see these pieces yourself.  Let them reveal their subtleties at their own speed.</p>
