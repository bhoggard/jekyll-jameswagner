<p>We visited the twice-weekly organic market in Prenzlauer Berg this afternoon.  It rained lightly for much of the day today, so the light in these pictures is just a little exotic.  The vegetables at the top on the left look especially weird, but I swear the color is very accurate.  I have no idea what the beautiful roots might be.  I can't read the sign.  This particular stall seemed to specialize in produce from Israel.</p>

<p>The second photo should give some idea of how gorgeous the produce was.  This cabbage and broccoli looked unbelievably perfect, and almost fresher than they would have while still in the ground.</p>

<p>The third image is that of the busy exchange at another stall, and the last offers a peek at the crowd and the ambience of the immediate neighborhood of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%A4the_Kollwitz">Käthe-Kollwitz</a>-Platz.  The easiest way to describe it to someone familiar with New York would be to say it's not unlike Brooklyn's <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Park_Slope">Park Slope</a> - only different.</p>
