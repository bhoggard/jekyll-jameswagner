<p>The Great American movie?  Not quite, but it'll do for this week at least.  Queer, yes, but not just for queers.  We saw <a href="http://www.rebeccatuynman.com/luster.html#"><u>Luster</u></a> last night at the <a href="http://www.newfestival.org/cgi-bin/iowa/films.html?film=231">New Festival </a>and got much more than we expected.</p>

<p>Ok, it's set in L.A., the actors are attractive (and not looking like Chelsea or Hollywood), it's very cool, very sexy, very beautiful, with a great soundtrack (half the scenes are in love with the perfect little music store) and the plot is not predictable.</p>

<p>Most oddly, with its stealth morality and easy didacticism, it could serve as the best ever after-school special, but only in a more perfect, grown-up world where we don't have the burden of our inherited prejudices.</p>

<p>Best line, from a very sexy guy-<i>naif</i>, "I'm a lesbian!"</p>

<p>Write your congressperson.  Get it into your neighborhood.  Don't lose this film!</p>
