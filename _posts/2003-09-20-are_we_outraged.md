<p>Does freedom of speech mean that distinguished institutions of higher education are <i>required</i> to invite nuts and war criminals in order to support a commercially-organized <a href="http://festival.newyorker.com/index.htm">event</a> described as <u>New</u> <u>Yorker</u> magazine's "three-day cultural fest, celebrating the finest in the arts, music, fiction, poetry, journalism, and humor"?</p>

<p><a href="http://www.counterpunch.org/cockburn02282003.html">Jeffrey Goldberg</a> will interview <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines03/0604-10.htm">Paul Wolfowitz</a> in the New School's <a href="http://festival.newyorker.com/schedule_new.cfm">Tishman Auditorium</a> at 3 pm tomorrow, Sunday, afternoon.</p>

<p>The possibilities of this <i>piece de theatre</i> sound even more delicious than that presented by the holy visit of the old <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200309#1111">lama</a> in our own secular and public <a href="http://www.dalailamanyc.org/index.php4?fuseaction=home.talk">Central Park</a> tomorrow at roughly the same time.*  Numbers count, whether huge or manageable, so a disturbance near either altar might attract some attention.  Creative signage can be very effective.</p>

<p>In any event, it seems that kind of disturbance would be very appropriate at each venue.</p>

<p>The West 12th Street event is certainly timely, since Goldberg is one of the most important sponsors of the fiction of Saddam Hussein's ties to September 11, Wolfowitz was one of the key architects of our invasion of Iraq, and just this week Bush has finally had to deny the connection authored by Goldberg, a connection which made the Wolfowitz-championed invasion acceptable to a frightened and gullible American public.</p>

<p>There will be no representation on that stage of those who either knew the truth, or who opposed our massive destruction of an entire nation - except of course for the protesters in the audience and outside the theatre.  All such are welcome, at the very least to save the honor of the New School.</p>
