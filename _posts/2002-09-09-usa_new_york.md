<p><a href="http://www.paulauster.co.uk/">Paul Auster</a> has a <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/09/09/opinion/09AUST.html">piece</a> about the American heartland in today's <u>NYTimes</u>.  It's a good read.  This is an excerpt.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Crazy New York, inspiring New York, fractious New York, ugly New York, beautiful New York, impossible New York — New York as a laboratory of human contradictions. America has had a tortured, even antagonistic relationship with our city over the years, but to an astonishing number of people from Michigan, Maine and Nebraska, the five boroughs are a living embodiment of what the United States is all about: diversity, tolerance and equality under the law. Alone among American cities, New York is more than just a place or an agglomeration of people. It is also an idea.</p>
</blockquote>
