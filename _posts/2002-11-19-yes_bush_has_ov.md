<p><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/19/opinion/L19VOTE.html">How else</a> do we account for the silence and obvious indifference of the overwhelming majority of the U.S. population in the face of the Republicans' radical refashioning of the American economy, society and state?</p>

<blockquote>
<p>November 19, 2002</p>

<p><b>Low-Turnout Mandate</b></p>

<p>To the Editor:</p>

<p>Re "Orwell Was Right" (letter, Nov. 16):</p>

<p>Decrying the continuing assault against our civil liberties, the letter writer asks, "Does a 40 percent voting turnout give the government a mandate to invade our privacy this way?"</p>

<p>The answer, of course, is yes. That low turnout is precisely what gives the government the mandate to act as if no one cares and no one is looking.   <br />
WILLIAM F. BENNETT<br />
Somerville, Mass., Nov. 16, 2002</p>
</blockquote>
