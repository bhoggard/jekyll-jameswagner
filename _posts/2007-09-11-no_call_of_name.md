GUANTANAMO DELENDA EST!</p>

<p>It's the eleventh of September  again.  Yes, it happens once a year.   But I'm not interested in adding to the <em>revanchisme</em> stoked by every mention of the terrible events which occurred in my city six years ago.  I am interested in the fact that even if we wanted to we would be unable to read a list of the names of the <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/6040054.stm">hundreds of thousands</a> of people we have killed in the name of our <em>own</em> dead (many of whom were from countries other than the U.S.).</p>

<p>Moreover, the <a href="/2007/08/guantanamo_dele_2.html">continuing shame</a> of our concentration camps at Guantanamo and elsewhere in the world doesn't seem to be worthy of the attention of many who actually do oppose the war in Iraq.</p>

<p>We are letting ourselves be ridden by fools, fanatics, politicians and arms suppliers - and those who profit from the evil mischief done in our name.  The killing could stop, the camps could be closed and the terror could be defused, but not if we refuse to look at the world outside  - and continue to let others exploit us.</p>
