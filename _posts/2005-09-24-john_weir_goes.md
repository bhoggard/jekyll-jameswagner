<p>The store of hurricane Katrina literature is already pretty vast, but it hasn't always been very, well, <em>literate</em>.  <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/1555834728/qid=1127535858/sr=8-1/ref=pd_bbs_1/104-4795431-6944717?v=glance&s=books&n=507846">John Weir</a> didn't get to Houston until some time after the disaster in New Orleans which saw thousands of people flee to Texas and into the Astrodome, but he came away with a story about . . . home.</p>

<p>It's no longer breaking news, and in fact evacuees in the Astrodome itself have now been [<em>re-evacuated</em>?] in the face of a new hurricane threat, but his is the kind of intelligent, primary-source account we will need in order to understand what went so terribly wrong in Louisiana this month.</p>
