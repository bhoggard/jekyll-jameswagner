<p>What kind of war is this?</p>

<p>Woman says airport security forced her to <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2002/US/08/08/airport.breastmilk.ap/index.html">drink breast milk</a>.  But we still can't manage to get <a href="http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/local/showcase/chi-020805ohare.story?coll=chi%2Dnews%2Dhed">luggage screeners </a>installed (best estimate, maybe sometime next year).</p>

<blockquote>
<p>A woman says a security guard at Kennedy Airport forced her to drink from three bottles of her own breast milk to demonstrate the liquid posed no threat to other passengers.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>In her defense, New York civil rights attorney Ron Kuby said, "The number of middle-aged, lactating white women who passed through al-Qaida training is probably negligible."</p>

<p>Meanwhile, at O'Hare Airport, like most sites, still without its quota of explosives-detection systems,</p>

<blockquote>
<p>federal and local officials today heralded the deployment of 200 federal [biped] screeners in the International Terminal, the smallest and already most secure terminal at the airport. They called the move a bold step to professionalize aviation security and regain the trust of travelers.</p>

<p>"It will be obvious to both the novice and the professional traveler that the security bar has been significantly raised," said Isaac Richardson, federal security director at O'Hare.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Uh huh.</p>
