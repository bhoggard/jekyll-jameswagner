<p>Tomorrow is big.</p>

<p><a href="http://occupywallst.org/">#OccupyWallStreet</a> is likely to move into another dimension with the "Community/Labor March to Wall St against Corporate Greed and the Big Banks".  A number of unions and community groups will be a part of the march of <a href="http://wearethe99percent.tumblr.com/">the 99 percent</a> Thursday afternoon.  These include the United Federation of Teachers, 32BJ SEIU; 1199 SEIU Workers United;  and Transport Workers Union Local 100;  PSC-CUNY United NY;  the Strong Economy for All Coalition;  the Working Families Party;  Vocal-NY;   New York Communities for Change;  Community Voices Heard;  Alliance for Quality Education;  [list in formation].</p>

<p>It has been announced that this march is "permitted", meaning that the permits which the  <a href="/2007/04/radical_homosex_1.html">NYPD requires</a> for any public assembly have actually been obtained, having been sought in deference to the preferences of some of the groups participating.</p>

<p>It was to start at City Hall (250 Broadway), but the assembly point has been changed, presumably in response to the anticipated numbers.  Now it will assemble near or just east of Foley Square <a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?q=Foley+Square,+Centre+St+%26+Duane+St+New+York,+NY+10007+to+Zuccotti+Park+(Liberty+Plaza)&hl=en">(map)</a>.  The marchers will go down Park Row past police headquarters and City Hall to Broadway, where they will turn left and continue five blocks to Liberty Park (until recently called Zuccotti Park).</p>

<p>Any doubts about the importance being vested in this event are likely to be dismissed after even a quick look at the Google results for "Community/Labor March to Wall St against Corporate Greed and the Big Banks".</p>
