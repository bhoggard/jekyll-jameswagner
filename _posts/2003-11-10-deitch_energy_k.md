Deitch on Wooster Saturday night - hardly a grownup to be seen</p>

<p>Deitch still pulls them in - both artists and their young fans - meaning a hugely-diminished SOHO cannot be ignored quite yet.</p>

<p>There were simultaneous openings Saturday on both Grand and Wooster Streets, and it was good to be there(s).</p>

<p>Highlights:  <a href="http://www.lflgallery.com/tim2.html">Tim Lokiec</a>, <a href="http://www.newyorkmetro.com/arts/cue/art/">Naomi Fisher</a> and <a href="http://snitzer.com/artistrepresented/bas.html">Hernan Bas</a> in the smaller venue, and Hisham Akira Bharoocha, <a href="http://www.pbs.org/art21/artists/hawkinson/">Tim Hawkinson</a> and <a href="http://www.ratio3.org/the_avatars_garrett1.html">Christopher Garrett</a> in the great barn on Wooster, where the attractive crowd was a distraction and a return visit should reveal some more (highlights).</p>

<p>Tim Lokiec's drawings were exceptionally beautiful, even (or especially) if you've already seen him at <a href="http://www.lflgallery.com/tim.html">LFL</a>.  Loved Tim Hawkinson's truck tire.</p>

<p>There is a <a href="http://www.ratio3.org/we_are_electric.html">site</a> for the curator of the Wooster St. show, Chris Perez, but there's no Deitch website!  Especially shocking in the circumstances of their moneyed hipsterdom.  Logistics:  76 Grand St. and 18 Wooster Street  (212) 343 7300</p>
