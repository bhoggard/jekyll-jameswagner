<p>Most thinking folks know there really is no independent mainstream press left in the U.S.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.commondreams.org/views03/0312-06.htm">Molly Ivins</a> is more generous, but even she's paid by the commercial media.  While she thinks it could still profit from some soul-searching to see why it has completely failed in its role, her own outline of the extent of that failure is a clear indictment of its alliance with the establishment.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>According to a poll conducted by The New York Times and CBS, 42 percent of Americans believe Saddam Hussein was personally responsible for the attacks on the World Trade Center, something that has never even been claimed by the Bush administration. According to a poll conducted by ABC, 55 percent believes Saddam Hussein gives direct support to al-Qaida, a claim that has been made by the administration but for which no evidence has ever been presented. President Bush has lately modified the claim to "al-Qaida-type" organizations. This is how well journalism has done its job in the months leading up to this war. A disgraceful performance.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>These beliefs are not found in any numbers anywhere else in the world.  Either Americans are uniquely stupid or we're getting the wrong information.</p>

<p>And then there is Matt Taibbi in the <a href="http://www.nypress.com/print.cfm?content_id=7902"><u>New</u> <u>York</u> <u>Press</u></a>.  Taibbi uses the story of Bush's recent staged showing in the East Room to show how the White House press corps "politely grabs its ankles" in Dubya's awesome presence.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The Bush press conference to me was like a mini-Alamo for American journalism, a final announcement that the press no longer performs anything akin to a real function. Particularly revolting was the spectacle of the cream of the national press corps submitting politely to the indignity of obviously pre-approved questions, with Bush not even bothering to conceal that the affair was scripted.</p>
