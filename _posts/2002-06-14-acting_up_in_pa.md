<p>It took some time before the <u>Gay</u> <u>City</u> <u>News</u> <a href="http://www.lgny.com/GCN3/PalestineNews.html">interview</a> with Steve Quester, who joined the International Solidarity Movement in Palestine this past April, was put on line, so I have been unable to send this until now.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>While the internationals are pledged to take only non-violent actions they do support the right of the Palestinians to use force.</p>

<p>"If somebody invades your town you have the right to shoot at them," Quester said. "No one element alone –– armed struggle, diplomacy, non-violent resistance –– is going to free the Palestinian people."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Steve, who is a dear friend, had written the following note at the time the article first appeared, about a week ago, and it should be included here, together with the Frederick Douglas quote he appended:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The issue of Gay City News that came out today<br />
includes a profile of me on the front page.  It's long<br />
on me and short on Palestinians, despite my best<br />
efforts.  It also completely ignores the role of women<br />
activists--see my response, below.</p>

<p>--Steve</p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>To the editors:</p>

<p>I was glad to have an opportunity to speak with Duncan<br />
Osborne about the dire situation faced by Palestinians<br />
today, and their heroic resistance to oppression<br />
("Zionism to Palestine, via ACT UP", Gay City News,<br />
June 7-20, 2002).  I was disappointed, though, that<br />
the (mostly male) editors of Gay City News chose not<br />
to include information from Mr. Osborne's interview<br />
with a lesbian who was in Palestine with me.  As I<br />
noted in my correspondence with Mr. Osborne, the<br />
movement for freedom and justice for Palestinians is<br />
led by women, many of them lesbians, in Palestine,<br />
Israel, and the U.S.  Men in the movement, like<br />
myself, are too often forefronted to the exclusion of<br />
the women doing the work.</p>

<p>I hope Gay City News will do features in the future on<br />
queer women and the struggle for Palestine.</p>

<p>Steve Quester<br />
Jews Against the Occupation (JATO),<br />
Palestine Activist Forum of New York (PAFNY),<br />
International Solidarity Movement (ISM)</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>

</blockquote>
