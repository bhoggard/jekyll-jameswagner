<p>Sometimes it's refreshing to know that the world is not sitting in the same living room.  In Kenya, some of the Masai needed to <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/06/03/international/africa/03KENY.html">hear about </a>September 11 in a way that that was most human to them.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Most Masai had learned of the attacks from the radio soon after they occurred. But the horrible television images passed by many Masai, who got electricity in their village only shortly before the attacks. In the oral tradition they rely on, Mr. Naiyomah sat them down and told them stories that stunned them.</p>

<p>Through his tales, Sept. 11 became real. The Masai felt sadness. They felt relief that Mr. Naiyomah was unscathed. They wanted to do something.</p>
</blockquote>
