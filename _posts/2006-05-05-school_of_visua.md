<p>We visited the two floors of the <a href="http://www.schoolofvisualarts.edu/index.jsp">School of Visual Arts</a> MFA open studios event last weekend where Sarah Schich was projecting a small moving image onto one wall of her darkened studio.  The walls were all shingled with seriously-blank sheets of white typewriter paper.  Across from the "screen" and also just above the floor, lit by one dim bare bulb hanging from the ceiling, was a typed list of names pinned to the wall.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Sylvia Plath</p>

<p>Edgar Allen Poe</p>

<p>Anne Sexton</p>

<p>Michel Foucault</p>

<p>Edna St. Vincent Millay</p>

<p>Yukio Mishima</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The little lantern relayed a looped series of six short animated videos illustrating the story of the deaths of six famous writers.</p>

<p>The image above is a still from the story, by the author's own account, of the social circumstances in which Michel Foucault contracted the disease, HIV, which eventually caused his death.</p>

<p>I know this may sound weird and probably disrespectful, but the animations are graceful, beautiful, and absolutely charming.</p>
