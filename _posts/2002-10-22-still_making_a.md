<p>These are our children!</p>

<p>Activists demonstrated <i>inside</i> and outside of the UN General Assembly yesterday, where they shouted, "No war in Iraq!"</p>

<p>These good folks, knowingly or not, were following in the footsteps and the soundbites of generations of worthy progressive protestors and <a href="http://www.actupny.org/diva/news-transcript.html">innovative interlopers</a>, most notably and most recently those spawned by AIDS activism.</p>

<p>Until they close down the world altogether, activists will always find a way to speak.</p>

<p>But I suppose the <u>NYTimes</u> will also always find ways to <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/10/22/international/middleeast/22PROT.html">distort</a> the news and the message.  What's with their gratuitious statement, "The protestors were apparently not armed or carrying any unusual items."?</p>

<p>One more humble question, this one for the City of New York:  Why were six people arrested <i>outside</i> on the street, apparently only for speaking?</p>
