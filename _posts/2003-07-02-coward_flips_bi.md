<p><img src="/images/Bush roughrider.jpg" border="0" alt="" /></p>

<p>Our daring commander-in-chief, who will always be the most protected individual on the planet, courageously <a href="http://www.reuters.com/newsArticle.jhtml;jsessionid=MI3UZFTBC3DFWCRBAEZSFFA?type=topNews&storyID=3029607">flipped the bird</a> at Iraqi militants who might be thinking of threatening our already-beleagured troops in the Middle East.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>"There are some who feel like that conditions are such that they can attack us there," Bush told reporters at the White House. "My answer is: Bring them on. We have the force necessary to deal with the situation."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>He's daring a people already on the brink of major insurgency to just try to kill and maim Americans.  Do "our boys and girls" need this kind of support from an idiot who has already said he placed them in great peril in the first place because his friend <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200307#940">god</a> told him to?</p>

<p>Even in the initial story about Bush's remarks, Reuters has to report that they have outraged Washington - well, at least the Democrats.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>"I am shaking my head in disbelief. When I served in the army in Europe during World War II, I never heard any military commander -- let alone the commander in chief -- invite enemies to attack U.S. troops," said New Jersey Democratic Sen. Frank Lautenberg.</p>

<p>Rep. Richard Gephardt of Missouri, a Democratic presidential candidate, said: "I have a message for the president: 'Enough of the phony, macho rhetoric. We should be focused on a long-term security plan that reduces the danger to our military personnel.'"</p>
</blockquote>
