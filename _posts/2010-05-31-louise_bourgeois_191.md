Louise Bourgeois <em>I do</em> 2010 archival dyes on cloth with embroidery</p>

<p><a href="http://www.cheimread.com/artists/louise-bourgeois/">Louise Bourgeois</a> died this morning.  I had hoped, and fully expected to see this magnificent, (can I say <em>ballsy</em>?) artist, and extraordinary human being continue well into her second century, but although she almost made it, it was not to be.</p>

<p>Her <a href="http://www.pbs.org/art21/artists/bourgeois/index.html">art</a> is likely to go on forever however;  her <a href="http://www.wsws.org/articles/2009/jan2009/bour-j14.shtml">legend</a> had already begun years ago.</p>

<p>"<a href="http://eyeteeth.blogspot.com/2010/05/i-do-louise-bourgeois-edition-for.html">I do</a>", an edition of an image of two flowers joined on a single stem designed for the <a href="http://www.freedomtomarry.org/pages/about-us">Freedom to Marry</a> campaign, was to be one of her last activist contributions to the world blessed by her presence.</p>

<p>I think I first became aware of her generosity and her personal activism in the early 90's when she agreed to contribute to the <a href="http://www.actupny.org/merchandise/index.html">ACT UP Art Box</a> (those balls again).</p>

<p><a href="http://arttattler.com/archivebourgeois.html">This site</a> includes some wonderful images of both the artist and the artist's work.</p>
