<p>This news arrived in our home only today, from <a href="http://members.aol.com/wockner/rexwockner.html">Rex Wockner</a>'s list, and for that I find great fault with the queer media.  Unless I have missed a lot, there have been no reports here of the death of <a href="http://hallmemoirs.com/gay_lesbian/144.shtml">Charlotte von Mahlsdorf </a>this past April.</p>

<p>Charlotte von Mahlsdorf lived, entirely openly and openly fully, as a cross-dresser under the twentieth century’s two most repressive regimes, the Nazis and the Communists.  She was, in her own words, "<a href="http://www.gruenderzeitmuseum.de/new/htm/charl.htm">my own woman</a>."</p>

<p>Wockner's notice:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>FAMED GERMAN TRANSVESTITE DIES</p>

<p>Germany's most famous transgendered person, Charlotte<br />
von Mahlsdorf (Lothar Berfelde), died in Berlin April<br />
30 of a heart attack. She was 74.</p>

<p>Von Mahlsdorf was awarded the Federal Cross of Honor<br />
(Bundesverdienstkreuz), the nation's highest civilian<br />
honor, in 1992 for founding East Berlin's <a href="http://www.gruenderzeitmuseum.de/new/">Grunderzeit<br />
Museum</a> which preserves furniture and household<br />
appliances from the period 1870-1900.</p>

<p>"I am not at all keen on medals," she said at the<br />
time. "But what I find even more important is that a<br />
homosexual, a transvestite, is honored in this way.<br />
.. I hope this encourages other gays and lesbians and<br />
demonstrates to heterosexuals that we too can achieve<br />
things."</p>

<p>Von Mahlsdorf emigrated to Sweden in 1997 after the<br />
museum was attacked by anti-gay hoodlums. She was on a<br />
visit to Berlin when she died.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>She was both subject and actress in Rosa von Praunheim's  1992 film, "<a href="http://www.rosavonpraunheim.de/werk/film/92frau/meine_eigene_frau_1.html"><i>Ich Bin Meine Eigene Frau</i></a> [I am my own woman]."</p>
