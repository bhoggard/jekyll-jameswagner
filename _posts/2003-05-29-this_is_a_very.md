<p>One of today's top stories on <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=578&ncid=578&e=3&u=/nm/20030529/ts_nm/bush_taxes_report_dc">Reuters</a> shows us that all is going according to plan (see the post <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200305#891">below</a>):</p>

<blockquote>
<p>WASHINGTON (Reuters) - The White House on Thursday denied suppressing a report that projects the U.S. government faces a long-term budget deficit of more than $44 trillion.</p>

<p>White House Budget Director Mitch Daniels said the allegation was "probably the most absurd thing that I can imagine."</p>

<p><i>However, he said the looming costs of Social Security and Medicare, which make up most of the forecast gap between government income and spending, were an important issue.</i>  [my italics]</p>

<p>"This is a very legitimate point," he said.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The news wire article is largely about the administration's deliberate deceit of Congress and the nation, but when we read Daniels' statement we end up tripping over the larger agenda - the destruction of all social programs other than those which subsidize the super rich.</p>

<p>While the sober American Enterprise Institute <a href="http://specials.ft.com/spdocs/WP-Fiscal-Smetters.pdf">study</a> (commissioned by Bush's then-Treasury Secretary Paul O'Neill) which is the subject of the story describes a budget imbalance of $44.2 trillion, which according to Reuters is "astronomical even by the standards of U.S. federal government accounting," the article ends with this shocking reference to its numerical scale:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>For this fiscal year, the government's cash shortfall is widely expected to be more than $300 billion while accumulated debt from previous budget deficits stands at around $6.4 trillion.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Remember, there was a big Congressional <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/05/29/politics/29CHIL.html">celebration</a>[see photo] in the White House yesterday when the tax cut was signed.  We know we can't expect social responsibility from Republicans or now even from Democrats, but fiscal responsibility was the only thing of which Republicans could legitimately boast for most of the party's history.  That of course was before the club became just a hangout for greedy thugs.</p>
