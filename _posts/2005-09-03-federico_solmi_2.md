<p><a href="/2005/03/federico_solmi.html">Federico Solmi</a> is in the midst of assembling a stack of a thousand drawings for his newest project, an ambitious hand-colored videoanimation based on the 1933 "<a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/B000AY3KN0/qid=1125790252/sr=8-1/ref=pd_bbs_1/103-3081311-2572635?v=glance&s=dvd&n=507846">King Kong</a>" movie, sort of updated for the twenty-first century, but filled with the kind of anachronisms which fire the imaginations of film buffs everywhere.</p>

<p>In addition to the cast of characters familiar to several generations the world over, the new film will feature the Statue of Libery, McDonald's golden arches, Charles Lindberg and the Spirit of St. Louis, Gucci, Prada, the Guggenheim and the Gagosian gallery on 24th Street.  Solmi and his beautiful wife Jennifer have the staring roles, but this time Solmi's idol <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0797382/">Rocco Sifreddi</a> will be confined to a billboard in Times Square.</p>

<p>We had a peek at some of the gorgeous drawings and a lot of the background magic inside his studio on Thursday, but Solmi will first be showing the film itself in a gallery in Cologne and a museum in Naples this fall.  He has also been invited to Miami in early December by a curator with the <a href="http://www.artinfo.com/NEWS/pulse_2005-6-30.htm">Pulse Art Fair</a>, and Barry and I will be able to see the finished work there.</p>

<p>I can't imagine it won't be shown somewhere in New York this season as well, especially since Solmi promises to add larger-scale drawings, and sculptures reproducing some of its most memorable characters.  Ask your local gallerists about their schedules.</p>
