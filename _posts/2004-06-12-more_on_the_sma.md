<p>A number of the workspaces tempted us during our visit to Smack Mellon's <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/curstud.html">Open Studios</a> Thursday evening, in adition to that of <a href="https://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/0041Smack Mellon's Open Studios Th58.html">Valerie Hegarty</a>.  We were especially intrigued by the work of <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/studioart/kim.html">Shin il Kim</a>, <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/studioart/loefke.html">Andrea Loefke</a> and <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/studioart/thomas.html">Austin Thomas</a>.</p>

<p>We'd seen the amazing videos of Kim at Axel Raben's <a href="http://www.axelraben.com/paperChase/pr/paperChase2.htm">Paper Chase</a> show last April, and Austin Thomas's constructing presence is difficult to avoid anywhere in New York these days.  (But, damn, I still haven't seen the neat folding outfit she created for that fantastic Ford <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/001368.html">El Camino</a> of hers!).  Finally, while I think this was the first time I'd seen any of the elements of Loefke's delightful, exploding world, I'm sure it won't be the last.</p>
