<p>Abbé Pierre died yesterday.  If there is such a thing as a "saint", <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2007/01/23/world/europe/23pierre.html?_r=1&oref=slogin">this man</a> clearly deserved the title, but he will never be canonized by the Church.  Too excessive.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>He wrote that as a young priest, he had sex with a woman, and further infuriated Roman Catholic authorities by advocating gay marriage.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Even Jeanne d'Arc, who finally made saint after waiting 600 years, had never talked that kind of nonsense.</p>

<p>This and (almost) everything else in this remarkable <u>NYTimes</u> obituary of a 20th-century St. Francis almost reads like pure invention;  it describes the perfect French or even <em>universal</em> hero of the poor, the homeless, the disenfranchised.  And there's recycling in there too!</p>

<p><strong>ADDENDUM:</strong>  In doing a little searching I've just discovered that l'Abbé was a good friend of <a href="http://www.reference.com/browse/wiki/Jacques_Gaillot">this</a> dangerous man.</p>
