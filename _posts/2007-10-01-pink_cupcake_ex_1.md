<p>The <a href="/2007/04/cupcake_preserv.html">pink cupcake</a> is dark tonight.   Dead.   And long may it rest, in oblivion.</p>

<p>I was immediately sorry for what the closing of Burgers & Cupcakes might mean to its employees, but minutes after I heard the news from Barry that the restaurant had closed (he had seen the sign on the window this evening) I told him that I actually felt sorry for the owners.</p>

<p>Then he reminded me that one of them had referred to that paragon of neighborhood  <a href="http://www.calientecab.com/future.html">aesthetics</a> and <a href="http://www.thevillager.com/villager_218/eateryisshamedby.html">sensitivity</a>, the Sixth Avenue branch of the huge Caliente Cab Company chain, as a model for what they described as (if not in the same word) their own <em>landmark</em> sign, a giant spotlighted, revolving cupcake poised on top of an illegal canopy above the curb.   Now I can feel totally comfortable saying that anyone who could argue that the archtypical Village monstrosity was an appropriate model for a new, improved 23rd Street totally deserves to go out of business.</p>

<p>We had a celebratory toast before a late supper tonight.</p>

<p>With <a href="/2007/08/chris_quinn_can.html">no thanks</a> to our City Council representative.</p>

<p><strong>UPDATES:</strong>  Today the giant cupcake itself is nowhere to be seen, although the illegal canopy on which it once rotated remains in place.  [reporting from the scene on Tuesday, October 2]  Now [later this afternoon] we've heard that someone had paid $400 to take it away.</p>
