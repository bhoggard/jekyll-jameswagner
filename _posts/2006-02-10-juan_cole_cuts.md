<p><a href="http://www.juancole.com/2006/02/cheney-authorized-libby-to-disclose.html">Juan Cole</a> cuts through all the mendacity this morning:  He describes, in the clearest possible fashion [and richly illustrated] the crimes of Bush, Cheney, Libby and Rove as they relate to the Valerie Plame story.</p>

<p>Cole's good, but it's really all over now, except for some of the shouting.  Even after the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2006/02/10/politics/10leak.html?hp&ex=1139634000&en=459da82bafdce84e&ei=5094&partner=homepage">revelations</a> in today's news, I see his account primarily as a lesson in why it was so important to get Alito appointed.</p>

<p>Listen to the administration's assertions of the legitimacy of their own spying ever since the wiretapping story broke.  They <em>know</em> that everything they have done and will yet do <em>really is</em> perfectly legal and constitutional, because the Supreme Court, as Bush has now constituted it, will end up ruling so.</p>

<p>And don't think that this crew will ever have to surrender their power.  Everything has finally been put in place for the perpetuation of a one-party state.</p>

<p>And we can't do a thing about it.</p>

<p>Those who have refused to play along have to be asking how much time they will have to get out of the country.</p>
