<p>Are we dizzy yet?  Some people were just beginning to sort out the old categories, but <a href="http://www.salon.com/ent/feature/2002/07/22/metrosexual/index.html">new species </a>seem to be popping out all over, contributing to a delightful confusion for those who welcome life, and a nightmare for the half-dead.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>MEET THE METROSEXUAL<br />
He's well dressed, narcissistic and bun-obsessed. But don't call him gay.</p>

<p>....</p>

<p>For some time now, old-fashioned (re)productive, repressed, unmoisturized heterosexuality has been given the pink slip by consumer capitalism. The stoic, self-denying, modest straight male didn't shop enough (his role was to earn money for his wife to spend), and so he had to be replaced by a new kind of man, one less certain of his identity and much more interested in his image -- that's to say, one who was much more interested in being looked at (because that's the only way you can be certain you actually exist). A man, in other words, who is an advertiser's walking wet dream.</p>
</blockquote>

<p><a href="http://www.marksimpson.com/">Mark Simpson</a>'s essay concludes with a caution however.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The final irony of male metrosexuality is that, given all its obsession with attractiveness, vanity for vanity's sake turns out to be not very sexy after all.</p>

<p>But then, it's much too late for second thoughts. Metrosexuality is heading out of the closet, and learning to love itself. Even more.</p>
</blockquote>
