<p>I spotted this garden planted just outside the long frontage of the Museum of Modern Art on West 53rd Street today.  I was on my way to the <a href="http://www.folkartmuseum.org/">American Folk Art Museum</a> located next door.</p>

<p><img alt="garbageflowers1.jpg" src="/mt_archives/garbageflowers1.jpg" width="600" height="450" /></p>

<p><img alt="garbageflowers3.jpg" src="/mt_archives/garbageflowers3.jpg" width="600" height="450" /></p>

<p><img alt="garbageflowers2.jpg" src="/mt_archives/garbageflowers2.jpg" width="600" height="450" /></p>

<p>Each "flower" bore one green leaf attached to its shiny metal stem.  The individual pieces had been signed and numbered.</p>

<p><img alt="garbageflowerslabel.jpg" src="/mt_archives/garbageflowerslabel.jpg" width="500" height="375" /></p>

<p>On the reverse of each leaf were the words, "<strong>Original - <a href="http://www.garbageflowers.com/">Garbage Flowers</a> - Genuine</strong>," arranged in an oval gently suggesting a logo.</p>

<p>Oh yes, when I passed the site again two hours later I was astounded to find that no one had removed a single blossom, and none had wilted, not one wit.</p>
