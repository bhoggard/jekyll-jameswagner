<p><em>institutional critiquing</em></p>

<p>Artist qi peng's blog on the Salt Lake City Fine Arts Examiner <a href="http://www.examiner.com/x-3879-Salt-Lake-City-Fine-Arts-Examiner">website</a> includes a number of conversations (which he characterizes, on his <a href="http://www.qipeng.net/qi%20peng%20art%20studio/identity.html">own</a> site as part of his conceptual art, that is, "'interviews' executed as a form of collaborative portraits") with artists and art mavens who might be located just about anywhere.</p>

<p>I agree with <a href="http://edwardwinkleman.blogspot.com/2009/03/getting-ready-for-my-arts-blogosphere.html">Ed Winkleman</a>'s assessment:  While the interviews are just one of the reasons to go there, you'll find some very good talk, notably an astounding exchange with artist <a href="http://www.examiner.com/x-3879-Salt-Lake-City-Fine-Arts-Examiner~y2009m3d16-EXCLUSIVE-ASSASSINATION-William-Powhida-Artist-Represented-by-Schroeder-Romero-Gallery">William Powhida</a> [hot photo, William], who is guaranteed good company every time.  But just before that, you'll find qi's "portrait" of the great and generous <a href="http://www.examiner.com/x-3879-Salt-Lake-City-Fine-Arts-Examiner~y2009m3d15-EXCLUSIVE-ASSASSINATION-Edward-Winkleman-Director-of-Winkleman-Gallery">Winkleman</a> himself.</p>

<p>Peng's interview with <a href="http://www.examiner.com/x-3879-Salt-Lake-City-Fine-Arts-Examiner~y2009m3d24-ASSASSINATION-James-Wagner-Art-Critic-Collector-and-Editor-of-ArtCat">this unworthy fanatic</a> showed up on his site two days ago.</p>
