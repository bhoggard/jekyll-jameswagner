<p><strong>ADDENDUM</strong> [the first five paragraphs below]<strong>:</strong></p>

<p>The patronizing West, and the U.S. in particular, has always backed dictators in Arab countries because it sees such regimes as the only alternative to fundamentalism, and yet over and over again that policy has produced the fundamentalist regimes it fears most.</p>

<p>We're now seeing that there is a third possibility, and we all better start supporting it before its too late everywhere.</p>

<p>By the way, I've just learned (from the Egyptian paper, Al-Masry Al-Youm) that The Pentagon is <a href="http://www.almasryalyoum.com/en/news/pentagon-monitors-events-egypt-advance-bilateral-defense-talks">hosting</a> senior Egyptian military leaders for annual bilateral defense talks this week, and that Pentagon Press Secretary Geoff Morrell, commenting on the talks, said: "That's just one example of how engaged we are with the Egyptians, even as these developments have taken place on the streets of Cairo and elsewhere."</p>

<p>No, I'm not making this up.</p>

<p>The same article describes the financial history of our relationship with the Mubarek regime:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Since its 1979 peace deal with Israel, Egypt has become the biggest recipient of US military aid after Tel Aviv, receiving nearly $36 billion in military assistance in annual installments of $1.3 billion.</p>
</blockquote>
