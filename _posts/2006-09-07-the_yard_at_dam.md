Susan C. Dessel <em>OUR BACKYARD:  A Cautionary Tale</em> 2006 sandbag tarps, EMS blankets, shellac, plaster gauze, cement, dimensions variable [view of installation]</p>

<p>The first part of the first real-time show either Barry or I have curated opens this Friday at <a href="http://www.damstuhltrager.com/">Dam, Stuhltrager</a> in Williamsburg.  The opening reception for Susan C. Dessel's <em>sculpture yard</em> installation, "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/2969">OUR BACKYARD:   A Cautionary Tale</a>", is the evening of September 8, from 7 to 9.   The address is 38 Marcy Avenue, just west of the BQE on the corner of Hope.</p>

<p>The inside galleries will be showing the work of five Spanish artists, Yolanda del Amo, Ruben Ramos Balsa, Rafael de Diego, Esther Manas and Javier Viver, in "Echo", <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/3072">an installation</a>  curated by Sara Abad & Elena Blanque also opening the same night.</p>

<p>Yes, of course we'll be there.</p>

<p>Check <a href="http://www.artcal.net/">ArtCal</a> for the remaining 110+ openings we care about in the next few days, and see <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/005774.html">Bloggy</a> for some suggestions for simplifying your cultural mapping for tomorrow, Thursday, by any account New York's biggest night of the year for new art.</p>

<p>The second part of our own show opens inside at Dam, Stuhltrager five weeks from now, on October 13.  More on that adventure in this space during the weeks to come.</p>
