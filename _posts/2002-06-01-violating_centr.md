<p>This one was for Rex!</p>

<p>Before he [under huge protest] succumbed to H.I.V. disease a few years ago, Rex Wasserman was a wonderful friend, a fanatical New Yorker, a fierce activist, and a City landscape architect with little love for Henry Stern's self-promoting theatrics.  When I saw an <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/2002-05-28/News_and_Views/Opinion/a-152302.asp">item by Stern </a>on the Opinion page of the Daily News this week, I thought of Rex and of the Park we all love so much, and I couldn't go on without adding a few words for myself and for my friend.</p>

<p>Today the News printed <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/2002-06-01/News_and_Views/Opinion/a-152768.asp">my letter</a>, unedited, in its own box at the bottom of the Letters page.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>[excerpt]  "Our natural shrine" has, in fact, been exploited repeatedly by installations intended to enhance reputations, movements and pocketbooks. For starters, I need only mention any number of rock or other music extravaganzas, at least one Disney film marketing event, rallies for religious figures and the most recent absurdity, a CBS "Survivor" episode.</p>
</blockquote>
