<blockquote>
<p><em>"Let the jury consider their verdict," the King said, for about the twentieth time that day.</p>

<p>"No, no!" said the Queen. "Sentence first -- verdict afterwards."</p>

<p>"Stuff and nonsense!" said Alice loudly. "The idea of having the sentence first!"</p>

<p>"Hold your tongue!" said the Queen, turning purple.</p>

<p>"I won't!" said Alice.</p>

<p>"Off with her head!" the Queen shouted at the top of her voice. Nobody moved.</p>

<p>"Who cares for you?' said Alice, (she had grown to her full size by this time.) "You're nothing but a pack of cards!"</em></p>
</blockquote>

<p>Surprise!  contrary to what most of us may have assumed,  there is <a href="http://onthescene.msnbc.com/baghdad/2006/11/saddam_verdict_.html">no verdict</a> yet in the political show trial of Saddam Hussein even if the sentence has arrived, <em>fabulously</em>, just in time for the American voting audience.</p>
