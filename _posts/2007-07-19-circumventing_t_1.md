<p>The <a href="/2007/07/new_abstraction.html">other</a> reason of my excitement about D'Amelio Terras is the show in the larger space, "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/5047">Circumventing the City</a>", curated by Rachel Uffner, with work by <a href="/2004/03/the_next_chinat.html">Sarah Braman</a>, <a href="http://rivingtonarms.com/exhibitions/2007/To-Build-A-Fire.php?zoom=zoom1&page=PR">David Brooks</a>, <a href="http://www.galerie-obadia.com/artist_detail.php?ar=43">Jedediah Caesar</a>, <a href="/2006/04/nicole_cherubin_3.html">Nicole Cherubini</a>, <a href="/2005/12/valerie_hegarty_1.html">Valerie Hegarty</a>, <a href="http://www.metropicturesgallery.com/index.php?mode=artists&object_id=11">Yuri Masnyj</a>, <a href="/2005/06/the_crg_gallery_1.html">Ian</a> <a href="/2006/12/fourth_estate_a_1.html">Pedigo</a>, <a href="/2007/06/in_defense_of_a.html">Jacob Robichaux</a>, <a href="/2007/05/sterling_ruby_a_2.html">Sterling Ruby</a> and <a href="http://www.paris-art.com/lieu_detail-2333.html">Erika Vogt</a>.</p>

<p>Like "Heralds of Creative Anachronism", this is a show of abstraction, and everything in this room too was created within the last year or so, but four of the ten artists are <em>not men</em>, and their medium is not just paint on canvas.  In fact, there really isn't anything here which might be called straight painting at all.</p>

<p>It's a beautiful show.</p>

<p>I was already familiar with and enthusastic about the exciting work of Braman, Cherubini, Hegarty, Pedigo, Robichaux and Ruby, and now I'm also going to be watching for Brooks, Caesar and Masnyj.</p>
