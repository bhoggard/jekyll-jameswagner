<p>We really overstayed our invitation welcome to the three shows at the Chinatown gallery <a href="http://www.canadanewyork.com/">Canada</a> which closed a few days ago.  In fact, Barry and I had wandered into the space after they had officially closed the official run of work by <a href="/2004/05/will_a_tissue_m.html">Brian Belott</a>, Jocelyn Shipley and Frankie Martin.</p>

<p>On one side of the front gallery Belott showed a large renaissance-revival oak library table overflowing with his handmade books, each of their pages bursting with his infinitely-inventive collages.  Belott covers the surface of every page of these found volumes until they can no longer close, but must stand upright in sensual invitation.  We poured through dozens of them before we could tear ourselves away and let <a href="http://www.canadanewyork.com/sitewide/viewer.php?group=/artbin/wallacewhitney">Whit</a> (Canada's co-founder) leave for the night.</p>
