<p>There will be a great congregation of friends and activists inside the <a href="http://www.cooper.edu/administration/about/great_hall.html">Great Hall</a> at the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cooper_Union">Cooper Union</a> tomorrow evening at six o'clock.  There they will be <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2010/05/01/nyregion/01bigcity.html">celebrating</a> the rich life of <a href="/2010/04/harry_wieder.html">Harry Wieder</a>, cut short, shockingly, in an accident in April.</p>

<p>Harry was a familiar friend and powerful advocate of many progressive causes, so I expect the room will resemble a portrait of the face of New York grassroots activism (of almost every sort) as it operated over the last few decades.</p>

<p>I also expect that this memorial will not be a lugubrious affair.  Harry meant a lot to the people who shared his life and his dedication.  But we also knew how to share in laughter, and there should be plenty of that tomorrow.</p>

<p>Harry was also completely familiar with the historic Great Hall, not least for his regular attendance at <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Act_up">ACT UP</a> meetings, which continued while they were being held there in the early 90's.  It was a time, difficult to imagine today, when the press of hundreds of AIDS activists (I'm sure I remember hearing the number 700 one week), attracted by the urgency of the issues and the energy of the coalition, had forced a move from the pre-restoration <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NYC_LGBT_Community_Center">Center</a> to a larger venue.  It was Cooper which welcomed us.</p>

<p>I've been back many times since those years, and Barry and I will be there tomorrow.</p>

<p>Speaking of ACT UP, and the kind of energy which seems in scarce supply these days, the incredibly-important ACT UP Oral History Project has just added 14 new interviews with ACT UP activists and add 9 important video clips and transcripts to its <a href="http://actuporalhistory.org/">web site</a>.  Visit, rummage around, then go out and change the world.</p>

<p>While working on this post I once <a href="/2010/04/harry_wieder.html">again</a> found myself Googling for an image of "Harry Wieder";  there aren't a great number, and most of them are mixed in with a much, much larger number of images of "Prince Harry".   Our Harry would love that.</p>
