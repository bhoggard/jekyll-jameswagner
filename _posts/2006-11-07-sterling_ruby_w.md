<p>Yeah, maybe <a href="/2005/04/chris_martin_at.html">Chris Martin</a>, <a href="/2006/10/xylor_jane_at_c.html">Xylor Jane</a> and now <a href="/2005/01/sterling_ruby_1.html">Sterling Ruby</a>.  I don't know that they would show so well together in a curated physical space, but they definitely can and do share a brilliantly-appointed chamber inside my own head and heart.   Ruby's images, and his choice of medium, are both just perverse enough to make his inclusion as a part of this imaginary trio seem less like a natural, but like that of the other two, his work strikes me as effortlessly grownup in a way even Peter Pan would be comfortable with.</p>

<p>Anyway.</p>

<p>No, Ruby does not currently have a show at <a href="http://www.foxyproduction.com/home">Foxy Production</a>;  I just happened to spot this exciting piece (brilliant gold paper slashed with crimson nail polish, mounted behind plexiglas for bonus shadow) the other day when I ventured behind the magic "curtain" which separates four wonderful <a href="/2006/11/violet_hopkins.html">Violet Hopkins drawings</a> from the gallery desk area.</p>
