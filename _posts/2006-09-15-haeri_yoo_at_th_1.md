Haeri Yoo <em>Untitled</em> 2006 acrylic and gouache on canvas 12" x 12" [installation view]</p>

<p>While I was in <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/3008">Thomas Erben</a>'s gallery yesterday I couldn't help thinking about the fact that I was looking at the work of one <a href="/2006/09/dona_nelson_at.html">established</a> artist, one just beginning to be recognized and sought, and a third who has hardly been seen at all.  Only while I'm writing this now do I also notice that each of these artists is a woman.</p>

<p>The artist who is becoming increasingly visible is <a href="http://www.thomaserben.com/artists/group_show_2006_1/Chitra%20Ganesh/CGanesh.php">Chitra Ganesh,</a> and she deserves even more than all the goodies that have been said about her.  The underknown artist is <a href="http://afonline.artistsspace.org/view_artist.php?aid=2841">Haeri Yoo</a>, whose small drawings Barry and I first <a href="/2004/11/queens_internat.html">encountered</a> in the <a href="http://www.queensmuseum.org/exhibitions/qi2004.htm">Queens International</a> two years ago.</p>

<p>I had not seen anything of hers on canvas until yesterday, but if this very beautiful small figured "landscape" is representative of what she can do, I expect my earlier enthusiasm to me more than just renewed if she contiues to work in this medium.</p>
