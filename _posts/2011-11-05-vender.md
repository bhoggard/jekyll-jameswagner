<p><img alt="OWS_Ali_surrounded.jpg" src="/images/OWS_Ali_surrounded.jpg" width="600" height="399" class="mt-image-none" /><br />
occupiers, media people, and passersby, together with one unidentified blond-haired, spiffy guy in a pin-stripe suit (perhaps, although improbably, representing the NYPD), quickly surrounded police and firemen on Friday afternoon;  the combined city units were shutting down a food vendor on what appeared to be specious cause;  the vendor is known to the community and has been sympathetic to its needs</p>

<p>On Friday afternoon I visited the occupiers in Liberty Square.  I stayed for a few hours, talking to friends and strangers, and I came back with the images in <a href="http://jameswagner.smugmug.com/Journalism/Liberty-Plaza-Nov-4-2011/">this</a> annotated photo album.</p>

<p>Incidentally, on Friday night Occupy Wall Street tweeted:  "The man who changed Tunisia's history was a street vendor. Police seized his goods".  The tweet included <a href="http://articles.cnn.com/2011-01-16/world/tunisia.fruit.seller.bouazizi_1_tunisian-history-street-vendor-police-officer?_s=PM:WORLD#OWSStreetVendorProject">this link</a> for the story of the origin of the movement which is now being called "Occupy".</p>
