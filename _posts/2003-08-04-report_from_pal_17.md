Olives are almost as important to this story as they are to Palestinians generally.</p>

<p><a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200307#955">Steve</a>'s been mostly staying out of trouble the last few days:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Not a lot to report, and no photo this time.  I've<br />
been helping with organization here in Qalqilya while<br />
members of the Qalqilya action group have been<br />
traveling back and forth to Mas'ha.  It is the<br />
intention of the Israeli government and their<br />
contracted construction company there to trap one<br />
house between the Apartheid Wall and the fence<br />
surrounding the neighboring Israeli settlement.  The<br />
Palestinian people living in the house would be<br />
trapped in a no-man's land, and I'm told by a member<br />
of the International Women's Peace Service that the<br />
army is proposing to give them permission to leave<br />
their home 3 times a day.</p>

<p>Israeli, Palestinian, and international activists have<br />
been gathering there to prevent the continued<br />
construction of the fence and resulting isolation of<br />
the wall.  I expect to join them tomorrow.  Check out<br />
<a href="http://www.palsolidarity.org/">www.palsolidarity.org</a> for updates.</p>

<p>Yesterday we received 6 new activists in Qalqilya--one<br />
Dane (half Palestinian and fluent in Arabic), one<br />
Swede, one Brit, one Australian, and two Italians. <br />
They're a fantastic group, and are off and running<br />
with our farmers' project.  I will work on the<br />
farmers' project from Jayyous--perhaps we'll have a<br />
march from Jayyous to Qalqilya, across the lands that<br />
seem to be slated for Israeli expropriation.</p>

<p>I'm off to Jayyous now--will write next from there.</p>

<p>Peace,<br />
Steve</p>
</blockquote>
