<p>For years we've been horrified by what's been coming out of the Republican camp, but now we can take a laugh break.</p>

<p>I found the <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/SaraBenincasa">first</a> of these four videos through the New York magazine blog <a href="http://nymag.com/daily/intel/2008/09/sarah_palin_jokes_is_mccains_v.html">site</a> while searching Google for results on <a href="http://nymag.com/daily/intel/2008/09/were_sorry_but_palin_babydaddy.html?imw=Y">Levi Johnston/"sex on skates"</a> after reading Maureen Dowd's "<a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/03/opinion/03dowd.html?scp=2&sq=maureen%20dowd&st=cse">Too Much Life?</a>" [print edition title], in the <u>NYTimes</u> this morning.  I usually skip her rants, but today I found it somewhat compelling, not least because jumping out of the page was the phrase:</p>

<blockquote>
<p><em>wild soap opera storylines erupting from the Palin family and the Alaskan wilderness</em> [my italics]</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The videos are by <a href="http://www.sarabenincasa.com/">Sara Benincasa</a>.  The one I saw was incredibly cute (and I mean that in the best way), but most important it was a truly hysterical parody, and not the least bit mean.  Yes, I know the real story isn't this family, but rather McCain's misjudgment and his cynicism.</p>

<p>Now excuse me while I go off to look at the other three videos.</p>
