<p>The current exhibition at Jack the Pelican, "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/8673">Mr. Benn's Spare-time Continuum</a>" will close tomorrow, Sunday, at 6.   This means that there are only two days left for a chance to enter into the squirmy/cozy comforts of what is described here in the gallery press release:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The timely anachronism of Richard Wilson's mechanistic renderings of super-tech ideas points to Britain in an era <em>when unassuming people lived in modest circumstances</em> [my emphasis, since I love that phrase].</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Wilson's introduction of Mr. Benn to New York gallery goers might have been a risky proposition, since the popular early-1970's BBC show, based on a popular children's book published a few years earlier, never made it to our shores, but the installation resonates with our own island's 21st-century fantasies, sentimentalities and anxieties.</p>

<p>The exhibition may be one of the strangest scenes to be found in an area gallery right now, but I've found myself going back to it in my mind since I left it one week ago, no small thing for this crowded ADD head.</p>

<p>The mirrored ball may suggest the iconic 1964/1965 New York World's Fair <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unisphere">Unisphere</a>, and the transporter room painting appears to be a riff on Seurat's pointillist "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges-Pierre_Seurat">A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte</a>"  [surely what all "trekkies" were thinking each time that set showed up in Star Trek].</p>

<p>If you go, enjoy the soft, tufted Chesterfield sofa and take advantage of the hot water urn and tea makings.</p>
