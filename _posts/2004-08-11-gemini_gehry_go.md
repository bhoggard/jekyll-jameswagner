<p>While in Los Angeles last week we stopped at the West Hollywood workshop and galleries of <a href="http://www.absolutearts.com/artsnews/2001/11/05/29324.html">Gemini G.E.L.</a> (Graphic Editions Limited).  Ok, to be honest, the draw had been the building itself, whose interest had been touted by an artist friend of ours;  we didn't know about the galleries, and hadn't really expected to get inside.  In fact, we were really welcomed, and graciously invited to walk about the exhibition spaces on our own.</p>

<p>Gemini has collaborated with famous artists nationwide in a great portfolio of first-rate prints, "Artists Coming Together," to help <a href="http://actforvictory.org/">ACT</a> (America Coming Together) defeat Bush and elect Democrats in federal, state and local elections in November.  The prints are available, for those with pockets ample enough, individually or as a series.  <a href="http://actforvictory.org/act.php/art/index">Here</a>'s more information.</p>

<p>Back to the building.  It's less than two blocks from the <a href="/2004/08/schindlers_stud.html">Schindler House</a>, and shows curious references to that structure, which was erected more than half a century earlier.   The architecture is that of <a href="http://www.frank-gehry.com/">Frank Gehry</a>, but it's the really good Gehry, the one whose early modest originality excited an entire world now so eager for duplicates of <a href="http://www.frank-gehry.com/pics-2/index.htm">Bilbao</a> flamboyance.  These are a couple of almost abstract images of the Gemini shelter's 1976 shapes and textures:</p>
