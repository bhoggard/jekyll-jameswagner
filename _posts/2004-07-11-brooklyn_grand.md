<p>It's just a slip of a thing right now, but some day the <a href="http://www.nylcv.org/Programs/WPC/blueprint/boroughs/brooklyn/pages/2_williamsburg/#c">site</a> which once saw ferries, loaded with farm produce and passengers, crossing to Manhattan every few minutes from downtown Williamsburg may be a real destination once again.  Meanwhile the small <a href="http://www.wirednewyork.com/parks/grand_ferry_park/default.htm">park</a> is a modest delight for a neighborhood cut off from its great river and hungry for park</p>

<blockquote>
<p>A red brick smokestack rising above a circular pattern of cobblestones was part of a molasses plant that Pfizer Pharmaceuticals used in the early 20th century for work that led, eventually, to the large-scale production of penicillin. The cobblestones were salvaged from the section of Grand Street where the park was constructed.</p>
</blockquote>
