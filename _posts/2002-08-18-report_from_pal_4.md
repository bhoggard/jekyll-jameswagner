<p>[message from Steve in Palestine as relayed through Donald here in New York]</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Sunday 8/18.  I spoke with Steve at 9 AM New York Time (4PM Palestine <br />
Time)</p>

<p>Still not much action to report, but lots of travelling. Steve says <br />
his affinity group are all staying in the Balata refugee camp at <br />
Nablus, where many homes are slated for demolition by <br />
Israeli "security".  Steve was on the road there when I spoke with <br />
him.</p>

<p>Yesterday Steve went with his friend Said to the West Bank.  They <br />
visited the family of Said's cousin, who was murdered by Israeli <br />
soldiers.  They spent the night there, and had lots of opportunity to <br />
watch Israeli tanks drive around the streets of the village.</p>

<p>Today Steve got into Ramallah through a special route through a <br />
cement factory, by-passing roadblocks.  He met with one of the <br />
leading organizers of ISM, and they talked about publicizing and <br />
recruiting for an ISM action during the West Bank olive harvest:  <br />
Internationals are needed to help minimize shooting of farmers by <br />
settlers during the harvest.  The occupation and the settlers have <br />
devastated the Palestinian economy, and <br />
reserving/defending/supporting the olive harvest is crucial to <br />
Palestinians.</p>

<p>Steve was surprised at how bustling and animated Ramallah in contrast <br />
with the curfew times.</p>

<p>Travel News:  (this part is conversational notes)<br />
When I spoke with Steve he was in transit from Ramallah to Nablus.  <br />
He had passed through the first check point.  The check points are <br />
typically humiliating, requiring Palestinians to line up and wait <br />
huge amounts of time just to get from one part of their country to <br />
another.</p>

<p>After passing through the first check point, he got a cab.  The <br />
driver knew how to gain access to some settler roads, which meant <br />
they could circumvent road blocks.  It also meant some really <br />
circuitous paths taken to get on and off the settler road.  When I <br />
spoke to him he was on his way to the check point for getting into <br />
Nablus.</p>

<p>Steve comments that the countryside is very beautiful.  But it's also <br />
very strange to look out and see little Palestinian communities in <br />
some places and fortified settler communities in others.  The <br />
settlements are in violation of a section of the 4th Geneva <br />
convention, which forbids transfer of invading population to invaded <br />
territory.  Ironically, this section of the convention was instituted <br />
in response to the [historical German geopolitical concept, <i><a href="http://history1900s.about.com/library/holocaust/aa110899.htm">Lebensraum</a> </i>especially as modified and implemented by the Nazi regime.]</p>

<p>That's all for now.</p>

<p>dsg</p>
</blockquote>
