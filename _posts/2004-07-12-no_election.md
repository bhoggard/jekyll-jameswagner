. . . until, whatever</p>

<p>I wrote these three paragraphs as part of my ruminations on the eve of the last Congressional election, in 2002, prior to the monstrous Iraq War but just in time to see Hussein used as the bogey from which we needed the Republicans to defend us.  The <a href="/2002/10/rigging_the_ele.html">post</a> was titled, "rigging the election."</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Almost two years ago, in the months after the 2000 elections, I bored or frightened my friends with my prediction that we would <b>never have another Presidential election</b>, and we would very likely be relieved of the messiness of another congressional election as well. I believed that the Republicans would never give up what had been so ill-gotten in the winter of 2000-2001.</p>

<p>I was certain that some pretext would be invented to distort the electoral process, or even entirely suspend the Constitutional niceties providing for the election of a Congress and a President, in order to protect us from enemies at home or aboad.</p>

<p>If they get away with it this fall, a Republican executive, a Republican Congress and a Republican judiciary will virtually guarantee their success with a frightened and gung-ho citizenry in 2004. Dictatorship accomplished.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>If some were ever bored by cries about the sky falling, none of us are today, but we are all certainly frightened.</p>

<p>Going forward, I expect to append certain posts with the seven words, "We will never have another Presidential election."  I would be delighted to have to admit I was wrong, but it doesn't look like it's going to happen.</p>
