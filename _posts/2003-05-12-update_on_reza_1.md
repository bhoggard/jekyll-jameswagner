<p><a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200305#855">Reza Baluchi</a> has now left Los Angeles, and this time he's travelling <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/05/12/national/12BIKE.html">on foot</a> on his long journey to New York City.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>So this morning, Mr. Baluchi began the fulfillment of that jailhouse promise. Wearing new shorts, new running shoes and a bad haircut, he said he carried no hard feelings, no chips on his shoulder, only a knapsack filled with a tent and reflective vest, nylon leggings, a sleeping bag, food, water and an A.T.M. card.</p>

<p>"I go by I self," he said in self-taught English. "New York. Everybody wait me there. Soon. Soon. I come. Peace. No war. American people very good."</p>
</blockquote>
