<p>[This is a report of Donald's latest communication from Steve in Palestine, written this morning in New York.]</p>

<blockquote>
<p>In the last report, I mentioned that the army was occupying a large <br />
house in Nablus, with the original 20 occupants confined to a single <br />
apartment.  Outside this house were two Palestinian trucks that the <br />
army had impounded.  The army had taken one of the driver's ID <br />
papers.  Internationals were advocating for the trucks and the ID to <br />
be released.</p>

<p>I spoke with Steve this morning 8/20 around 7 am New York Time.  He <br />
was in the Askar refugee camp outside Nablus.</p>

<p>He had stayed for a long time with other members of JATO outside the <br />
abovementioned house, advocating for the truck drivers, while JATO <br />
members also brought food and medicine for the people in the house.  <br />
The area was under curfew, but the presence of so many internationals <br />
encouraged other neighbors to come out of their homes.  They brought <br />
food for the JATO folks.  This ticked off the army, and soldiers <br />
ordered all Palestinians out of the area, including a representative <br />
from the Union of Palestinian Medical Relief Committees.  The <br />
soldiers did not order the internationals out of the area.  Steve <br />
left at that time.</p>

<p>Over the time they stayed in front of the house, JATO people <br />
witnessed blindfolded prisoners brought to the house.  One of them <br />
was obviously beaten up.</p>

<p>Late at night, about 25 soldiers left the house in various armored <br />
vehicles, as Steve put it "to go out capturing and murdering".  When <br />
the soldiers came back around 3 am, they had something in writing <br />
identifying the area outside the house as a "military area" and told <br />
the internationals to beat it.</p>

<p>The truck driver whose ID was taken eventually got it back, as well <br />
as his truck.  The other truck driver still hasn't gotten his truck <br />
back.  This truck is filled with vegetables, which will rot, and <br />
Nablus is full of people who are hungry.</p>

<p>Steve was in Nablus today for a meeting, and heard gunfire as <br />
soldiers deployed in the Kasbah.</p>

<p>ISM is working to put together some strategy around dealing with <br />
occupied homes.  Right now everything they are doing at occupied <br />
homes is ad hoc.</p>

<p>Steve says he has heard about an apartment building in a nearby <br />
village where several apartments are occupied by the army, and the <br />
other residents of the building are locked into their apartments.  <br />
They have to pound on their own doors for up to two hours to get food <br />
or medicine (like in prison).</p>

<p>I forgot to ask Steve if occupying homes was common throughout the <br />
West Bank, or was a practice isolated to Nablus.</p>

<p>Steve says that it serves no purpose except to harass Palestinians <br />
and make life difficult for them.  Nablus is completely encircled by <br />
the army, and they could run their operations from their camps, but <br />
choose to take over homes because it is more intimidating and <br />
offensive.  Paraphrasing a remark made by Moshe Dayan regarding <br />
harassment of Palestinians 30 years ago "Let them live like dogs.  <br />
They can leave if they don't like it"</p>

<p>End of report<br />
dsg</p>
</blockquote>
