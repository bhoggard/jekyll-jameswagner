<p>No reason for putting this up this at this particular moment.  I just neglected to post this wonderful rant I found in <u>Newsday</u> when it first appeared, over ten days ago.  It's been in my head ever since, and I thought that finally letting it out here might let me move on [I don't like to have to think about Giuliani.  I really, really don't].</p>

<p>Jimmy Breslin [omigosh, someone I know was actually surprised to hear he was still around when I mentioned his name recently!] has no love for our former mayor, and he's not shy about writing about the man's bogus reputation as somehow divine, even when <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/news/opinion/columnists/ny-nybres073698923mar07,0,2364178,print.column?coll=ny-opinion-columnists">his column</a> is really about another cheap charlatan, George W. Bush.*</p>

<blockquote>
<p>He was a nobody as a mayor and in one day he became a hero. This sudden career, this door opening to a room of gold, all started for Rudolph Giuliani when his indestructible bunker in a World Trade Center building blew up. He had personally selected it, high in the sky, and with tons of diesel fuel to give emergency power.</p>

<p>And Guiliani walks on. He walks from his bunker, up Barclay Street and went on television. Went on and announced his heroism and then came back every hour or so until he became a star, a great figure, a national hero, the mayor who saved New York.</p>

<p>Most of this comes from these dazed Pekinese of the Press. Giuliani was a hero with these news people. He did not pick up a piece of steel or help carry one of the injured off.</p>

<p>He made the trade center his private cathedral. Police commanders were terrified of letting you in. There was only Rudy, who flew his stars, Oprah and the like, down to see it.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>*<br />
Breslin begins this March 7 column, with characteristic restraint, "In his first campaign commercial. George Bush reached down and molested the dead."</p>
