<p>Beautiful in its simplicity and its morality, and an argument against the blind anger and fear that surrounds us all, this <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/05/09/opinion/L09MIDD.html">letter</a> in the New York Times today should pull us all up short.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Some 30 years ago, a Hebrew University law faculty member wrote, "A border is secure when those living on the other side do not have sufficient motivation to infringe on it."</p>

<p>As another Hebrew University professor put it: After every victory, "the abyss of mutual hatred will deepen and the desires for vengeance will mount."</p>

<p>A few months after the 1967 war, Yeshayahu Leibowitz, a leading Israeli intellectual, said that the occupation was unjust and would lead to the subjugation of the Palestinians, and even to the corruption of Israeli society.</p>

<p>Ancient Israel gifted the world with the revolutionary idea that it is justice, not military might, that brings peace. Now is the hour for that wisdom to be reborn.</p>
</blockquote>
