<p>It seems appropriate, in the circumstances of the tragic story of a <a href="http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/local/chicago/chi-0211180202nov18.story">murder</a> in Chicago and the uproar it has caused among certain Catholic zealots, to be reminded of a ten-year-old proclamation which originated with the Vatican Sacred Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith (once known as the office of The Holy <a href="http://es.rice.edu/ES/humsoc/Galileo/Things/inquisition.html">Inquisition</a>).</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Most shocking of all, [a 1992 Vatican proclamation authorized by both Cardinal Joseph Ratzinger and by Pope John Paul II] suggests that when lesbians and gay men demand civil rights, "neither the Church nor society should be surprised when ... irrational and violent reactions increase".</p>

<p>This implies that by asking for human rights, lesbians and gay men<br />
encourage homophobic prejudice and violence: we bring hatred upon ourselves, and are responsible for our own suffering. The Catholic Church, it seems, blames the victims of homophobia, not the perpetrators.</p>

<p>---from the essay, "<a href="http://www.outrage.org.uk/catholic.htm">Catholic Homophobia</a>," by Peter Tatchell</p>
</blockquote>
