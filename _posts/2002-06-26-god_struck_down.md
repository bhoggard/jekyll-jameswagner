<p>Finally, <a href="http://www.reuters.com/news_article.jhtml?type=topnews&StoryID=1137297">one</a> for the handful of Americans who do not believe in invisible friends and who are not part of a cult!</p>

<blockquote>
<p>A federal appeals court found the U.S. Pledge of Allegiance unconstitutional on Wednesday....</p>

<p>"A profession that we are a nation 'under God' is identical ... to a profession that we are a nation 'under Jesus,' a nation 'under Vishnu,' a nation 'under Zeus,' or a nation 'under no god,"' it said.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The words, "under God," were first added in 1954 in the midst of our <i>last</i> big right-wing witch-hunting god-fearing jingoistic fake-war boom time, in a year dominated by Senator Joseph McCarthy.  I should be amazed it's still there, but I'm not, and I have no illusions the Supreme Court would agree with this Federal Appeals Court decision.</p>
