<p>Seven minutes in Cathedral Square, half an hour in the Hermitage, and an especially-miniaturized "Nutcracker," all in <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A18497-2002May27.html?referer=email">a day's work </a>for the emperor who already knows all he has to know.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>President Bush's father, when he was in the White House, introduced the world to "speed golf," involving a breathless race through 18 holes. The younger Bush, on his European trip, practiced a variation: robo-tourism.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>But don't suppose the subtleties of European culture escape him in his high-speed bubble:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Arriving for a caviar dinner at Russian President Vladimir Putin's country residence, Bush viewed the immaculate grounds and told his host: "Nice of you to mow the grass for us." At the French president's palace, he noted that Jacques Chirac is "always saying that the food here is fantastic."</p>
</blockquote>
