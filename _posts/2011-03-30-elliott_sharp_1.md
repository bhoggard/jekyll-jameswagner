<p>I'm not sure what we're in for tomorrow (April 1), but when I heard <a href="http://www.panix.com/~esharp/">Elliott Sharp</a> and <a href="http://www.thecodeinternational.com/fluffy">Steve Horowitz</a> were involved, I was already half there.</p>

<p>Over three days this weekend, beginning with a Sharp-curated evening of performance on Friday at 8 pm, the <a href="http://www.emfproductions.org/">Electronic Music Foundation (EMF)</a> is presenting what it calls "The Extended Piano Festival:  Works for the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disklavier">Disklavier</a>."   It looks like the music, in addition to its considerable performance art elements, may be accompanied by a certain amount of visual art throughout the Festival.</p>

<p>Some of the other names in the press release include, but are not limited to, Claudio Ambrosini, Dan Becker, Anthony Coleman, Nicolas Collins, Michael Evans, Fred Frith, Annie Gosfield, Seth Horvitz, Dafna Natali, Veniero Rizzardi, Frank Rothkamm, Carl Stone, and Hans Tammen.</p>

<p>Many of these have works being performed tomorrow (and/or will be performing it themselves), including Sharp, <a href="http://music.columbia.edu/~luke/">R. Luke DuBois</a>, <a href="http://www.jennylin.net/">Jenny Lin</a>, <a href="http://www.moderecords.com/profiles/stefanobassanese.html">Stefano Bassanese</a>, Viniero Rizzardi, <a href="http://www.miyamasaoka.com/">Miya Masaoka</a>, <a href="http://www.pamelaz.com/">Pamela Z</a>, <a href="http://www.nicolascollins.com/">Nicolas Collins</a>, and <a href="http://www.lukasligeti.com/">Lukas Ligeti</a>.</p>

<p>Steve Horowitz, has the space pretty much to himself (and the Disklavier) for a CD release performance the next night, Saturday.</p>

<p>Meanwhile, during daylight hours on Saturday and Sunday (April 2 and 3) people will be able to visit "a body of installed works for the Disklavier" curated by Sharp and Horowitz, composed or "performed" by <a href="http://www.composers21.com/compdocs/ambrosic.htm">Claudio Ambrosini</a>, <a href="http://www.danbecker.org/Dan_Becker-Composer/Welcome.html">Dan Becker</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Coleman">Anthony Coleman</a>, <a href="http://www.dallosto.com/">Diego Dall'Osto</a>, Carl De Pirro, <a href="http://www.fredfrith.com/">Fred Frith</a>, <a href="http://www.anniegosfield.com/">Annie Gosfield</a>, <a href="http://context.fm/blog/?page_id=6">Seth Horvitz</a>, <a href="http://dafnalula.blogspot.com/">Dafna Naphtali</a>, Veniero Rizzardi, <a href="http://rothkamm.com/">Frank Rothkamm</a>, <a href="http://www.sukothai.com/">Carl Stone</a>, <a href="http://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paolo_Troncon">Paulo Troncon</a> and <a href="http://tammen.org/">Hans Tammen</a>.</p>

<p>I have to thank a good number of the artists whose names I've mentioned above for finally pulling me to New York from Providence in the 1980s;  the other half may have been born after I arrived, and for that none of us can take any responsibility.  By 1985 I had already assembled a huge collection of recordings by the giants of what at that time I and others called New York "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Downtown_music">downtown music</a>," or "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise_music">noise music</a>."  There was also the designation, "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experimental_music">experimental music</a>," but none of these terms seemed to help much when it came time to approach a record store's bins.  When I moved to Manhattan I was shocked to often find myself sitting (sometimes standing) in live performances where there might be as few as ten other people in the audience - this when I might have half a dozen of that performer's records at home - and everyone but me seemed to be a musician and friend of the performer(s)!</p>

<p>All Festival performances and music installations are at <a href="/2004/05/thinking_outsid.html">White Box</a>, 329 Broome St.  Yes, the music is still pretty much <em>downtown</em>.</p>

<p>For more information, go to <a href="http://www.emfproductions.org/upcomingevents1011/extendedpiano.html">EMF</a> or <a href="http://www.whiteboxny.org/piano.html">White Box</a>.</p>
