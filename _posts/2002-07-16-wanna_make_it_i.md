<p>A savy and amusing <a href="http://www.inversetheater.com/ActorResources/NYCMove1.htm#hotel">navigation</a> of the perils in store here for those who just can't take it elsewhere any longer, and it's not just for <a href="http://www.dictionary.com/search?q=gypsies">gypsies</a>.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I can't help quoting from Kirk Wood Bromley's recent play about <a href="http://www.inversetheater.com/Productions/current.htm"><u>The</u> <u>American</u> <u>Revolution</u></a>:</p>

<p><b>Admiral Howe- </b>Tell me, Major Andre, of yon Manhattan, <br />
Where I expect to sleep tomorrow night. <br />
<b>Major Andre- </b>You will, sir, I'm afraid, get no sleep there. <br />
<b>Cornwallis- </b>For yon Manhattan is the noisiest, <br />
Filthiest, sleaziest, sauciest mess <br />
Of anti-civilized, counter-cultural, <br />
Money-grubbing yahoos ever festered <br />
Unflusht in the devil's antique outhouse. <br />
Madness and mayhem, sir, that is Manhattan.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Some things never change.  See, Manhattan does respect tradition!</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I know dozens of people who leapt at New York City, but couldn't get a toe-hold, and so fell back to where they came from. Thinking over these experiences, I've concluded that the three tricks to successfully living in New York are to find:</p>

<p>1. An apartment you can tolerate, <br />
2. A job you like,<br />
3. A community you love.</p>

<p>Focus first on these things, not on getting an acting job right away. There's no sense getting cast in a play if you don't have an apartment and a job. You'll be distracted and may even be forced to drop out of the show (a real career killing move, by the way) if you find you can't make it in the city.</p>
</blockquote>
