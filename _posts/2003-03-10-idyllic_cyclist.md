<p><a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200302#707">Reza K. Baluchi</a> is free at last.  The U.S. Immigration and Naturalization Service has <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/03/10/national/10BIKE.html">decided</a> not to appeal a judge's <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200302#714">decision</a> eleven days ago which officially granted him political asylum.  During the interim Baluchi has been kept in the Arizona jail which has been his cage since November.</p>

<p>Even the I.N.S. could not have been indifferent to the appeal of a pure soul and what must have been an enormous outpouring of support from those who had heard of his plight.</p>

<p>Our hero was to be picked up today by his lawyer, Suzannah Maclay.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Mr. Baluchi intends to pick up his bicycle and camping equipment from storage in Yuma, Ariz., and head for Los Angeles. When is ready, he said, he will run from there across the country, his peace mission resumed.</p>
</blockquote>
