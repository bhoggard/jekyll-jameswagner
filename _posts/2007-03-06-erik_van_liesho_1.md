<p>The Antwerp gallery <a href="http://www.stellalohausgallery.com/">Stella Lohaus</a>, appearing at the Armory for the first time, devoted their entire booth to the drawings of a single artist, <a href="http://www.stellalohausgallery.com/artist.asp?id=12">Erik van Lieshout</a>, who is increasingly known in Europe for <a href="http://www.friezeartfair.com/projects/2003/110817.shtm">his ambitious installations</a>.</p>

<p>It was an excellent move, for the work is very good, and being also very striking (not always a corollary) it managed to attract considerable attention even without the larger bells and whistles of which van Lieshout seems more than capable.  This shot of a part of one wall of the gallery's booth is necessarily a very inadequate representation of the artist's New York installation, and it's totally incapable of even suggesting this native Brabants artist's complexity.</p>
