<p>Don't let them keep getting away with it.  [The Democrat-Republican party oligarchy, that is.]  And we won't even be helping Pataki when we do it!</p>

<p>Barry says it all on <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/">Bloggy</a>:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I see no reason to vote for Carl McCall in this election. Pataki, whom I despise as much as the next person, appears ready to win in a landslide. He has been endorsed by all of the major newspapers in the state, and the latest polls show that McCall might even get less votes than Golisano.</p>

<p>So my advice: McCall's going to lose big anyway, so vote for the Green candidate, <a href="http://www.stanleyaronowitz.org/">Stanley Aronowitz</a>. You'll help keep the Green Party on the state ballot without them having to go through and expensive petition process.</p>

<p>Aronowitz is a great candidate. He was a steelworker and union organizer, and he is currently Distinguished Professor of Sociology and Urban Education at the CUNY Graduate Center.</p>

<p>Every endorsement of Pataki has talked about how <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/10/20/nyregion/20ALBA.html?ex=1035864000&en=a194cfe65e5b0eef&ei=5062&partner=GOOGLE">dysfunctional</a> Albany is, with most decisions made by just three people: Pataki, Sheldon Silver, and Joe Bruno. Why reward the two major parties by letting them continue to operate this way? 98% of state legislators are re-elected in each election.</p>

<p>I'm very, very serious about this. We have watched the Democrats collapse in front of the Republicans over civil rights, drug laws, health care, tax cuts for the wealthy, and war. When you have a chance to vote for a Green, particularly when doing so doesn't help a Republican, you must do it.</p>

<p>Good coverage of NY Politics, including the poll numbers, can be found on <a href="http://www.politicsny.com/">PoliticsNY.com</a>.</p>
</blockquote>
