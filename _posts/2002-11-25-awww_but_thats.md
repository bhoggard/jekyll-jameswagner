<p>He's done little enough to keep us out of the mess we're in now, since he voted for the new Department and for the office, but at least Chuck Schumer <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/11-25-2002/news/wn_report/story/38340p-36202c.html">knows</a> what kind of "big brother" he <i>doesn't</i> have in mind, even though it's too late now to do anything about it.</p>

<blockquote>
<p><b>CHOICE FOR 'BIG BROTHER' IS ALL WRONG, CHUCK SAYS</b></p>

<p>By KENNETH R. BAZINET<br />
DAILY NEWS WASHINGTON BUREAU</p>

<p>WASHINGTON - Sen. Chuck Schumer slammed Iran-Contra figure John Poindexter yesterday as the wrong man to head the Pentagon's new "big brother" program, saying the retired admiral barely escaped jail.<br />
"If we need a big brother, John Poindexter is the last guy on the list that I would choose," said Schumer (D-N.Y.).</p>

<p>He demanded that Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld replace Poindexter, who is overseeing a Pentagon anti-terrorism project, Total Information Awareness, charged with developing a vast database allowing unprecedented access to Americans' electronic transactions, from banking business to video rentals.</p>

<p>Poindexter, who was national security adviser under President Ronald Reagan, and Marine Lt. Col. Oliver North were convicted in 1990 of lying to Congress in the weapons-for-hostages scandal. The convictions were overturned on the grounds their right to a fair trial was violated.</p>

<p>The conviction "was overthrown on a technicality that had nothing to do with the facts of whether he lied to Congress," Schumer said yesterday on ABC's "This Week."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>He "demanded?"  Doesn't he realize who he's talking to?</p>
