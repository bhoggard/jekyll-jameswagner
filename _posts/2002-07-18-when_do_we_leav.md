<p>Unfortunately I can't locate the documentation for this at the moment, but a recent poll of Berliners found that fully 61 percent believed our dear Shrub is a dangerously incompetent maniac.  I don't know any people who might be better equipped to make such a judgment, and the scrappy Berliners would probably agree about <a href="http://www.lonelyplanet.com/destinations/europe/berlin/history.htm">their qualifications</a>.</p>

<p>If such an estimate matches my own, and it does, what shall I do with it as an American resident in America?</p>

<p>I saw this related <a href="http://www.kuro5hin.org/print/2002/7/16/14843/7227">observation</a> today on a discussion site, and it should disturb or provoke each of us:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I am a citizen of the United States. I see the <a href="http://commondreams.org/headlines01/1026-04.htm">PATRIOT</a> act, I see <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines02/0714-06.htm">TIPS</a>, and I think to the folks I grew up knowing as a kid, the German Jewish couple who "saw the signs and got out while they could", and remember stories of many others like them who saw where their country was going and had the foresight to get out before it was "patently obvious there was a problem"... which brings us to the modern day...</p>
</blockquote>

<p>But it's not just that I don't want to live under a government that is repressive, or worse.  I don't want to have to live with  people who have no problem with government moving in that direction (who will never go to the barricades, or even contact their people in Congress or, damn, at the very least, talk to each other about their apprehension), and that is what we are experiencing already.</p>

<p>How bad does it have to get before the alarm bells go off?</p>
