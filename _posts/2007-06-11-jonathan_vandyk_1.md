<p>Barry and I had to see what <a href="http://www.austinthomas.org/">Austin Thomas</a> was up to <a href="http://atfreespace.blogspot.com/">now</a>, so we headed out to further Williamsburg (the Morgan stop on the L) for <a href="http://www.jonathanvandyke.com/">Jonathan VanDyke</a>'s installation/homage to the abandoned hair salon which has/will become <a href="http://www.pocketutopia.com/">Pocket Utopia</a>.  This rather ephemeral set-up, "The Salon of the Covered Bride," was inspired at least in part by a press image of "the runaway bride," <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jennifer_Wilbanks">Jennifer Wilbanks</a> (who staged her own kidnapping in 2005 to prevent her wedding).</p>

<p>The exhibition represented phase one in the storefront's transition to full gallery mode, and it was granted barely ten days of life.  The end came at sundown today, but I think I was able to capture and preserve a hint of VanDyke's weird genius in these photographs.</p>

<p>I have to admit that when I first walked into the space I had more than a little difficulty distinguishing the relics from the art, since so much of the hair salon environment remained, but by the time we had to leave I was finding dynamite subjects everywhere my eye would rest.</p>

<p>I'm very sorry it's all gone now, but on its evidence alone I wouldn't want to miss anything else Thomas might invite into this terrific new space.</p>
