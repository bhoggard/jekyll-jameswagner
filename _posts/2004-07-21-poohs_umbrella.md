<p>Spotted on the way home from Williamsburg, on the uptown platform of the 14th Street IND station around midnight one rainy evening earlier this week:  An attractive and serious young man, comfortably slouched on the bench, reading a copy of Ernst Cassirer's "<a href="http://www.bartleby.com/65/ca/Cassirer.html">The Myth of the State</a>," small headphones holding his thoughts in place.  At his feet rested a beautiful, wet, Winnie-the-Pooh folding umbrella.</p>

<p>Unfortunately I didn't have the nerve to use my camera;  this time I had to just squeeze my eyes and record it without mechanical assistance.</p>

<p>Lovely, New York.</p>
