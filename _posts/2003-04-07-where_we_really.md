<p>Another <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/04/07/national/07PRIS.html?pagewanted=print&position=top">milestone</a> for the land of the free!</p>

<blockquote>
<p>An estimated 12 percent of African-American men ages 20 to 34 are in jail or prison, according to a report released yesterday by the Justice Department.</p>

<p>The proportion of young black men who are incarcerated has been rising in recent years, and this is the highest rate ever measured, said Allen J. Beck, the chief prison demographer for the Bureau of Justice Statistics, the statistical arm of the Justice Department.</p>

<p>By comparison, 1.6 percent of white men in the same age group are incarcerated.</p>
</blockquote>
