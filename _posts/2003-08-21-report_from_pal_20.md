<blockquote>
<p>Tel Aviv, Thursday, August 21, 2003</p>

<p>Again, no photos attached--my apologies.  I forgot to<br />
bring my CD-ROM to the Internet cafe.</p>

<p>Yesterday was a stressful and depressing day in<br />
Jayyous.  In the evening, some farmers and<br />
internationals went down to the gate to try and cut<br />
the wire that was holding it shut (for some reason,<br />
the chain and lock had not been put back on after the<br />
gate was opened for some construction workers working<br />
on the Wall).  We were especially concerned about<br />
"Farhat's" 12,000 cabbage seedlings, which needed to<br />
be planted before they died.</p>

<p>We had a complex operation, with some folks at the<br />
municipality on the top of the hill watching out for<br />
security cars approaching, some of us halfway down the<br />
hill, out of sight, with big clippers, and some folks<br />
at the gate.  Work on the wire ceased when security<br />
showed up, followed by Border Guard and army--8<br />
vehicles in all.  Ora from JAtO and Abu Ali negotiated<br />
and pleaded with the uniformed men to open the gate<br />
for the farmers waiting.  Eventually, they allowed the<br />
3 farmers through--without internationals.  We were<br />
concerned about their safety out there with all those<br />
soldiers, but they were OK.  The affront to Jayyous<br />
farmers' dignity, pleading for permission to enter<br />
their own land, makes me want to cry.</p>

<p>This morning, we went down to plant the cabbage<br />
seedlings.  The farmers came down to the gate in a<br />
taxi, and the security refused to let it<br />
through--totally arbitrarily, and completely beyond<br />
their mandate.   We proceeded on foot, and managed to<br />
get all the cabbage into the ground.</p>

<p>Afterward, Gabriel from SUSTAIN, Ora from JAtO,<br />
Christy from Olympia and I left Jayyous for Tel Aviv. <br />
It was really hard to say goodbye to the Jayyous Peace<br />
Activists--I can't wait to see them again next year.</p>

<p>Gabriel had a long talk with the Israeli Society for<br />
Citizens' Rights about the situation with the gates in<br />
the Apartheid Wall.  They may be able to mount a<br />
successful case in the High Court of Israel to change<br />
the situation.</p>

<p>We had planned to go to Nablus tomorrow for a Saturday<br />
demonstration, but with the invasions of Nablus,<br />
Tulkarm and Jenin by the Israeli army, the demo may be<br />
called off.  The Israeli army assassination of a Hamas<br />
leader in Gaza today signals a whole new round of<br />
strike and counterstrike.  I continue to believe that<br />
this is exactly what the Israeli Right wants--the more<br />
warfare there is, the more support they get.</p>

<p>It's good to be able to relax on the beach in Tel<br />
Aviv.  In the morning, we'll go to Tel Aviv District<br />
Court to attend a hearing of Andreas and Andrew, Swede<br />
and Scot from ISM Nablus who were arrested for<br />
chaining themselves to a home slated for demolition in<br />
Balata Refugee Camp (the cruelty of bulldozing<br />
refugees' homes boggles the mind).  [see the photo and <br />
link on my <a href="https://jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200308#1031">post</a> yesterday, and now the "after" photo <br />
below]</p>

<p>After that, perhaps to Nazlat Isa.  It's a village <br />
right over the Green Line from the Baqa el-Gharbiya, <br />
the town I lived in 1985-1986.  Nazlat Isa is one of <br />
the communities isolated between the Wall to the east <br />
and the Green Line to the west.  Most of Nazlat Isa's <br />
commercial district was demolished to make way for the <br />
Wall last winter, and the Israeli army began <br />
demolishing what's left today.</p>

<p>Steve</p>
</blockquote>
