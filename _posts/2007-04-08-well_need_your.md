<em><strong>We'll just Need to Scan Your Eyes for Our Files</strong><br />
Imad Salman Ichleef, 37, was questioned yesterday by American soldiers about insurgent activity in his neighborhood of Ghazaliya in baghdad. Using a biometric recording device, one of the soldiers scanned Mr. Ichleef's retinas, collected his fingerprints and photographed his face. The interview was part of a strategy to put Iraqi males into a database. Mr. Ichleef's family waited patiently so they could get back to their lunch.</em>  [the full <u>NYTimes</u> caption to the single photo printed by the paper on April 7]</p>

<p>Because I had been unable to find his image on the <u>NYTimes</u> website or anywhere else, after doing a <a href="/2007/04/we_need_your_ey.html">post</a> about it yesterday I emailed <a href="http://www.ashleygilbertson.com/">Ashley Gilbertson</a> asking if he could help me find and link to one.</p>

<p>Today I received a generous reply.  He began with:  "I'm out in the badlands right now so can't talk."  He attached these four awesome images to an email which was probably sent from Iraq.</p>

<p>Wow.</p>

<p>He wrote that he had no idea which photograph the paper had used [it was the third, and it appeared in black and white].</p>

<p>Thanks, Ash.</p>

<p>A memoir of Gilbertson's experience in Iraq, "<a href="http://www.amazon.com/Whiskey-Tango-Foxtrot-Photographers-Chronicle/dp/0226293254/ref=sr_1_1/102-1336858-1508903?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1176097561&sr=8-1">Whiskey Tango Foxtrot: A Photographer's Chronicle of the Iraq War</a>", is being published by the University of Chicago Press and is due for release November 1.</p>
