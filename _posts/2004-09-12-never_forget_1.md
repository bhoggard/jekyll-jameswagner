<p>Only moments before I snapped this picture we had left a restaurant downtown, for a dinner totally unrelated to the third anniversary of September 11.   The image is a view from Broadway and Fulton Street of Creative Time's "<a href="http://www.creativetime.org/programs/archive/2002/tribute/ct.html">Tribute in Light</a>," recreated on the edge of the site of the World Trade Center once again yesterday.  (note:  the partially-completed building to the right is the new 7 World Trade Center, rising on the <a href="/2004/03/now_we_can_say.html">site</a> where I had worked up to the year before the <a href="http://www.emporis.com/en/wm/bu/?id=114932">original</a> was destroyed - fortunately with no loss of life)</p>

<p>The first reminder of the day's significance however was this amazing sandwich board we passed seconds before.</p>

<p><img alt="neverforgetkitchenopen2.JPG" src="/mt_archives/neverforgetkitchenopen2.JPG" width="450" height="600" border="0" /></p>

<blockquote>
<p>God Bless America<br />  <br />
<i>never forget</i><br />  <br />
Kitchen Open<br /> <br />
$6 Beer Pitchers All Day</p>
</blockquote>

<p>I'm now wondering which image I'm going to remember best.</p>

<p>[apologies for the poor quality of the pictures, but it was very dark, very late and the wine was fine; and no, we never figured out what made the small flecks in the light beams, here seen as wavy lines, although they really made the towers dance last night]</p>

<p><b>UPDATE:</b>  The mystery of the flecks which are visible in the beams of light has apparently been solved. and it's not a feelgood answer.  <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/tommoody/?29076">Tom Moody</a> posts an account via <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/treehouse/comment/14973/">Alex</a> (scroll down) that the wavy lines in my photo apparently represent "behaviorally trapped" migratory birds who had become confused by night become day.  This was probably not a good thing at all, and it makes me very sad.</p>
