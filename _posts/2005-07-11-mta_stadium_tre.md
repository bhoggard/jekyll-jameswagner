<p><a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/004969.html">Barry</a> has just about covered the issue, with the help of <u>Newsday</u>'s estimable <a href="/2004/04/subway_insecuri.html">Ray Sanchez</a>, but a <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/07/08/opinion/l08london.html?n=Top%2fOpinion%2fEditorials%20and%20Op%2dEd%2fLetters">letter</a> to the editor published in the <u>NYTimes</u> helps to illustrate the scale of the criminal incompetence and negligence of those at the top by bringing up the most recent scandal involving the MTA:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>To the Editor:</p>

<p>The terrorist blasts in London and a similar attack last year in Madrid dramatically point to the vulnerability of New York's transit system to a similar attack.</p>

<p>Despite setting aside nearly $600 million [state and federal money] to secure the transit network against a terrorist strike, the Metropolitan Transportation Authority has accomplished little since 9/11. It was not until March 2003 that the agency announced a plan to address the transit system's weaknesses.</p>

<p>In fact, the lion's share of the money has not been allocated. The agency's most public initiative is a failed proposal to ban photography by straphangers.</p>

<p><em>Its foot-dragging is especially unsettling when contrasted with the speed with which it rushed through a deal for the proposed West Side stadium.</em> [the italics are mine]</p>

<p>Instead of issuing color-coded alerts, the federal government and the M.T.A. should urgently undertake measures with existing money to enhance security.</p>

<p>Manuel Cortazal<br />
Bronx, July 7, 2005</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Wish us all luck.  It looks like we're going to need it.</p>
