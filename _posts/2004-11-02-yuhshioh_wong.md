<p>I told myself while at the opening on October 23rd that I really had to stop reporting on so many <a href="http://www.foxyproduction.com/">Foxy Production</a> shows (they're very, very good), since it looks like I'm virtually living on the sixth floor above 27th Street.</p>

<p>But that same evening, just before my almost-resolution, I had asked for some JPEG images of  Yuh-Shioh Wong's work (one of three artists included in the current show), and they just arrived today.  Now there was just no way I wasn't going to share these wonderful sculptural paintings with others.</p>

<p>Barry and I had first seen her work as drawings at *sixtyseven when that gallery (now simply <a href="http://www.sixtysevengallery.com/">sixtyseven</a>) was still in Williamsburg and we thought they were terrific, but we had missed what photographs document must have been an extraordinary installation at the excellent <a href="http://www.atmgallery.com/">ATM Gallery</a> in the East Village.</p>

<p>The prominent three-dimensional physicality, the robust surface textures, and the lusty colors describe what are totally winsome shapes, but there are hints that something just a little more disturbing is working itself out here.</p>
