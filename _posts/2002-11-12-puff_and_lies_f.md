<blockquote>
<p>White House officials said Mr. Bush, whose <b>service in the Texas Air National Guard during the Vietnam War </b>meant that he was not sent to fight in Southeast Asia, had never before visited the monument as president.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>But we've known for several years that there is no record that Bushie had any effective participation in the military at any time.  How is it that the media can deliver this <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/12/international/12BUSH.html">puff piece</a> with a straight face, and with absolutely no clarification, while they mouth the White House press release?</p>

<p>There is apparently no account, official or unofficial, of where Bush was during most of the entire term of the Texas Air National Guard duty his family was able to secure for him in lieu of service in Vietnam.  He apparently failed to report for duty and skipped off to Alabama to work on a political campaign.  He was ultimately suspended from flight duty and didn't show up during the last two years of his service.  This is not "service during the Vietnam War."  Ask a real veteran.  No, ask the <u>NYTimes</u> and the <u>Los</u> <u>Angeles</u> <u>Times</u>, among others.</p>
