a silent cry from a witness across the street two days ago</p>

<p>Whatever the bureaucratic, commercial or political story which lies behind the <a href="http://gothamist.com/2008/01/21/kent.php">human tragedy</a> of New York City's dreadful and totally irresponsible eviction of over 200 men, women, children and their pets from their homes in the darkness nine days ago on one of the coldest nights of the year, if this doesn't radicalize New Yorkers, we deserve whatever we get.</p>

<p>But there is no acceptable outcome to this particular tale other than the quick return of these people and a proper accounting of the official malfeasance which resulted in their removal in the first place.</p>

<p>Bloomberg, Markowitz, Quinn, anybody out there?   We do note that that very decent local member of the City Council, David Yassky, has been with this story from the beginning, was at the scene on Sunday, and appears to be very <a href="http://gowanuslounge.blogspot.com/2008/01/475-kent-confusion-anger-calls-to-allow.html">supportive</a> of the vibrant and creative community which has lived and worked inside this massive, 11-story Williamsburg building block for ten years.</p>

<p>For more on the story of 475 Kent, see <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/006853.html">Bloggy</a> and any number of other on-line sites.</p>

<p>The images below were taken this past Sunday night.  They show tenants retrieving their possessions (boxes, art, bicycles, baby carriages, parakeets, etc.) in the last moments before the building was finally padlocked, for a painfully-indefinite period.  A large crowd gathered across the street in the bitter cold to observe the sad scene.</p>

<p>If you go to Barry's <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/sets/72157603809351914/show/">flickr images</a>, note the Police van parked on the sidewalk adjacent to the large crowd which was repeatedly pushed back from the parking lane onto that part of the sidewalk not occupied by an NYPD vehicle.</p>
