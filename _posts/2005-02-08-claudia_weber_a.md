<p>Claudia Weber is represented by one photograph and two sculptural installations at <a href="http://www.momentaart.org/">Momenta Art</a> this month.  I like them all a lot.  The installations both excite and scare me for their fugaciousness [I worked on that noun a bit].  For more, see <a href="http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/004662.html">bloggy</a> and go to the gallery in Williamsburg before March 7.</p>

<p><img alt="KaradyJennifergoatmine.jpg" src="/mt_archives/KaradyJennifergoatmine.jpg" width="500" height="530" /><br />
Jennifer Karady  <em>Pageant Talent: Katrina Johnson, Miss Nimrod 2003, Nimrod, MN</em> 2004 Chromogenic color print on Fujiflex, mounted on Plexiglas 30" x 30" installation view</p>

<p>In the second gallery space Jennifer Karady shows photographs which engage, and disturb, with their sensitive examinations of some very special [dependent but apparently quite fulfilling] relationships between people and the animals closest to them.</p>

<p>While looking around unsuccessfully for larger images from the show, on a day on which the people at Momenta are not around to help with a jpeg, I discovered that Karady has herself <a href="http://www.tillamookcheddar.com/shows/collar/artists/jennifer_karady.html">collaborated</a> with a friend from a neigboring species, <a href="http://www.tillamookcheddar.com/index.html">Tillamook Cheddar</a>, a diminutive and very charming abstract expressionist with a solid <a href="http://www.tillamookcheddar.com/bio.htm">c.v.</a> of her own.</p>
