<p>Don't underestimate the Brits, or the seniors.  <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&u=/031117/ids_photos_wl/r2002290262.jpg">Reuters</a> supplied the picture and the following caption:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Lindis Percy, an anti-war protestor, stands on top of the gates of Buckingham Palace with an upside-down U.S. flag with the inscription 'Elizabeth Windsor and Co we don't want him here,' November 17, 2003 as a British police officer stands below. The woman, wearing a fluorescent waistcoat, dodged tight security to scale the six-meter high wrought iron gates on the eve of U.S. President's George W. Bush's state visit to Britain.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The BBC <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/politics/3279179.stm">reports</a>, "Mrs Percy, of Hull, who was arrested and later bailed, said she was 'amazed' she had been able to unfurl a banner declaring Mr Bush was not welcome in the UK."<a href="http://www.levellers.org.uk/Lindis-Percy.htm">Lindis Percy</a> is a creative and limber 61-year-old grandmother of four.  She is a career health worker and a respected activist with an impressive history.</p>

<p>I suspect there is one thing Lindis Percy is not, in spite of the family <a href="http://www.geocities.com/percyfamilyhistory/one.html">name</a>.   Judging from the text of her excellent sign, she may not be a monarchist, unlike so many of her countrymen, but also unlike so many of the submissive subjects of Britain's current unwelcome guest.</p>

<p>Incidently, Bushie didn't use the gates.  He landed on the palace grounds in a helicopter, to save the emperor the embarassment of being confronted on the roads by thousands of outraged "allies".</p>
