<p>[again, a report from Steve, still in Palestine, relayed through Donald]</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Steve and company walked through the checkpoint outside nablus no <br />
problem.</p>

<p>ISM people are doing a checkpoint watch to advocate for people not <br />
allowed through.  Steve stayed there a couple hours and helped get <br />
people through, including sick people.  He witnessed an entire family <br />
get turned away, luggage and all.  They were trying to get back home <br />
to Jordan.</p>

<p>Steve went to Balata refugee camp.  He says it is "carnivalesque".  <br />
Even during curfew it is packed with people, vendors hawking.  There <br />
are 30,000 people in the camp.  Packed.</p>

<p>Internationals have a definite role to play in stopping the <br />
demolition of Palestinian homes.  Steve and others from his group <br />
spent the night in Askar refugee camp, outside Nablus, with 4 <br />
different families of people shot by the Israeli military.  <br />
Meanwhile, in the old city of Nablus, a home was demolished that was <br />
not housing an international.</p>

<p>The demolitions are usually done around 2 in the morning.</p>

<p>Today Steve watched a tank go down a street in Nablus shooting at <br />
rock throwing kids.  His group visited a house in Nablus.  It's a <br />
big, beautiful house, and it has a great view of the city, so it has <br />
been taken by the Israeli military.  The twenty Palestinians who <br />
lived there have been shoved into a single apartment. confined to one <br />
apartment. Folks from JATO brought medicine and food to those people,</p>

<p>Outside the building there are two trucks being held for driving <br />
during curfew.  One of the trucks is full of food which will rot.  <br />
Steve and his group hope to persuade military officers to release the <br />
truck (they also have one of the driver's ID papers, which means he <br />
can't go anywhere until he gets them back).</p>

<p>That's all for now.</p>

<p>Dsg</p>
</blockquote>
