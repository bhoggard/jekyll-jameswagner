<p>Right now I'd say that summer art is art that somehow can survive the distraction of high levels of heat and humidity.  In my case that's a pretty tall order, so I'm impressed that even in my weather-compromised state I managed to register a number of worthies in a relatively-abreviated swing through Williamburg Sunday afternoon.</p>

<p>A peek at a sample of the shows:</p>

<p><img alt="schifinoKimMGWS.jpg" src="/mt_archives/schifinoKimMGWS.jpg" width="550" height="437" /><br />
Kim Schifino [installation view of a grouping of "Manny", "George", "Willy" and "Sam", silk-screened wood cut outs]  This work,  installed as shadowboxes, is part of a single-artist show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/2821">Cinders Gallery</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="LublinerPhilHotDogs.jpg" src="/mt_archives/LublinerPhilHotDogs.jpg" width="625" height="371" /><br />
<a href="http://www.lakerelax.com/">Phil Lubliner</a> [installation view of a grouping of three works: "Hot Dogs, Corned Beef, Highland Park" craft paper and acrylic 84" x 28.5";  "Hot Dog (chicago Style)" clay, acrylic, pen and plywood 12" x 8" x 4"; and "Corned Beef (Jewish Delicatessen)" clay, acrylic, pen, plywood and plants 12" x 8" x 3.5"]  This installation is part of a group show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/2864">McCraig-Welles</a></p>

<p><img alt="SchumannMaxFuckMeHard.jpg" src="/mt_archives/SchumannMaxFuckMeHard.jpg" width="600" height="417" /><br />
<a href="http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9C04E2DE143FF931A15754C0A9639C8B63&sec=&pagewanted=print">Max Schumann</a> <em>untitled (Fuck Me Hard and Slow Big Daddy McMac)</em> 2005 acrylic on cardboard 34" x 68" [installation view]  This piece is part of a group show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/2803">Klaus von Nichtssagend Gallery</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="CarterRobStills.jpg" src="/mt_archives/CarterRobStills.jpg" width="575" height="434" /><br />
<a href="http://www.robcarter.net/">Rob Carter</a> <em>Four Extracts from Stills</em> 2004-2005 DVD color/sound [still from video installation]  The video is shown on a player mounted on a wall next to three of the artist's photo-based works, and is part of several group shows at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/location/view/2/270">Dam, Stuhltrager</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="WilliamsBrookeWallHands.jpg" src="/mt_archives/WilliamsBrookeWallHands.jpg" width="600" height="399" /><br />
Brooke Williams A Wall of Hands 2006 172" x 72" [large detail of installation]  This is a work mounted in the rear gallery at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/2794">Outrageous Look</a>, which is also showing art by Tom Burke and Sachar Mathias.</p>
