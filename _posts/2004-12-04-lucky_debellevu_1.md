Lucky DeBellevue <i>untitled</i> (2004) chenille stems and plastic ball 31" x 11" x 82.5" detail view</p>

<p>The gallery show closed three weeks ago, on the day I visited it and snapped this picture, but the image continues to attract me, both in the memory of it and in my regular glimpses of it as I scroll through my image upload file - reasons enough to re-visit it now and get it out there.</p>

<p>This is a detail of a gorgeous work by Lucky DeBellevue.  It was included in a very good show, "<a href="http://www.cohanandleslie.com/content.php?mode=past&scr_w=1017&object_id=43">When the lights go out...</a>", mounted by Cohan and Leslie in mid-October.  The press release said that his sculptures ". . . resemble many known forms but represent nothing."  I like that.</p>

<p>We've both loved his work for years, but the only chenille stems Barry and I have in our apartment are those which compose an <a href="/2004/07/studio_360.html">Eric Doeringer</a> Lucky DeBellevue "Bootleg."  We do however cherish a small ink drawing by DeBellevue himself.</p>
