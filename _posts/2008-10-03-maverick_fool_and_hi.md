<p>I've just sat through my first - and last, <em>ever</em> - Presidential or Vice-Presidential debate.   As Barry <a href="http://twitter.com/">twitter</a>ed, immediately after we had together watched a real TV show in real time probably for the first time since 9/11:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I feel I lost IQ points watching that. I hope I get them back. What we call a "debate" is a travesty of that concept.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Two of my own thoughts:   I think the Republican "principal" should be watching his back:  My headline refers to his "dummy", but in this country dummies have a history of taking over everything, even supplanting fools.</p>

<p>And after listening to Ms. Palin's painful memorized deliveries, I never want to hear anyone visit the word "maverick" again.  John McCain has only done two "maverick-ish" things in his lifetime:  The first was the moment he asserted that he was capable of performing as President of the United States;  the second was the moment he decided to tell us that, in a pinch (or something of that sort), Sarah Palin would be able to do the same.</p>

<p>I may be significant of nothing, but does anyone remember the original Ford <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ford_Maverick_(North_America)">Maverick</a>, a slightly-gussied-up version of the Falcon, a tired earlier model?   I do, for reasons not related to any virtues which might later have been associated with it, sentimentally or otherwise.  Let it suffice to say, the Maverick was not a "memorable" car in any sense which could be related to worthiness.</p>

<p>Like the current Republican slate, just lipstick and paint on cheap plastic and rusting tin.</p>
