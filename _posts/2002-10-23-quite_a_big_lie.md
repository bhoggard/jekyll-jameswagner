<p>Weapons inspectors left Iraq in 1998 without U.N. approval in a predawn evacuation on the orders of their chief, Richard Butler, hours before American and British attacks on Iraq.  Butler had been advised by Washington of a possible U.S.-led bombing attack, but the official explanation given for the withdrawal was that Iraq was not cooperating with the inspectors.</p>

<p>Since 1998, the media, which had originally reported the facts basically as described above, has totally revised the story into one which features Iraq throwing inspectors out of the country.</p>

<p>Does anyone need further proof of the media's collusion with the White House junta?</p>

<p>I have been talking about this for months, and lately I have heard this <a href="http://www.fair.org/extra/0210/inspectors.html">quite Big Lie</a> repeated even by sources usually critical of Washington propaganda, like <u>The</u> <u>Nation</u> and WNYC's Brian Lehr.</p>

<p>If the sources of our information are so corrupted, and if even the sceptical are so easily hoodwinked (or become so vulnerable because of just plain laziness), how can we possibly survive as a democracy?</p>

<p>Want <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines02/010799-01.htm">more</a> on the story of the "weapons inspectors?"</p>

<blockquote>
<p>[from the <u>NYTimes</u> in a 1999 story]  WASHINGTON, Jan. 6 -- United States officials said today that American spies had worked undercover on teams of United Nations arms inspectors ferreting out secret Iraqi weapons programs.</p>

<p>Iraq has long condemned the inspectors as tools of American intelligence. In October it issued a statement saying it would never cooperate with United Nations teams riddled with ''American spies and agents.''</p>
</blockquote>
