<p>[This is my friend's first, brief, report following his arival in Palestine.  The text is that of his contact in New York who will regularly be in touch with Steve by cellphone while he's there.]</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I spoke with Steve on the phone this morning (Wednesday 8/14).  He <br />
says he is fine and really has very little to report at this time.</p>

<p>He is staying in Jerusalem through tonight.  Tomorrow night he will <br />
be in Bethlehem, and the following night (Friday) in Beit Sahor.</p>

<p>Also, Brooklyn NYC Council member Bill DiBlasio* is part of a <br />
delegation visiting Israel and is speaking this evening at the King <br />
David Hotel.  Steve plans to go and let DiBlasio know what he thinks.</p>

<p>There are lots of international activists in Nablus.  They are <br />
staying with the families of Palestinians who have been shot, to <br />
protect them from having their homes blown up**.</p>

<p>When I spoke to him, he was on a dirt road in the West Bank, just <br />
outside of Jerusalem at a settlement called Gilo***.  Last time he was <br />
there he got shot at a lot, but this time his group has found the <br />
Israeli army camp abandoned.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>*  A number of Council members are currently <a href="http://www.haaretzdaily.com/hasen/pages/ShArt.jhtml?itemNo=196888&contrassID=1&subContrassID=7&sbSubContrassID=0&listSrc=Y">visiting Israel</a>, but there are no plans for any of them to speak to Israeli arabs or Palestinians.</p>

<p>** The Israeli government has re-introduced its <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A52289-2002Aug6.html">policy</a> of destroying the homes of supected "terrorists."</p>

<p>*** <a href="http://www.jqf-jerusalem.org/journal/2000/jqf10/gilo.html">Gilo</a> is an Israeli settlement on Palestinian land in East Jerusalem</p>
