<p>Every graduating class needs a <a href="http://www.motherjones.com/mother_jones/JA95/bernstein.html">Tony Kushner </a>a lot more than it needs some suit.</p>

<p>Invited to <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/05/27/education/27VASS.html">address </a>this year's graduating class at Vassar, the activist and playwright told its members and their guests that the future did not look bright and that no one he knew was truly happy.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The best they could hope for, he said, was to be "happyish."</p>

<p>"This is a time of crisis," he said. "And in a time of crisis, you have to focus on being real."</p>

<p>In his 20-minute address, Mr. Kushner urged the graduates to think to themselves: "I am here to organize; I am here to be political."</p>

<p>"It's boring to organize, but do it," he said. "The world ends if you don't."</p>
</blockquote>
