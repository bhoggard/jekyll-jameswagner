<p>Bloggers do it on line - and some do it in studios too.</p>

<p>These folks don't just blog about Art;  they're making some of that stuff themselves.  There are some real treasures at "<a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/5809">The Blogger Show</a>", installed at Agni Gallery in the East Village through January 12.  Unfortunately I can only show a very few of them here.</p>

<p>The show was organized by <a href="http://urbanbytes.blogspot.com/2007/10/john-morris-nyc-artist-pittsburgh.html">John Morris</a> and Pittsburgh's <a href="http://www.diggingpitt.com/index.html">Digging Pit Gallery</a>.  It's actually a joint venture with Panza Gallery in Millvale, Pennsylvania.  A list of the thirty or so artist/bloggers from around the country who are represented in the New York edition, together with the names of their blogs, can be found on the show's own <a href="http://thebloggershow.diggingpitt.com/">site</a>, which is actually a blog itself.  Looking at the art they produce it would seem they have nothing in common but their blogging, but there's also an enormous, shared enthusiasm for other people's work, something which couldn't be missed when I met many of them at the New York opening earlier this month.</p>
