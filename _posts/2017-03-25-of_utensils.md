Patti Smith <em>Arthur Rimbaud's utensils</em> n.d.</p>

<p>I came across this photograph by Patti Smith while looking on line at the very few photo images we have of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Rimbaud">Arthur Rimbaud</a>. My short trip began with <a href="https://news.artnet.com/art-world/patti-smith-rimbaud-france-899480">the news of Smith's purchase of a house in Roche</a>, in the Ardennes, built near the foundations of the one in which the French poet had grown up (His mother's original farm house had been destroyed in the First World war).</p>

<p>While there may still be so much to be said about Rimbaud, all I'll say in this post is that once again I'm feeling a wee bit closer to him, and it's because of this knife and spoon, which had  apparently been used by the great boy: They are virtually identical to some that I've been using for almost half a century.</p>

<p>They're old (nineteenth century), made of a material I know as 'German silver' [<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nickel_silver">nickel silver</a>, or <em>Maillechort</em> in France]. Their shapes are very fine, and they never tarnish. Like Rimbaud.</p>

<p>I now love them all more than ever.</p>
