<p><strong>UPDATE:</strong></p>

<p>In Britain the media is interested in the <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/4627453.stm">Battle of Trafalgar</a> <em>and</em> Tom Hurndall, even though both are dead.  We get runaway brides and the ten commandments on a lawn.  If you live in the right place once in a while you get a peek at a real story, but only a peek and only on terms supported by a larger agenda.</p>

<p>Lest anyone think that the nationality of the victim is key to the quality of justice extended or press coverage provided, the <a href="/2003/03/rachels_story.html">story</a> of the very American <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Corrie">Rachel Corrie</a> is more than a caution.  Oh, and she and her family were just as photogenic, just as white, just as blond as the Hurndalls.  The usual popularity of the type is familiar to everyone in America;  <em>viz.</em> the description of the ubiquitous missing children and young women in never-ending reports on the pretend-news programs of CNN and Fox.  Sometimes war and politics trumps everday racism, even in America.</p>

<p>In a tiny <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/06/28/international/28briefs.html?">article</a> [scroll down] on page 8 today, the <u>NYTimes</u> reports that the Israeli soldier who killed <a href="/2004/01/saints.html">Tom Hurndall</a> two years ago has been found guilty by a military court.</p>

<p>For more, see <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4625743.stm">this</a> BBC story for a description of the crime, and <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/middle_east/4625355.stm">this</a> one for a bit on the trial itself.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>The defendant was led out of the court in handcuffs and tried to attack a number of photographers and cameramen filming him.</p>

<p>More than 50 people crowded into the small courtroom on a military base in southern Israel, to hear the verdict - which took more than an hour to read out.</p>

<p>In addition to the manslaughter verdict, [Taysir] Hayb was found guilty of obstruction of justice, incitement to false testimony, false testimony and improper conduct.</p>

<p>The court was told Hayb fired at Mr Hurndall from an Israeli army watchtower, using a sniper rifle with a telescopic sight.</p>

<p>Witnesses said Mr Hurndall, from north London, had been escorting children away from gunfire when he was hit in the head by a single shot.</p>

<p>The Israeli army initially disputed this account, but under pressure from Mr Hurndall's family and the British government it ordered a full investigation. It later indicted Hayb, <em>a member of Israel's Bedouin Arab minority</em> [my italics].</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The identity of the defendent serves to further dramatize the story as both a personal tragedy and as representative of the much larger human disaster fed by the U.S.-supported Israeli government occupation policy, the incompetence of Palestinian leadership, and the silence of good people everywhere.</p>
