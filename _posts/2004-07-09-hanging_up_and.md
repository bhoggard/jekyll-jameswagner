<p><a href="http://www.matthewmarks.com/exhibition.php?view=detail&ID=251&viewMode=&pressID=91&detail=&image=0&section=exhibitions">Matthew Marks</a> on 24th Street opened its doors Wednesday on night to a gem of a show, "Deliver Us From Evil," curated by Matthew himself.   I includes the work of four bodies of artists who seemed to be working out of territory carved a century and a half earlier by their fifth colleague, Honore daumier.  It was a masterly selection in every sense.</p>

<p>The contemporary artists include R. Crumb, Jake and Dinos Chapman, Dr. Lakra and Keegan McChargue.  The names we recognize right off the bat betray the theme of societal caricature which runs through all the work shown, most of them executed on paper.</p>

<p>In the center of the gallery space are several plinths displaying exquisite small sculptures, including the pieces by the inventive young Mexican artist which are shown in the image above.  The Chapman Brothers meanwhile had made a standard McDonalds Happy Meal their very own, as seen in the image below.</p>

<p><img alt="ChapmanMcDonalds.JPG" src="/mt_archives/ChapmanMcDonalds.JPG" width="500" height="571" border="0" /><br />

<p>The crowd was almost as diverting as the art, even aside from our pleasure in running into our friend <a href="http://www.lagalerie.de/lagalerie_english_deutsch/l_a_galerie_artists/hiroshi_sunairi_english/hiroshi_sunairi_basel/hiroshi_sunairi_basel.html">Hiroshi Sunairi</a>, who introduced us to <a href="http://www.atmgallery.com/artwork/yuh/press.html">Yuh-Shioh Wong</a>, whose work we have been excited about for some time, and <a href="http://www.newmuseum.org/now_upcoming.php">Yukie Kamiya</a>, newly-appointed as an assistant curator with the New Museum.   Here is part of the scene on opening night, standing in front of the one wall which included a mix of the work of all the artists included in the show:</p>
