<p>Another <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/05/opinion/L05HUMM.html">letter</a> in the <u>NYTimes</u> today puts the lie to the boasts of <a href="http://a332.g.akamai.net/f/332/936/12h/www.edmunds.com/media/news/feature/vehicle/hummer/03.hummer.h2.plate.500.jpg">Hummer</a> owners and GM's marketing campaign.  Note that the vehicle in question is the "small" Hummer</p>

<blockquote>
<p>To the Editor:<br />
I'm not surprised that the H2 is such a hot seller (Business Day, Nov. 2). America is full of self-centered people, desperately craving attention from strangers.<br />
Hummer's general manager says, "The people that buy this product, they're daring." What's so daring about driving a military vehicle to do errands? Riding a bicycle is daring.<br />
  BRIAN DRYE<br />
Seattle, Nov. 2, 2002</p>
</blockquote>

<p>The question should also be directed toward the owners of less <i>ueber</i> SUVs.</p>
