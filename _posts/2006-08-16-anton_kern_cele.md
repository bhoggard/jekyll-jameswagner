<p>For the rest of this week those who haven't over the years been visiting the shows mounted at Anton Kern, one of New York's least predictable and most idiosyncratic galleries, have a chance to see what they've missed.   The show still installed in the gallery's rooms on West 20th Street ended officially July 28, but it will not be struck until next week.</p>

<p>"Implosion" is  the Kern's tenth <a href="http://www.antonkerngallery.com/current.php">anniversary show</a>, and while it might seem a cool and very modest tribute to the artists and to the gallery itself, coming as it does in the middle of the New York Summer doldrums, don't be fooled.  The work is first-rate and, typically, it seems to owe almost nothing to the competition in the neighborhood.</p>
